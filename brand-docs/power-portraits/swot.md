# Power Portraits — SWOT Analysis

## Strengths

- **Directed experience.** Ben's filmmaking background means clients get genuine direction — not "tilt your head slightly." This is the single biggest differentiator. Most photographers are technically skilled but can't direct people.

- **Two-tier pricing model.** Full sessions ($450) for premium clients, Portrait Days ($149) for everyone else. This captures both ends of the market without diluting the brand.

- **Full marketing stack behind it.** PP is marketed by NB's entire pipeline — landing pages, drip campaigns, lead scoring, analytics. Most photographers rely on Instagram and referrals only.

- **Fast turnaround.** 3-5 business days vs industry standard of 2-4 weeks. This is a competitive advantage that costs nothing to maintain.

- **Personal brand crossover.** Ben's YouTube/personal brand creates organic awareness for PP. Every behind-the-scenes video or portrait post on Ben Alek Conner channels drives traffic to PP.

---

## Weaknesses

- **No studio.** Operating mobile/outdoor limits the types of sessions offered and perceived professionalism. Some clients specifically want "studio headshots."

- **No portfolio depth.** Pre-launch means the portfolio is thin. Need 20-30 strong images before the brand feels credible.

- **One photographer.** Can't scale beyond ~20 sessions/month without hiring. Bottlenecked on Ben's time.

- **No reviews or testimonials.** Social proof is zero. First 10 clients need to be incentivised to leave reviews.

- **Competing for Ben's time.** PP shares Ben's attention with NB (primary business), BAC (personal brand), and CLG (demand letters). It may get deprioritised.

---

## Opportunities

- **LinkedIn personal branding trend.** More professionals care about their online image than ever. The market is growing.

- **Corporate team packages.** One team booking = 4-10 sessions in a single day. Efficient and high-value.

- **Portrait Day as a lead gen event.** $149 sessions bring in clients who may upgrade to full sessions later. It's a funnel, not just a product.

- **Collaboration with NB clients.** Every NB marketing client needs headshots for their website, social, and about pages. Cross-sell is built in.

- **Photobook projects.** Ben's personal photobook work creates editorial-quality portfolio pieces that elevate PP's brand.

- **Event photography expansion.** Corporate events, conferences, networking nights — adjacent market with repeat bookings.

---

## Threats

- **AI headshot generators.** Tools like Remini, Aragon, and HeadshotPro generate decent headshots from selfies for $20-$50. Quality will keep improving.

- **Smartphone camera quality.** iPhone portrait mode is "good enough" for many people. The gap between phone and studio is narrowing.

- **Economic sensitivity.** Headshots are discretionary. In a downturn, they're the first thing cut.

- **Established photographers with SEO.** Competitors who've been ranking on Google for years will be hard to outrank in the short term.

- **Weather dependency.** Outdoor shoots are weather-dependent. Need reliable indoor backup locations.

- **Burnout from multi-business juggling.** PP could suffer from divided attention across Ben's portfolio of businesses.
