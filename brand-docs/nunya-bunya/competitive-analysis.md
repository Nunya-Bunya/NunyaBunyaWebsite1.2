# Nunya Bunya — Competitive Analysis

## Market Overview

The Brisbane digital marketing agency market is crowded with agencies ranging from solo freelancers ($500/month) to enterprise firms ($10,000+/month). Most compete on reputation, portfolio size, or industry specialisation. Very few compete on technology or pricing transparency.

---

## Direct Competitors

### 1. Digital Eagles (Brisbane)
- **Size:** 20-50 employees
- **Pricing:** $2,000-$8,000/month
- **Strengths:** Established reputation, Google Partner, strong SEO track record
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive, corporate feel, long contracts (6-12 months), slow turnaround
- **How NB wins:** 5-10x cheaper, no contracts, faster execution with AI

### 2. Distl (Brisbane)
- **Size:** 10-20 employees
- **Pricing:** $1,500-$5,000/month
- **Strengths:** Clean branding, good website, strong local SEO focus
- **Weaknesses:** Premium pricing excludes most SMBs, traditional agency model
- **How NB wins:** We serve the market they price out. Their $1,500 minimum is our mid-tier.

### 3. Toby Creative (Brisbane)
- **Size:** 5-10 employees
- **Pricing:** $800-$3,000/month
- **Strengths:** Affordable end of agency market, good Google Ads management
- **Weaknesses:** Generic creative, no marketing automation, basic reporting
- **How NB wins:** Superior reporting (live dashboards vs monthly PDFs), automation, content quality

### 4. Solo Freelancers (Upwork/Fiverr)
- **Size:** 1 person
- **Pricing:** $200-$800/month
- **Strengths:** Cheap, flexible, no overhead
- **Weaknesses:** Unreliable, no systems, no analytics, limited skill range
- **How NB wins:** Same price point but with enterprise-grade tools, automation, and consistency

---

## Indirect Competitors

### DIY Tools (Canva, Mailchimp, Buffer, Wix)
- Business owners doing it themselves
- **Threat level:** Medium — they save money but waste time and get mediocre results
- **How NB wins:** "You've been doing it yourself for a year. How's it going?" We offer to take it off their plate for less than they'd expect.

### GHL / GoHighLevel White-Label Agencies
- Agencies reselling GoHighLevel as their own platform
- **Threat level:** High — similar positioning (all-in-one, affordable)
- **How NB wins:** We own our stack. GHL agencies pay $97-$497/month to GoHighLevel and pass the cost to clients. We pay $16/month total. Also, GHL's UX is notoriously bad.

## Opportunities

1. **SMBs priced out of agency market** — There's a massive underserved segment between "doing it myself" and "agency retainer." NB fills this gap.
2. **Anti-SaaS sentiment growing** — Business owners are tired of $500/month tool stacks. Self-hosted is a selling point.
3. **AI adoption curve** — Most agencies haven't integrated AI into production. NB is 2 years ahead.
4. **Local Brisbane focus** — Face-to-face relationships still matter for SMBs. Being local is an advantage over remote agencies.

---

## Threats

1. **Established agencies dropping prices** — If bigger agencies introduce affordable tiers, they could compete on brand alone
2. **AI making marketing too easy** — If tools like ChatGPT make it trivially easy for business owners to DIY, the agency model weakens
3. **Client education burden** — SMBs don't understand marketing automation. Explaining the value takes time.
4. **Platform dependency** — If Mautic, Twenty, or Matomo stagnate or break, we're exposed (mitigated by open-source community)
