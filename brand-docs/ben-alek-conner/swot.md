# Ben Alek Conner — SWOT Analysis

## Strengths

- **Unique skillset combination.** Director + marketing consultant + AI systems builder. This exact intersection is virtually unoccupied. Filmmakers don't understand marketing. Marketers don't understand storytelling. AI builders don't understand either. Ben does all three.

- **Living proof of concept.** The entire Nunya Bunya marketing stack — self-hosted, AI-powered, fully automated — is a walking portfolio piece for consulting work. "I built this. Let me show you how."

- **Multiple revenue streams.** Directing, consulting, speaking, content. If one dries up, others sustain. This is more resilient than a single-service business.

- **Authentic creative voice.** Ben has genuine filmmaking ambition (features, ORCA, short films) — this isn't a "personal brand" built for LinkedIn clout. The authenticity is real and audiences can feel it.

- **Content compounds.** Every YouTube video, every blog post, every ORCA episode is a permanent asset that builds authority over time. Unlike advertising, content doesn't stop working when you stop paying.

---

## Weaknesses

- **No established audience yet.** YouTube subscribers, LinkedIn followers, and email list are all small. Authority needs to be built from near-zero.

- **Unclear positioning.** "Director and consultant" can confuse people. Are you a filmmaker? A marketer? A tech guy? The answer is "all three" but that's hard to communicate quickly.

- **Time competition with NB.** Nunya Bunya is the primary business and takes priority. BAC creative projects (films, ORCA, YouTube) often get deprioritised.

- **No directing reel.** Limited professional directing samples to show potential clients. Need to create spec work or take on low-pay projects to build the reel.

- **Solo operation.** Directing requires crew. Consulting and content are solo activities, but scaling directing work means hiring or partnering.

- **Brisbane is a small film market.** Limited commercial directing opportunities compared to Sydney or Melbourne. May need to pursue remote/interstate work.

---

## Opportunities

- **"AI for creatives" niche on YouTube.** Very few creators are covering this intersection. BAC can own it with consistent content.

- **ORCA Film Awards as authority builder.** The awards body creates credibility in the film industry. Press credentials at festivals = networking with industry players. Target: 1,000 voting members by 2030.

- **Screen Queensland funding.** PROOF initiative and other grants can fund short film and feature development. This creates portfolio pieces funded by someone else.

- **Corporate video boom.** Every company needs video content now — social media, website, internal comms. Most hire production houses at $10K+. BAC can offer director-quality work at indie budgets.

- **Speaking + workshops.** Conference demand for "AI in creative work" and "open-source marketing" topics is high and growing. Each engagement builds the network and creates content.

- **Cross-sell from NB.** Every NB marketing client needs content (video, photography). BAC directs it, NB distributes it. Built-in pipeline.

---

## Threats

- **AI-generated video.** Sora, Runway, Kling, etc. are improving fast. If AI can generate professional-quality video, the value of a human director decreases for commercial work.

- **Personal brand burnout.** Being the product is psychologically taxing. The pressure to constantly create content, be visible, and "perform" authenticity is real.

- **YouTube algorithm dependency.** Platform risk — if YouTube changes the algorithm or monetisation, content strategy may need to pivot.

- **Feature film is a money pit.** Narrative filmmaking is expensive, risky, and rarely profitable. The creative side could become a financial drain if not managed carefully.

- **Market confusion.** Potential clients may not understand the BAC offering because it spans too many categories. A focused niche (e.g., "AI filmmaker") might convert better than a broad positioning.

- **Competition from AI-native creators.** A new generation of creators is emerging who are AI-native from day one. BAC's advantage is the combination of traditional craft + AI, but that gap may narrow.
