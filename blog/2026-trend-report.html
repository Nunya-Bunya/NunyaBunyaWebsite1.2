The 2026 Trend Report

7 things every Brisbane business needs to know right now — with practical steps you can take today.

March 2026 Edition

If you run a business in Brisbane or South East Queensland, the way your customers find you is changing — fast. Google is different. Social media is different. What worked in 2024 won’t work in 2026.

This report cuts through the noise and gives you the seven biggest shifts happening right now, and more importantly, what you should actually do about each one. Just the stuff that actually matters to your bottom line.

Brisbane’s economy recently surpassed $200 billion[1] (that’s enough to buy every person in Australia a brand new Toyota HiLux) and employment is growing at 3.3% — faster than any other Australian capital.[1] There are more customers out there looking for local businesses than ever before. The question is: can they find yours?
The Trends
1

Google Is Answering Questions Without Sending People to Your Website

80%+
of Google searches now end without a single click to any website.[2][3] That’s like 8 out of every 10 people walking into a shopping centre, getting what they need from the directory sign, and leaving without entering a single shop.

Google has rolled out “AI Overviews”[14] — AI-generated answers that appear at the very top of search results. When someone searches a question, Google now often answers it right there. The user gets their answer and never visits anyone’s website.

What this means for you: Simply having a website isn’t enough anymore. Your website needs to be structured so Google’s AI can read, understand, and reference it. If Google’s AI pulls info from your site, you still get visibility — even without the click.

What to Do About It
  • Structure your website content with clear headings, FAQs, and direct answers to common questions your customers ask
  • Add “schema markup” to your site (your web developer or marketer can do this) — it helps Google understand what your business does
  • Focus on being the most helpful, specific answer to questions in your industry — generic content gets ignored
  • Make sure your Google Business Profile is fully filled out — it’s a major data source for AI results
2

Google Ads Is Handing the Steering Wheel to AI

Google is pushing hard towards AI-managed advertising. Their new systems (“AI Max” and “Performance Max”) can now write your ad headlines, choose your keywords, and even build landing pages — all automatically.[6][7]

The big shift: Instead of manually picking every keyword and writing every ad, you now tell Google your business goals and budget, and their AI builds campaigns around that. Less hands-on, but smarter oversight is essential.

22%
of mobile searches now show paid ads in the local map pack — up from just 1% in early 2025.[4][5] That jumped 22x in one year. Imagine if your electricity bill did that.
What to Do About It
  • If you’re running Google Ads, make sure you (or your marketer) are using the latest AI campaign types — they perform significantly better than old-style manual campaigns
  • Feed Google good data: upload your customer lists, set up proper conversion tracking, and give the AI clear goals
  • For local businesses: consider running local Google Ads — the map pack is now a paid battleground
  • Don’t let AI run completely unsupervised — review weekly and set clear budget guardrails
3

“Near Me” Searches Are More Important Than Ever

Hyperlocal search is the biggest opportunity for Brisbane businesses in 2026. When someone searches “coffee near me,” “plumber Brisbane,” or “car rental Lockyer Valley,” Google shows a map with three businesses. Those three spots get the vast majority of calls and visits.

The businesses that win aren’t necessarily the biggest — they’re the ones with a complete Google Business Profile, consistent information across the internet, genuine reviews, and a website that mentions their service area.

What to Do About It
  • Claim and fully complete your Google Business Profile — fill in every single field, add photos weekly, respond to every review
  • Make sure your business name, address, and phone number are identical everywhere online (website, Facebook, Yellow Pages, industry directories)
  • Actively ask happy customers for Google reviews — it’s one of the strongest ranking signals
  • Create pages on your website for each area you serve (e.g., “Plumbing Services in Paddington” not just “Our Services”)
  • Post updates to your Google Business Profile at least weekly — Google rewards active profiles

Short-form video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) continues to dominate, but the real trend in 2026 is community building. Facebook Groups, local community pages, and niche interest hubs are where loyal customer relationships are built. Australian consumers prefer businesses that feel local and relatable over generic global brands.

What to Do About It
  • Start with one platform and do it well — don’t spread yourself thin across five platforms with mediocre content
  • If you’re a local service business, Facebook is still your best bet — largest AU audience and strongest local community features
  • Invest in short video — even a 30-second clip explaining a common question outperforms polished corporate content
  • Build or join local community groups related to your industry — be helpful, not salesy
  • Focus on authenticity over production quality — customers connect with real people, not stock images
5

Australia’s Under-16 Social Media Ban Changes the Game

Australia has become the first country in the world to legally ban social media for children under 16.[11] Platforms like Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat must now remove or block under-16 accounts.[12]

Why this matters for your business: If any part of your marketing targets families or younger demographics, you need to rethink how you reach them. Younger audiences are being pushed towards YouTube (classified as a video platform, not social media), gaming platforms, and messaging apps.

What to Do About It
  • Review your ad targeting to ensure you’re not wasting budget on age groups no longer on the platforms
  • If you target families, consider shifting some budget to YouTube, email marketing, or local community channels
  • This is actually an opportunity: with fewer under-16s on feeds, your ads may reach your actual target audience more efficiently
6

AI Tools Are Levelling the Playing Field for Small Businesses

In 2026, AI has moved from a buzzword to a practical tool that small businesses use every day. Affordable tools now let small businesses automate customer service, create content, analyse data, and personalise marketing.

But here’s the catch: customers can smell AI-generated content from a mile away. The businesses that win are the ones using AI behind the scenes to work smarter, while keeping their customer-facing communication genuine and human.

What to Do About It
  • Use AI tools to save time on repetitive tasks: drafting social posts, answering common customer questions, summarising data
  • Never publish AI-generated content without a human review — add your personality, local references, and real expertise
  • Use AI for customer insights — tools can analyse your reviews, enquiries, and social comments to spot patterns
  • Start small: pick one area where you spend the most time on repetitive work, and find an AI tool that helps
