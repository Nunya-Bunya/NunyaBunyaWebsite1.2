3.3 The Service Provider will issue valid tax invoices as required by the A New Tax System (Goods and Services Tax) Act 1999 (Cth).

6. WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

6.1 Service Provider Warranties. The Service Provider warrants that:

(a) it has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement and perform the Services;

(b) the Services will be performed with reasonable care and skill;

(c) the Deliverables will not, to the best of the Service Provider's knowledge, infringe any third-party intellectual property rights;

(d) it holds all licences, registrations, and qualifications required to perform the Services.

6.2 Client Warranties. The Client warrants that:

(a) it has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement;

(b) all materials and information provided to the Service Provider are accurate and do not infringe any third-party rights;

(c) it will use the Services and Deliverables in accordance with all applicable laws.

6.3 Consumer Guarantees. Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits any guarantee, right, or remedy available under the Australian Consumer Law (Schedule 2 to the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (Cth)) that cannot be excluded by agreement.

7. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

7.1 Subject to clause 7.3, the Service Provider's total aggregate liability to the Client under or in connection with this Agreement (whether in contract, tort, statute, or otherwise) is limited to the total fees paid by the Client to the Service Provider under this Agreement in the six (6) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

7.2 Subject to clause 7.3, neither Party shall be liable to the other for any:

(a) loss of profit or revenue;

(b) loss of business or contracts;

(c) loss of anticipated savings;

(d) loss of data;

(e) indirect, special, or consequential loss or damage;

whether or not such loss was foreseeable or either Party had been advised of its possibility.

7.3 The limitations in clauses 7.1 and 7.2 do not apply to:

(a) liability arising from fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence;

(b) death or personal injury caused by negligence;

(c) liability that cannot be limited or excluded under applicable law, including under the Australian Consumer Law.

8. TERMINATION

8.1 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement without cause by giving the other Party thirty (30) days' written notice.

8.2 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party:

(a) commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy the breach within fourteen (14) days of receiving written notice specifying the breach;

(b) becomes insolvent, enters into administration, receivership, or liquidation; or

(c) commits fraud or engages in conduct that, in the terminating Party's reasonable opinion, brings that Party or the terminating Party into disrepute.

8.3 Effect of Termination.

(a) Upon termination, the Client shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the date of termination.

(b) If termination is by the Client for convenience, the Client shall pay the Service Provider a cancellation fee equivalent to [20% / specify] of the remaining contract value, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

(c) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party's confidential information.

(d) Clauses relating to intellectual property, confidentiality, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution survive termination.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Notice of Dispute. If a dispute arises out of or in connection with this Agreement, the Party raising the dispute must give written notice to the other Party specifying the nature of the dispute.

9.2 Negotiation. The Parties shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve the dispute through good faith negotiation within twenty-one (21) days of the dispute notice.

9.3 Mediation. If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, either Party may refer the dispute to mediation administered by a mediator agreed upon by the Parties or, failing agreement, appointed by the Resolution Institute (or equivalent body). The costs of mediation are shared equally unless otherwise agreed.

9.4 Litigation. If the dispute is not resolved by mediation within thirty (30) days of the mediator's appointment (or such longer period as agreed), either Party may commence court proceedings.

9.5 Urgent Relief. Nothing in this clause prevents either Party from seeking urgent injunctive or equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction.

10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Queensland, Australia. The Parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Queensland.

10.2 Entire Agreement. This Agreement (including any attached scope of work, schedule, or variation) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior representations, negotiations, and agreements.

10.3 Amendment. This Agreement may only be amended by a written document signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

10.4 Assignment. Neither Party may assign or transfer its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party (not to be unreasonably withheld), except that the Service Provider may subcontract specific tasks to qualified subcontractors, provided the Service Provider remains responsible for the performance of such subcontractors.

10.5 Waiver. Failure to exercise or delay in exercising a right or remedy does not constitute a waiver of that right or remedy.

10.6 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

10.7 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance caused by events beyond its reasonable control (including but not limited to natural disasters, acts of government, or widespread infrastructure failure), provided the affected Party notifies the other in writing as soon as practicable.

10.8 Notices. Notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email (with confirmation of receipt) or registered post to the addresses specified in this Agreement.

10.9 Electronic Execution. This Agreement may be executed by electronic signature, which shall be deemed valid and effective.

10.10 Independent Contractors. The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.
