Process:

  1. Generate invoice using UNIVERSAL INVOICE TEMPLATE
  2. Save as PDF: YYYY-MM-DDBusinessCodeInvoice[InvNumber][ClientName].pdf
  3. File in /Finance/Invoices-Sent/[Business]/[Year]/
  4. Send to client via email with subject: Invoice [Invoice#] — [Business Name] — [Month/Service]
  5. Log in MASTER BUDGET TRACKER (Accounts Receivable tab)
  6. Follow up if unpaid: Day 1 after due date (reminder), Day 7 (phone call), Day 14 (formal demand)

5.2 Expense Tracking

  • All business expenses recorded within 48 hours of incurring
  • Use designated expense tracking app (Xero or MYOB)
  • Receipt required for all expenses over $10
  • Receipt filing: photograph with app immediately, file digitally in /Finance/Receipts/[Business]/[YYYY-MM]/
  • Personal vs. business: never co-mingle; separate bank accounts per entity
  • Credit card reconciliation: weekly (Friday)

5.3 Bookkeeping

  • Bookkeeper reviews all transactions: monthly
  • BAS (Business Activity Statement): quarterly, lodged by 28th of following month
  • GST: collected at 10% on all applicable sales; remitted quarterly
  • Payroll (if applicable): processed fortnightly via payroll software
  • Annual accounts: prepared by registered tax agent; FY close July 31
  • Accountant handoff: all documents by August 31 each year

5.4 Cash Flow Management

  • Minimum cash reserve: 3 months operating expenses per entity
  • Weekly cash position check: Friday EOD
  • Surplus cash: swept to high-interest business savings account monthly
  • Large unexpected expense (>$5,000): Ben approval required
  • Payment terms negotiation: always push for shortest payment terms possible on receivables

5.5 Contractor Payments

  • All contractors must have signed agreement on file before first payment
  • Payments processed: within 7 days of approved invoice
  • Annual PAYG/contractor reconciliation: June each year
  • Superannuation for eligible contractors: paid quarterly (as required by law)

6.2 Content Creation Workflow

Step 1: Ideation (Monthly, last week of prior month)

  • Review content calendar for following month
  • Brainstorm topics using Claude Code (prompt: content ideas for [business] targeting [audience])
  • Map to content pillars and business goals
  • Assign to creator (Ben / VA / contractor)

Step 2: Creation (Tuesday batch sessions)

  • Write copy: first draft in Notion or .md file
  • Visual assets: Canva Pro or Adobe suite
  • Video: CONTENT STUDIO or mobile
  • Review: Ben approves all external-facing content before scheduling

Step 3: Scheduling

  • Schedule 5–7 days ahead
  • Use scheduling tool (Buffer, Later, or native schedulers)
  • Confirm UTM links are appended to any linked content

Step 4: Publish & Engage

  • Monitor for first 2 hours post-publish: respond to comments
  • Engagement window: 24–48 hours actively monitored

Step 5: Repurpose

  • Top-performing content: repurpose across other platforms within 7 days
  • Long-form blog post → LinkedIn article → social snippets → email

6.3 Content Approval Flow

  • VA/contractor creates draft → saves in /Content/Drafts/ → tags Ben in Slack
  • Ben reviews within 48 hours → approves or provides edits
  • Approved content → moved to /Content/Approved/ → scheduled

6.4 Content Quality Standards

  • All copy proofread before publishing (Grammarly or equivalent)
  • No AI-generated content published without human review and personalisation
  • Brand voice maintained per each brand's style guide
  • No posting of client information without explicit written consent
  • Legal content: reviewed by qualified solicitor before publishing

7. CLIENT COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS

7.1 New Lead Response

  • Respond to all new enquiries within 2 business hours
  • Lead response script: acknowledge, qualify, book discovery call
  • CRM entry: create contact record within same business day

7.2 Onboarding New Clients

  1. Send welcome email within 24 hours of signing
  2. Provide onboarding questionnaire / intake form (within 48 hours)
  3. Schedule kickoff call (within 5 business days of signing)
  4. Send contract + invoice for deposit (upfront payment required before work begins)
  5. Create client folder in MASTER WORKSPACE
  6. Add to project management tool
  7. Assign primary point of contact

7.3 Ongoing Client Communication

  • Proactive updates: at least fortnightly for active projects
  • No client should ever have to chase for an update
  • Milestone completions: notify client within 24 hours
  • Concerns or complaints: acknowledge within 2 hours; resolution plan within 24 hours

7.4 Difficult Client Situations

  • Never respond emotionally to difficult messages; wait 30 min minimum
  • Escalate to Ben for any dispute over $500 or involving potential legal action
  • Document all client communications in CRM
  • Termination of engagement: formal written notice per contract terms

7.5 Client Exit Protocol

  • Final invoice sent same day as project completion/termination
  • File retention: per business requirements (law firms: minimum 7 years)
  • Off-boarding: confirm all deliverables handed over; revoke system accesses
  • Testimonial/referral request: sent 14 days after project completion (where appropriate)

9. CRISIS & EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

9.1 What Constitutes a Crisis

  • Legal claim or complaint against any business
  • Data breach or security incident
  • Key client threatening to cancel (revenue >$2K/month)
  • Public negative press or viral negative social media
  • Financial emergency (inability to meet payroll or key obligations)
  • Serious injury or incident on a shoot/event
  • Regulatory or compliance action

9.2 Immediate Response Protocol

  1. Stop and assess — do not react publicly or in writing until assessed
  2. Notify Ben immediately — phone call, not message, within 30 minutes of incident
  3. Document — write factual summary of what happened, when, who is involved
  4. Do not admit liability — in any written or verbal communication
  5. Engage relevant expert — lawyer (for legal/compliance issues), PR (for reputational issues), IT (for security)
  6. Communicate internally — brief relevant team members on a need-to-know basis
  7. Communicate externally — only after legal/strategic advice; single spokesperson (Ben)

9.3 Data Breach Protocol

  1. Identify and contain the breach immediately
  2. Notify Ben within 1 hour
  3. Assess scope: what data, how many individuals, what risk
  4. Notify OAIC (Office of the Australian Information Commissioner) if required under Privacy Act (within 30 days)
  5. Notify affected individuals if required
  6. Document entire incident response
  7. Post-incident: security audit and remediation
