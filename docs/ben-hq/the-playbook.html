The Client Onboarding Pipeline

Every client — internal or external — goes through these two plays before anything else.

The Opening Gambit

What: Full audit of the client's brand + a complete strategy for improvement. This is the FIRST thing we do for any new client. Diagnose before you build.

Produces:

  1. Brand Audit Report — Web presence, social media, local SEO, email, reputation. Each category scored 0-10.
  2. Strategy Document — 90-day plan covering SEO, social, email, content, outreach, paid ads (if applicable).
  3. KPI Dashboard — Current metrics + 30-day and 90-day targets. What we track, how we track it, what good looks like.
  4. Action Plan — Prioritized task list: Week 1 (foundation), Week 2-3 (content engine), Week 4 (outreach), Ongoing.
  5. Tracking Setup — Matomo, Shlink links, Mautic segments, conversion tracking.

Agent: opening_gambit (Directive + Execution in NunyaBunyaAgents)

Status: LIVE — run for every new client

First deployment: Conner Law Group (3 firms: Kevin Conner PI [WA+OR], MBCS Estate Planning)

The Fitout

What: Build everything the Opening Gambit recommended. Website, Google Business Profile, social accounts, email sequences, booking links, content engine, automations, tracking, outreach. This is the execution phase.

Produces:

  1. Website — Built or rebuilt per strategy
  2. Google Business Profile — Setup checklist (manual steps)
  3. Social Accounts — Created, branded, first posts published
  4. Email Marketing — Lead magnet + welcome sequence + nurture sequence in Mautic
  5. Booking System — Cal.com event types per service
  6. Content Engine — First 4 blog posts + 30-day social calendar + tweet queue
  7. Tracking — Matomo, Shlink, UTMs, conversion events, monthly report template
  8. Automations — n8n workflows for blog-to-tweets, alerts, engagement
  9. Outreach — Prospect list, Mautic sequence, follow-up cadence (configured but NOT activated until approved)
  10. Fitout Summary — One-page summary of everything live, all links, what's automated vs. manual

Agent: the_fitout (Directive + Execution in NunyaBunyaAgents)

Rule: The Fitout does NOT start until the Opening Gambit is reviewed and approved by Ben.

Status: LIVE

First deployment: Conner Law Group (after Opening Gambit complete)

The Polish

What: Second full audit of everything The Fitout built. Finds every gap, broken link, missing meta tag, untested flow — and fixes it immediately in the same pass. No separate plan. Just audit and fix.

What it does:

  1. Re-audits every deliverable from The Fitout (website, GBP, social, email, booking, content, tracking, automations, outreach)
  2. Fixes everything it finds — code, config, content, copy
  3. Documents anything requiring manual action (GBP verification, social logins)
  4. Records KPI baselines (Day 0 numbers for all metrics)
  5. Produces client handoff document (everything they need to know)

After The Polish, the client has:

  • Fully audited brand presence
  • Complete marketing strategy with targets
  • Polished website, social accounts, email sequences, booking, tracking
  • Content engine running
  • Automations active
  • KPI baselines recorded
  • Ready for ongoing monthly execution ($3,500/mo retainer)

Agent: the_polish (Directive + Execution in NunyaBunyaAgents)

Opening Gambit → The Fitout → The Polish → Ongoing Retainer
  (diagnose)      (build)      (perfect)    ($3,500/mo)

Chapter 1: Zero to One

Get your first engagements, replies, views, likes, shares, conversions. Everything to get all your firsts.

The Goal

Before you can scale, you need proof that anything works at all. Zero to One is about getting the first signal — a stranger downloads your freebie, a business replies to your email, someone books a call, anyone engages with your content. You're not optimising yet. You're just proving the machine turns on.

Play 1: Build the Content Engine

What: Generate blog posts for all brands, turn each into 5 tweets, queue them for auto-posting.

How we built it:

  • Claude generates blog posts from topic + context
  • Each blog post gets fed through generate-tweets.py → 5 tweets per post
  • Tweets stored in Supabase tweet_queue, approved, posted on schedule
  • Brand-aware scheduling: right brand at the right time of day

Play 12: The LinkedIn Content Play

What: Post the same Ben Alek Conner content (ownership, distribution, solopreneur philosophy) on LinkedIn. LinkedIn organic reach is 10-50x Twitter right now. One good post can get 10,000+ impressions.

Content format that works on LinkedIn:

  • Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
  • Start with a hook line
  • Use the "I did X. Here's what happened." format
  • End with a question
  • No hashtags (they actually hurt reach on LinkedIn now)

Play 15: The Content Swap

What: Find Brisbane businesses with audiences (cafes, coworking spaces, gyms, event venues) and offer a trade: you shoot free photos of their space/team, they share your work with their audience.

Examples:

  • Shoot a coworking space's team → they post it and tag you → their members see your work
  • Shoot a cafe's interior/staff → they use the photos on their social media with your credit
  • Shoot a gym's trainers' headshots → they share with their PT clients

Every swap = exposure to a new audience that you'd never reach through your own channels.

Time: 1 session per week

Rules for Zero to One

  1. Don't optimise yet. You're testing, not perfecting.
  2. Track everything. If it can be measured, measure it.
  3. One week minimum before judging if a play works.
  4. Record failures. A play that doesn't work is still a lesson.
  5. Manual is fine. Automate what works, not what you hope will work.
