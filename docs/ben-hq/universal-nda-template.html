TEMPLATE VARIANTS

This document contains two variants:

  • VARIANT A: Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (both parties share confidential information)
  • VARIANT B: One-Way (Unilateral) Non-Disclosure Agreement (one party discloses)

Delete the variant that does not apply. Both variants use the same defined terms and clauses except where noted.

In connection with this Permitted Purpose, each Party may disclose to the other Party certain confidential and proprietary information. The Parties agree as follows:

CLAUSES

1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, visually, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to:

(a) business plans, strategies, forecasts, and projections;

(b) financial information, revenue figures, pricing, and cost data;

(c) client lists, customer data, and supplier information;

(d) technical data, trade secrets, know-how, inventions, and processes;

(e) software, source code, algorithms, and technical specifications;

(f) marketing plans, campaigns, and creative materials;

(g) personnel information and organisational data;

(h) intellectual property and proprietary methods;

(i) any information derived from the above.

1.2 "Disclosing Party" means, with respect to any particular item of Confidential Information, the Party that discloses that information to the other Party.

1.3 "Receiving Party" means, with respect to any particular item of Confidential Information, the Party that receives that information from the other Party.

1.4 "Representatives" means a Party's directors, officers, employees, contractors, agents, legal advisers, and financial advisers, each of whom has a need to know the Confidential Information for the Permitted Purpose and is bound by obligations of confidentiality no less restrictive than those set out in this Agreement.

2. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

2.1 Each Party, as a Receiving Party, agrees to:

(a) hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence;

(b) not disclose, distribute, or communicate any Confidential Information to any person other than its Representatives who need to know such information for the Permitted Purpose;

(c) use the Confidential Information solely for the Permitted Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever;

(d) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care;

(e) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorised disclosure or use of Confidential Information.

2.2 Each Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

3.1 The obligations in clause 2 do not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:

(a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party or its Representatives;

(b) was rightfully in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction on disclosure;

(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Confidential Information;

(d) is received from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or

(e) was approved in writing by the Disclosing Party for release.

4. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES (LEGAL COMPULSION)

4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) to the extent permitted by law, provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement before disclosure;

(b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate relief; and

(c) disclose only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required to comply with such requirement.

5. RETURN AND DESTRUCTION OF INFORMATION

5.1 Upon the written request of the Disclosing Party, or upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

(a) return to the Disclosing Party all Confidential Information in tangible form; or

(b) destroy all Confidential Information (including copies and notes) and certify in writing that such destruction has occurred.

5.2 The Receiving Party may retain one archival copy of Confidential Information in its legal files to the extent required by law or regulatory obligations, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any right, title, licence, or interest in or to the other Party's Confidential Information or intellectual property, except as expressly stated in this Agreement.

6.2 All Confidential Information remains the property of the Disclosing Party. Disclosure of Confidential Information does not constitute an assignment or licence of any intellectual property rights.

7. NO WARRANTY

7.1 All Confidential Information is provided "as is." Neither Party makes any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for purpose of any Confidential Information disclosed.

8. DURATION

8.1 Standard Term: This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for a period of two (2) years unless earlier terminated by either Party on thirty (30) days' written notice.

8.2 Extended Term (Optional — select if applicable): [Delete if not applicable] Notwithstanding clause 8.1, this Agreement shall continue for a period of five (5) years from the Effective Date.

8.3 Survival: The obligations under this Agreement with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the term shall survive expiry or termination of this Agreement for a further period of three (3) years.

9. REMEDIES FOR BREACH

9.1 The Parties acknowledge that:

(a) a breach of this Agreement would cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy; and

(b) in the event of a breach or threatened breach, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting any bond.

9.2 The Receiving Party shall be liable for all loss, damage, costs (including reasonable legal costs on a solicitor-client basis), and expenses suffered or incurred by the Disclosing Party as a result of any breach of this Agreement.

10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Governing Law: This Agreement is governed by the laws of Queensland, Australia. Each Party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Queensland, Australia.

10.2 Dispute Resolution: Before commencing any legal proceedings (other than seeking urgent injunctive relief), the Parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute through good faith negotiation for a period of twenty-one (21) days following written notice of the dispute.

10.3 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and understandings.

10.4 Amendment: This Agreement may only be amended by written agreement signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

10.5 Waiver: A failure to exercise or delay in exercising a right or remedy under this Agreement does not constitute a waiver of that right or remedy.

10.6 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

10.7 No Agency: Nothing in this Agreement creates any partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the Parties.

10.8 Counterparts: This Agreement may be executed in counterparts (including electronic counterparts), each of which constitutes an original.

DOCUMENT NOTES & COMPLETION GUIDE

Before using this template:

  1. Select Variant A (Mutual) or Variant B (Unilateral) and delete the other
  2. Insert the Effective Date
  3. Complete all party details (full legal name, ACN/ABN, address)
  4. Describe the Permitted Purpose specifically
  5. Select the appropriate duration (Standard 2 years or Extended 5 years) in clause 8
  6. Have a Queensland-admitted solicitor review before execution
  7. Both parties must sign in the presence of a witness (who is not a party to the Agreement)
  8. File executed copy in: MASTER WORKSPACE/[Business]/Clients/[ClientName]/Agreements/
  9. Save as: YYYY-MM-DD[BusinessCode]NDA[PartyName]Signed.pdf
