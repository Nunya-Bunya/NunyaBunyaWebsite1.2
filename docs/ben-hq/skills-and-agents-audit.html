PART 4: HOW, WHEN & WHY TO USE EACH RESOURCE

TIER 1 — USE IMMEDIATELY (This week)

1. Content Quality Scoring (content-ops + content-eval)

Source: ai-marketing-skills/content-ops/ + ai-marketing-skills/content-eval/

WHY: Your agents produce content, but you have no quality gate. Right now, mediocre blog posts and social captions go straight to your approval queue, and YOU are the quality gate. That doesn't scale.

WHEN: Insert between content generation and approval queue. Every piece of content gets scored before it reaches you.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. After your Content Generator / Social Media Agent / Content Machine produces output
  2. Pass output through content-ops expert panel (7-10 simulated experts score it)
  3. If score < 90, it auto-iterates (up to 3 rounds)
  4. Only content scoring 90+ reaches your approval queue
  5. Save scoring data to Supabase for pattern learning

Result: You only review good content. Bad content gets caught and improved automatically.

2. Content Idea Scoring (content-eval)

Source: ai-marketing-skills/content-eval/

WHY: You're generating content from keywords and topics without evaluating whether those topics are worth producing. Some blog posts will get zero traffic because the idea was bad, not the execution.

WHEN: BEFORE your weekly content production pipeline runs. Score ideas first, then only produce the winners.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. Generate 20-30 content ideas per client from your existing sources
  2. Run through content-eval 7-expert panel
  3. Only ideas scoring 85+ enter your Content Generator pipeline
  4. Kill list gets logged (so you don't revisit dead ideas)
  5. Winners get a 4-week production schedule

Result: Every piece of content you produce has a reason to exist.

3. X/Twitter Quality Gate (x-longform-post)

Source: ai-marketing-skills/x-longform-post/

WHY: Your Twitter automation posts content hourly across 4 brands. If any of those tweets read like obvious AI slop, it damages brand credibility. The 24-pattern humanizer check catches AI-sounding patterns before posting.

WHEN: Add as a filter in your n8n tweet poster workflow, between generation and posting.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. After your blog-to-tweets or tweet generation step
  2. Run the 24-pattern AI slop detector
  3. If patterns detected → rewrite automatically
  4. Only humanized tweets pass through to posting
  5. Log detection rate to track improvement over time

Result: Your auto-posted tweets actually sound human.

TIER 2 — USE THIS MONTH (April-May 2026)

4. Growth Engine (Experimentation Framework)

Source: ai-marketing-skills/growth-engine/

WHY: You have 18 plays in THE PLAYBOOK but no way to know which ones are actually working. The Growth Engine creates proper A/B experiments with statistical analysis, auto-promotes winners, and suggests next experiments.

WHEN: After your plays have been running for 2+ weeks and you have baseline data from Matomo/Mautic.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. Define experiments for each active play (e.g., "Does email subject line A or B get more opens?")
  2. Connect to Matomo (page views, conversions) and Mautic (open rates, click rates) for data
  3. Log data points daily via your n8n workflows
  4. Growth Engine auto-scores experiments and promotes winners to a living playbook
  5. Weekly scorecard posted to Slack #alerts

Result: Data-driven decisions instead of gut feelings. You stop doing things that don't work.

5. AI Search Optimization (GetCito + LocalSEO ai-local-search)

Source: getcito/ + localseoskills/ai-local-search/

WHY: Over 50% of local searches now show AI Overviews. If NB, PP, and client businesses aren't optimized for AI search, they're invisible to a growing share of searchers. This is where SEO is heading — get ahead now.

WHEN: After your traditional SEO is running (which it is). Layer this on top.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. Run GetCito audit on all your live sites (NB, PP, Pawesome, ORCA)
  2. Identify how each brand appears (or doesn't) in AI search results
  3. Use LocalSEO ai-local-search skill to optimize: entity clarity, content structure, web presence breadth
  4. Add AI search visibility checks to your weekly SEO cycle
  5. Track changes over time in Matomo + GetCito

Result: Your brands show up in Perplexity, ChatGPT search, and Google AI Overview — not just Google's blue links.

6. GBP Optimization (LocalSEO skills)

Source: localseoskills/gbp-optimization/ + localseoskills/gbp-posts/

WHY: You collect GBP metrics daily with your GBP Metrics Collector, but you're not ACTING on them. GBP optimization (categories, attributes, photos, Q&A, posts) directly impacts map pack ranking.

WHEN: Now — for Power Portraits and Pawesome Dog Walkers (both are local businesses that live or die by local search).

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. Load gbp-optimization skill, run audit on PP and Pawesome GBP profiles
  2. Optimize categories, attributes, business descriptions, photos
  3. Set up gbp-posts cadence (weekly GBP posts with keywords)
  4. Wire GBP posting into your n8n weekly content workflow
  5. Track map pack position changes via your existing GBP Metrics Collector

Result: PP and Pawesome rank higher in Google Maps. More calls, more bookings.

7. Citation Management (LocalSEO)

Source: localseoskills/local-citations/

WHY: NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across directories is a top-5 local ranking factor. If PP or Pawesome have inconsistent or missing listings across Yellow Pages, True Local, Yelp, etc., it hurts rankings.

WHEN: After GBP optimization. Do a citation audit, then build/fix citations.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. Run citation audit for PP and Pawesome
  2. Identify inconsistencies and missing listings
  3. Build Tier 1 citations first (Google, Apple, Bing, Facebook, Yelp)
  4. Then Tier 2-3 (industry-specific, local directories)
  5. Log all citations in a tracking sheet

Result: Clean, consistent presence across all directories. Better local rankings.

8. Revenue Attribution (revenue-intelligence)

Source: ai-marketing-skills/revenue-intelligence/

WHY: When you land your first NB retainer client, they'll ask "what am I getting for $3,500/month?" You need to show content → lead → revenue attribution. Without this, you can't prove ROI.

WHEN: As soon as you have paying clients. Set up the attribution pipeline now so data is collecting from day one.

HOW TO INTEGRATE:

  1. Connect Matomo (traffic source → page → conversion)
  2. Connect Mautic (email → click → action)
  3. Connect Twenty CRM (lead → deal → revenue)
  4. Map content pieces to closed deals using first-touch/multi-touch models
  5. Generate attribution reports showing "this blog post generated $X in revenue"

Result: You can prove ROI to clients. Retention goes up. Upsells become easy.
