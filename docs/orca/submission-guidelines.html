# ORCA Film Awards 2026 — Submission Guidelines

Official Filmmaker Submission Guidelines

Brisbane, Australia | Submissions Open: 1 February 2026 | Final Deadline: 31 August 2026

Welcome to ORCA Film Awards

ORCA Film Awards exists to celebrate the craft of cinema — to honour the filmmakers who push the boundaries of storytelling, who pour their vision onto the screen with courage and conviction. Whether you are an emerging voice or an established auteur, ORCA is the platform where Australian and international cinema meets its audience.

We are proud to be Brisbane's premier film awards event and content platform, dedicated to recognising excellence across every discipline of filmmaking. These guidelines have been designed to ensure a transparent, fair, and prestigious submission process for all entrants.

Please read this document in full before submitting. Incomplete or non-compliant submissions may be disqualified without refund.

1. Eligibility Criteria

1.1 Who May Submit

  • Filmmakers of any nationality, background, or career stage are welcome to submit.
  • Films may have been produced anywhere in the world.
  • At least one key creative (director, producer, or writer) must be contactable and available for communication throughout the judging period.
  • Films must not have been previously submitted to ORCA Film Awards in a prior year in the same category.

1.2 Film Types Accepted

ORCA Film Awards accepts entries across the following primary categories:

1.3 Length Categories

Films are assessed within the following length divisions:

Submission fees are applied per division. If a film qualifies in multiple divisions, the entrant selects the most appropriate category at submission. Changes after payment are not guaranteed.

1.4 Completion Period

Films must have been completed (principal photography and post-production finalised) no earlier than 1 January 2023. Retrospective submissions for older works will not be accepted.

1.5 Language Requirements

Films in any language are accepted. Non-English language films must include English subtitles embedded in the submission file or provided as a separate SRT/VTT file. Failure to include subtitles for non-English dialogue may result in scoring penalties at the judges' discretion.

2.3 Required Accompanying Materials

Every submission must include the following:

  1. Completed online submission form — filmmaker details, film details, synopsis, genre, runtime
  2. Film file — per technical specifications above, uploaded via the secure portal
  3. Poster or key art — minimum 1920 x 2700px, JPEG or PNG
  4. Director's statement — 100 to 400 words, plain text or PDF
  5. Short synopsis — up to 150 words
  6. Full credits list — PDF or plain text, listing all principal cast and crew
  7. Proof of rights declaration — a signed declaration confirming the entrant holds all necessary rights to submit the work (template provided in the submission portal)

2.4 Optional Supporting Materials

  • Behind-the-scenes stills (minimum 2400px wide, JPEG or TIFF)
  • Trailer or teaser (MP4 or MOV, under 3 minutes)
  • EPK (Electronic Press Kit) in PDF format
  • Previous festival screening history

3.6 Student Film

Student submissions receive a 30% discount across all tiers. Proof of enrolment (current student ID or letter from institution) must be uploaded at the time of submission. Discounts cannot be applied retrospectively after payment.

3.7 Fee Waivers

A limited number of fee waivers are available for filmmakers experiencing genuine financial hardship. Applications must be submitted in writing to submissions@orcafilmawards.com.au before the submission deadline. Waivers are granted at the discretion of the ORCA team and are not guaranteed.

4. Submission Process

Step 1 — Create Your Account

Visit the ORCA submission portal at portal.orcafilmawards.com.au and create a filmmaker account. Use a valid email address you monitor regularly. All correspondence regarding your submission will be sent to this address.

Step 2 — Start a New Submission

From your dashboard, select "New Submission" and choose your film category and length division. Fill out all required fields completely and accurately. Incomplete forms cannot be progressed to payment.

Step 3 — Upload Your Film File

Upload your film file via the secure uploader. Large files may take significant time depending on your internet connection. Do not close your browser during upload. You will receive an upload confirmation email once the transfer is complete.

Step 4 — Upload Supporting Materials

Upload your poster, director's statement, credits list, proof of rights declaration, and any optional materials. All files are stored securely and accessed only by authorised ORCA staff and judges.

Step 5 — Review and Confirm

Review your submission summary carefully before proceeding. Ensure all details — film title, director name, runtime, category — are accurate. Corrections after payment require written request and cannot always be accommodated.

Step 6 — Pay the Entry Fee

Select your applicable fee tier and proceed to payment. Payments are processed via secure Stripe gateway. Accepted methods: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Apple Pay, Google Pay. A tax invoice will be issued by email.

Step 7 — Submission Confirmation

You will receive a confirmation email with your unique submission reference number. Keep this number — it is your identifier throughout the entire process. Your film is now officially entered into ORCA Film Awards 2026.

5. What Happens After Submission

5.1 Initial Review

All submissions undergo an initial technical and eligibility review by the ORCA submissions team within 10 business days of receipt. If your submission is found to be ineligible or technically non-compliant, you will be notified by email with the reason. Where possible, you may be given an opportunity to rectify the issue and resubmit.

5.2 Selection Phase

Eligible submissions are assigned to judging panels. Each film is reviewed by a minimum of two qualified judges. Judges are film industry professionals — directors, cinematographers, editors, screenwriters, producers — selected for their expertise and credibility.

5.3 Shortlisting

Shortlisted films are notified by email no later than 30 September 2026. Shortlisted filmmakers are invited to attend the ORCA Film Awards ceremony and are included in the public nominees announcement.

5.4 Finalists and Winners

Finalists are announced publicly on 15 October 2026. Winners are revealed exclusively at the ORCA Film Awards ceremony in Brisbane. Winners receive an ORCA Award trophy, a certificate of achievement, and are promoted across all ORCA platforms and marketing channels throughout the season.

5.5 Screening Rights

By entering ORCA Film Awards, you grant ORCA a non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to screen or exhibit your film during the ORCA Film Awards event and associated programming for the 2026 season. This does not affect your ownership or any commercial distribution rights.

7. Rights and Permissions

7.1 Ownership

You must own or control all rights necessary to submit and exhibit the work, including music, footage, and performance rights. ORCA takes no responsibility for rights disputes arising from submitted content.

7.2 Third-Party Content

If your film incorporates third-party music, stock footage, archival material, or other licensed content, you must hold valid licences for festival and awards event use. Submission of a work containing improperly licensed material may result in disqualification.

7.3 ORCA's Use of Your Materials

ORCA may use your film's title, poster, still frames, director's statement, and synopsis for promotional purposes related to the ORCA Film Awards (website, social media, press materials, ceremony presentations). If you have objections to specific promotional uses, please notify us in writing at the time of submission.

8. Terms and Conditions

  1. Non-Refundable Fees. All submission fees are non-refundable, including in cases of disqualification, technical issues, or withdrawal.
  2. One Submission Per Film Per Category. Each film may be entered in multiple categories, but duplicate entries within the same category will be removed and no refund issued.
  3. Accurate Information. Entrants are responsible for the accuracy of all submission information. Misrepresentation — including false claims of student status — may result in disqualification.
  4. Withdrawal. If you wish to withdraw your film from consideration, notify us in writing before 31 August 2026. Withdrawal does not entitle you to a refund.
  5. Force Majeure. ORCA Film Awards reserves the right to postpone, cancel, or modify the event or any of its programs due to circumstances beyond its control. In such cases, ORCA's liability to entrants is limited to reasonable notification of changes.
  6. Disqualification. ORCA reserves the right to disqualify any submission that violates these guidelines, contains unlawful content, or is submitted in bad faith.
  7. Governing Law. These terms are governed by the laws of Queensland, Australia.
  8. Agreement. By submitting a film and paying the entry fee, you confirm that you have read and agree to these submission guidelines and terms in full.
