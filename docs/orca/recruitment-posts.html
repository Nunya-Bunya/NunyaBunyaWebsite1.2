ORCA Film Awards -- 10 Days of Recruitment Posts

Instructions: Post one per day, March 26 - April 4. Adapt as needed. Each entry has a Twitter/X version (under 280 characters) and a longer Instagram/Facebook version.

Day 1 -- Wednesday, March 26

Theme: Introduction / What is ORCA

Twitter/X:

The Oscars celebrate the same 10 films every year. We celebrate the other 10,000.

ORCA Film Awards is recruiting volunteer voters and critics. No experience needed. Just a genuine love of underseen cinema.

Voting is open now through April 4. DM us or link below.

Instagram/Facebook:

Every year, thousands of incredible films get made. Foreign dramas that never leave the festival circuit. Documentaries that reshape how you see the world. Indies that play one weekend and vanish. Animation that pushes the medium forward.

The Oscars ignore most of them. We don't.

ORCA Film Awards is an awards body -- like the Oscars, but for the films the Oscars overlook. We nominate and vote on the best underseen films of the year.

Right now, our 2026 voting period is open, and we're looking for volunteer voters and critics to join us.

What you'd do:

-- Watch and evaluate nominated films

-- Write film criticism and essays

-- Vote on which overlooked films deserve recognition

-- Join a community of cinephiles who care about this stuff

No experience required. No fees. Just a love of film.

Voting runs through April 4. Link in bio to sign up.

Hashtags: #ORCAFilmAwards #UndersseenCinema #FilmTwitter #IndieFilm #ForeignFilm #FilmCriticism #Cinephile #SupportIndieFilm

Day 2 -- Thursday, March 27

Theme: The problem we're solving

Twitter/X:

Name a film you loved this year that has zero chance at an Oscar nomination.

That's exactly the kind of film ORCA exists for. We're recruiting voters now -- 9 days left. Come help us fix the awards conversation.

Instagram/Facebook:

Think about the best film you saw this year. Now think about whether it'll get an Oscar nomination.

If the answer is no -- if it's too foreign, too small, too weird, too niche, too honest -- that's exactly why ORCA exists.

We don't compete with the Oscars. We fill the gap they leave behind. Every year, we nominate and celebrate the films that deserve recognition but won't get it from the mainstream awards circuit.

We're recruiting volunteer voters and critics right now. Our 2026 voting period closes April 4.

If you're the kind of person who watches a film nobody's heard of and immediately wants to tell someone about it -- you belong here.

No experience needed. Sign up link in bio.

Day 3 -- Friday, March 28

Theme: What voters actually do

Twitter/X:

What do ORCA volunteer voters actually do?

Watch underseen films. Write about them. Vote on the best ones. Talk about cinema with people who care as much as you do.

That's it. No gatekeeping. Voting open through April 4.

Instagram/Facebook:

People keep asking what being an ORCA voter actually involves, so here's the honest breakdown:

You watch films. Specifically, the kinds of films that don't get wide releases or mainstream awards attention -- foreign language films, documentaries, experimental work, tiny indies, animation, short films.

You write about them. Not 5,000-word academic papers (unless you want to). Just genuine criticism and engagement with the work.

You vote. You help decide which underseen films get the recognition they deserve through our annual awards.

You join a community. You talk about film with other people who stay up too late watching obscure cinema and have opinions about aspect ratios.

That's the gig. No prerequisites. No membership fees. No film school degree required.

If you care about cinema beyond what's playing at your local multiplex, you're qualified.

8 days left to join before voting closes April 4.

Hashtags: #ORCAFilmAwards #FilmCriticism #FilmWriting #Cinephile #FilmVoting #JoinUs #UndersseenCinema #FilmCommunity

Theme: Specific films / making it tangible

Twitter/X:

Films like these are why ORCA exists -- the ones that play 3 festivals, get 4.2 on Letterboxd, and vanish without a single awards mention.

Someone has to champion them. Why not you?

Volunteer voting open through April 4.

Instagram/Facebook:

You know those films. The ones you discover on Letterboxd at 1am. The ones your friend who studied abroad recommends. The ones that play Cannes or Venice or TIFF and then... nothing.

No theatrical release. No awards buzz. No discourse. Just a small group of people who saw it and can't stop thinking about it.

That's the space ORCA lives in. We exist to make sure those films don't disappear without recognition.

Our volunteer voters watch these films, write about them, and vote on the best of the year. It's a community of people who believe cinema is bigger than what Hollywood decides to market.

We're adding voters right now for our 2026 cycle. Voting closes April 4 -- 7 days from today.

Come be part of this.

Instagram/Facebook:

Here's what ORCA is NOT:

-- Not a film festival (we don't accept submissions)

-- Not an industry insider club

-- Not a corporate awards show

-- Not exclusive or gatekept

Here's what ORCA IS:

-- An awards body for underseen cinema

-- Run by film lovers, for film lovers

If that sounds like your people, we're recruiting volunteer voters right now. Voting closes April 4.
