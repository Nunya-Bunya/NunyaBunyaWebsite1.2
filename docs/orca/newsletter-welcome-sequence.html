Email 24 (Replace) — Day 0

Subject: Welcome to ORCA — here are 5 films to start with

Preview: Films the Oscars won't tell you about.

Hi {contactfield=firstname|film lover},

Welcome to the ORCA Film Awards community. You've just joined a growing group of cinephiles who believe great cinema shouldn't need a gold statue to be recognized.

Here's the deal: every year, the Academy overlooks dozens of extraordinary films. We find them, celebrate them, and make sure they get the audience they deserve.

To kick things off, here are 5 films from our past nominees you should watch this week:

  1. Past Lives (2023) — A haunting meditation on fate, choice, and the lives we almost lived. Celine Song's debut is pure cinema.
  2. The Worst Person in the World (2021) — Joachim Trier's portrait of a woman in her late twenties navigating love and identity in Oslo. Funny, devastating, and completely honest.
  3. Decision to Leave (2022) — Park Chan-wook at his most restrained and romantic. A detective falls for his murder suspect. Every frame is a painting.
  4. Saint Omer (2022) — Alice Diop's courtroom drama about a woman on trial for infanticide. Based on a real case. Quiet, devastating filmmaking.
  5. Close (2022) — Lukas Dhont's story of two 13-year-old boys whose intense friendship is pulled apart by social pressure. Will wreck you.

Over the next few days, we'll share more about what ORCA does, how you can get involved, and why we think the conversation around great cinema needs to change.

— The ORCA Team

Email 25 (Replace) — Day 3

Subject: Why these films matter

Preview: The Oscars have a blind spot. We don't.

Hi {contactfield=firstname|there},

The Oscars have always had blind spots. Foreign language films. Documentaries that aren't about war. Indies without A-list casts. Animation that isn't Pixar.

ORCA was created to fill that gap.

We're not anti-Oscar. We're pro-cinema. We believe that a film from Senegal deserves the same critical attention as a film from Hollywood. That a documentary shot on an iPhone can be as powerful as a $200M blockbuster. That "underseen" doesn't mean "undeserving."

What we actually do:

  • Nominate films across 20 categories that the Academy ignores
  • Publish original film criticism, reviews, and essays
  • Build a community of volunteer voters and critics
  • Curate weekly film recommendations (like the ones you got in our last email)

Our long-term vision: 1,000 voting members by 2030, press credentials at major festivals, and eventually a full awards ceremony that rivals the Oscars in prestige — if not in budget.

Sound ambitious? Good. Great cinema deserves ambitious advocacy.

Email 26 (Replace) — Day 6

Subject: Want to vote? Here's how to join us.

Preview: We're looking for film critics, not film snobs.

Hi {contactfield=firstname|there},

ORCA runs on volunteers. Our voters are film lovers from around the world — critics, bloggers, filmmakers, professors, and regular people who just watch a lot of movies.

Here's what being an ORCA voter looks like:

  • You watch the nominated films (we give you the list)
  • You vote across 20 categories during our voting window
  • You get your name listed as an official ORCA voter
  • You join a private community of cinephiles who actually care about this stuff

Requirements: Love film. Watch at least 20 of the nominated films. That's it. No credentials needed. No gatekeeping.

We're currently building toward our goal of 1,000 voting members. Right now, every voice matters more than it will once we're established.

If this sounds like your thing, register to vote below. It takes 2 minutes.

CTA: Register as a Voter

CTA Link: https://orcafilmawards.com/vote

If voting isn't your thing, that's fine too. Stick around for the weekly film recommendations and essays. We're glad you're here either way.

— The ORCA Team
