ORCA Film Awards — Source of Truth

Last Updated: 2026-04-05

Identity

  • What ORCA is: An awards body where members nominate and vote on existing films. It is NOT a film festival. It does NOT accept submissions from filmmakers.
  • Based in: Brisbane, QLD. National (Australia-wide) reach target.
  • Revenue: Pre-revenue. No monetization plan yet. Focus is on building credibility, membership, and content.
  • Legal structure: TBD (Pty Ltd vs incorporated association)
  • ABN/GST: Not yet registered

Trajectory (in order)

  1. Voting body
  2. Film news/review publication
  3. Press credentials at major festivals
  4. Full awards ceremony (long-term: rival the Oscars)

Long-Term Targets

  • 50 voting members by end of 2026
  • 1,000 voting members by 2030
  • Physical awards ceremony (eventual)

Phase 2: Film Publication Strategy

The Big Picture (what we're actually building)

Building in this order:

  1. Content (articles) — establish taste and voice
  2. Taste (editorial identity) — what "good writing" looks like on ORCA
  3. People (writers/community) — early contributors become future voters
  4. Authority (recognition) — press credentials, festival coverage
  5. Awards (later) — feels earned, not forced

Ben's Role: Editor-in-Chief

  • Write 1 article per week minimum
  • Set the tone for depth, taste, and quality
  • Early on, Ben IS the brand
  • Light editing of contributor work (polish tone + clarity, don't over-control voice)

What to Write (high-leverage content)

Avoid generic reviews. Focus on:

  • "Why [film] matters" (not just review)
  • "Underrated films you missed this month"
  • Deep dives into one scene / theme
  • Festival coverage (even if remote)
  • "5 Underrated Festival Films You Haven't Heard Of"
  • "Themes Emerging from [Festival] 2026"
  • "Why [Festival] Still Matters for Indie Voices"
  • Comparative pieces ("Berlin vs Cannes — politics in festival programming")

Weekly Rhythm

  • 3-10 articles total (mix of Ben + contributors)
  • Quality over quantity — 1 great article beats 20 mediocre posts

Editorial Rules

  • No AI spam
  • Original voice
  • Focus on the niche (indie, foreign, underseen)

Volunteer Writer Program

Who to Recruit

Obsessed film nerds with taste. Not "top writers." Target:

  • Reddit film subs, Letterboxd-style communities
  • Twitter/X film circles
  • Discord film servers
  • Letterboxd comments sections
  • Film students at Australian universities
  • Festival volunteers (they see films early, have access to press screenings)

Recruitment Pitch (tone)

Don't say: "write for my website"

Say: "We're building a platform focused on thoughtful discussion of independent and under-seen films. Early contributors will shape the voice of the site and become founding members of our voting body."

What Writers Get

1. Identity

  • "Founding Contributor" title
  • Profile pages on the site
  • Recognition

2. Influence

  • Their writing shapes what films get attention
  • Input on future awards

3. Pathway

  • Top writers become editors
  • Editors become core voting members
  • Contributors = stakeholders in the platform

Writer Commitments

  • 1 article per month minimum
  • 1 festival writeup per season

Contributor Roles

  • Festival Correspondent
  • Features Editor
  • Reviews Editor

Contributor Pipeline

Phase 1: Contributors (write articles)

Phase 2: Core community (shape editorial direction)

Phase 3: Voting body (nominate and vote on awards)

Scaling Targets

  • Start with 3-5 contributors
  • Don't spread too thin early

Press Accreditation Strategy

How Press Credentials Work

Most festivals offer "Press" credentials to writers with:

  • A live outlet (website with published content)
  • Portfolio of writing
  • Coverage plans

Steps to Get Press Passes

  1. Build enough published content on the ORCA website
  2. Start with smaller/local festivals (easier to get approved)
  3. Submit editorial calendar + examples of existing coverage
  4. Apply on behalf of ORCA writers

Priority Festivals for First Press Applications

  • Flickerfest (shorts, Australia)
  • Melbourne International Film Festival (MIFF)
  • Brisbane Asia Pacific Film Festival
  • Gold Coast Film Festival
  • Adelaide Film Festival
  • Sydney Film Festival

Eventual International Press Targets

  • Cannes, Venice, TIFF, Sundance, Berlinale

Partnership Opportunities

Reach out to:

  • Smaller festivals — offer coverage and reviews
  • Local cinemas
  • Film societies

Content Strategy Per Festival

Before the festival:

  • Publish overview and anticipation piece
  • Introduce key films to watch

During (remote screening window):

  • Reviews and top picks
  • Curated lists (shorts, docs, hidden gems)

After:

  • Year-round takeaways
  • Connect local taste with global coverage

Seasonal Content Focus

January - March: Introductory festival essays, roundups from early fests, filmmaker profiles

April - June: Cannes buzz deep dives, comparative pieces, Aussie focus around Sydney/Melbourne

August - October: Editor's picks from Edinburgh & MIFF, "hidden gems from fall festivals", documentary spotlights

Year-End: Best of year roundups, seasonal cinematic themes, awards season build-ups (TIFF/Venice/IDFA feed Oscar discourse)

Remote Coverage Tips

  1. Online passes — Many festivals offer digital/online screening passes (Sundance, SXSW, others via Eventive platform at watch.eventive.org)
  2. Press screeners — Even in-person festivals sometimes provide virtual screeners for press
  3. Curate themes, not just reviews — thematic roundups perform better than individual reviews
  4. Remote windows are short — usually just a few days, plan content accordingly

Key Insight: The Gap ORCA Can Fill

Most Australian outlets do NOT send large rosters of writers to major international festivals. They send 1-2 delegates, syndicate freelance reviews, and rely on remote press access. There is room for a platform that recruits and supports a network of writers who attend festivals and publish consistently — especially covering indie, foreign, and niche cinema from an Australian perspective.

Reference: Matt Neglia Model

Matt Neglia (Next Best Picture) is a key inspiration — built a credible awards-focused publication with a distinctive voice. ORCA aims for a similar model but in its own lane: focused on underseen/indie/foreign films rather than mainstream Oscar contention.

Key Warnings

  • Don't spread too thin early. Better: 1 great article/week + 3-5 contributors than 20 mediocre posts with no identity.
  • Consistency is everything — weekly output, curation over volume, community ownership.
  • Most people try to skip straight to awards — that's why they fail. Build content, taste, people, authority FIRST.
