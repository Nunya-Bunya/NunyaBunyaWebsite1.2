1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Conner Law Group is a Brisbane-based legal services umbrella organisation comprising four strategically aligned business units: Merkel & Conner (commercial and business law), Conner Injury Law (personal injury litigation), MBCS (medical-legal consulting and support services), and KevLaw AI (legal technology SaaS platform). Together, these entities form a vertically integrated legal services ecosystem capable of serving individuals, businesses, and the broader legal industry across Queensland and nationally.

The firm operates from Brisbane, Queensland, and is positioned to capitalise on Queensland's growing population, expanding business sector, and the significant volume of personal injury claims processed through the Queensland court system and the Motor Accident Insurance Commission (MAIC). In parallel, KevLaw AI represents a high-growth, scalable revenue stream extending the firm's commercial footprint well beyond the traditional legal services market.

Financial objective: Conner Law Group targets multi-million AUD in combined annual revenue within 36 months, with KevLaw AI contributing a growing share of recurring SaaS revenue alongside established legal service streams.

This business plan sets out the strategic, operational, and financial framework by which Conner Law Group will achieve these objectives. It is intended for internal planning, senior leadership alignment, and authorised external briefings.

To deliver exceptional legal outcomes for individuals and businesses through expert representation, compassionate client service, and technology-driven efficiency — making quality legal services accessible across Queensland and beyond.

Vision Statement

To become Queensland's most trusted, innovative, and integrated legal services group — a firm where clients receive not merely legal advice, but complete legal solutions backed by deep expertise, genuine care, and industry-leading technology.

Core Values

Every client engagement, every legal submission, and every internal decision is guided by unwavering ethical standards. We do not cut corners, and we do not compromise on honesty.

3. BUSINESS STRUCTURE & BRAND ARCHITECTURE

Conner Law Group operates as the parent umbrella brand over the following four entities:

3.1 Merkel & Conner — Commercial & Business Law

A commercial law firm serving small-to-medium enterprises, startups, and established Queensland businesses with expertise in contracts, corporate structuring, employment law, property transactions, dispute resolution, and regulatory compliance.

3.2 Conner Injury Law — Personal Injury Litigation

A plaintiff-side personal injury firm specialising in motor vehicle accidents, workplace injuries, public liability claims, and medical negligence under Queensland's statutory and common law frameworks, including CTP claims via MAIC.

3.3 MBCS — Medical-Legal Consulting & Support Services

A specialist medical-legal support unit providing medico-legal report coordination, independent medical examination (IME) scheduling, medical chronologies, file preparation, and expert witness liaison for law firms across Queensland and nationally.

3.4 KevLaw AI — Legal Technology SaaS

A proprietary legal technology platform offering AI-assisted tools for law firms including document automation, precedent generation, client intake workflows, file management dashboards, and compliance tracking. Sold on a subscription (SaaS) model to law firms nationally.

4. SERVICES OVERVIEW

4.1 Conner Injury Law — Personal Injury Services

Motor Vehicle Accidents (CTP Claims)

Claims under Queensland's compulsory third party (CTP) insurance scheme administered by MAIC. Representation from initial notice through to negotiation, compulsory conference, and litigation in the District and Supreme Courts of Queensland.

Workplace Injuries

Workers' compensation claims under the Workers' Compensation and Rehabilitation Act 2003 (Qld), including statutory claims with WorkCover Queensland, common law damages claims, and self-insurer disputes.

Public Liability

Claims arising from injuries suffered on public or private premises, including slip and fall incidents, inadequate maintenance, and occupier's liability under the Civil Liability Act 2003 (Qld).

Medical Negligence

Complex claims involving substandard medical treatment, surgical errors, misdiagnosis, and failure to obtain informed consent. Handled by experienced practitioners in partnership with specialist medical experts.

Total Permanent Disability (TPD) & Income Protection

Superannuation-linked TPD claims and income protection disputes managed alongside personal injury matters.

4.2 Merkel & Conner — Business Law Services

Commercial Contracts

Drafting, reviewing, and negotiating service agreements, supply agreements, shareholder agreements, joint venture agreements, franchise agreements, and non-disclosure agreements.

Corporate Structuring

Advice on entity selection, company formation, trust structures, restructuring, and corporate governance for Queensland businesses.

Employment Law

Representing employers and executives in unfair dismissal matters, enterprise bargaining, employment contract preparation, restraint of trade enforcement, and workplace investigations.

Property & Leasing

Commercial property transactions, due diligence, lease reviews, and development agreements.

Business Dispute Resolution

Mediation, arbitration, and litigation services for contractual disputes, partnership disputes, and commercial litigation in Queensland courts.

Startup & SME Advisory

Founder agreements, IP protection, investment term sheets, and regulatory compliance for emerging Queensland businesses.

4.3 MBCS — Medical-Legal Consulting & Support Services

Medico-Legal Report Coordination

End-to-end coordination of independent medical examinations and medico-legal reports for law firms handling personal injury, workers' compensation, and medical negligence matters.

Medical Chronology Preparation

Detailed, court-ready medical chronologies prepared by experienced paralegals and medical professionals.

Expert Witness Liaison

Identification, briefing, and management of expert witnesses across medical, engineering, and vocational disciplines.

File Preparation Services

Outsourced file preparation and bundling for hearings and mediations, enabling law firms to reduce internal resource demands.

Rehabilitation & Return-to-Work Coordination

Liaising with treating practitioners and rehabilitation providers to support clients' recovery and document functional capacity for legal proceedings.

4.4 KevLaw AI — Legal Technology SaaS

Document Automation

AI-assisted generation of legal documents including correspondence, court forms, notices of claim, demand letters, and retainer agreements using firm-specific precedents.

Client Intake Automation

Customisable digital intake forms integrated with CRM and matter management platforms, reducing administrative burden and improving data capture accuracy.

AI Legal Research Assistant

Jurisdiction-aware legal research prompting and summarisation tools calibrated for Queensland and Australian law.

Compliance Tracker

Automated deadline and compliance tracking for litigation milestones, statutory timeframes, and court filing deadlines.

Precedent Library Management

Cloud-based precedent management with version control, access permissions, and AI-assisted drafting suggestions.

5. TARGET MARKETS

5.1 Conner Injury Law — Primary Markets

  • Individuals injured in motor vehicle accidents across South East Queensland, with a focus on Greater Brisbane
  • Workers injured in Queensland workplaces, particularly in construction, manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare
  • Individuals injured in public spaces including shopping centres, hospitality venues, and recreational facilities
  • Clients with complex or high-value personal injury claims requiring experienced senior practitioners

Demographics: Adults aged 18–65, employed or formerly employed, with limited prior experience of the legal system. Many clients will be referred by treating practitioners, allied health providers, or prior clients.

5.2 Merkel & Conner — Primary Markets

  • Queensland SMEs with 2–200 employees across professional services, construction, retail, hospitality, and technology sectors
  • Business owners seeking transactional and advisory legal support without top-tier CBD firm pricing
  • Startups and scale-ups in Brisbane's growing technology and creative economy
  • Property developers and investors requiring commercial property legal services

5.3 MBCS — Primary Markets

  • Personal injury law firms in Queensland and nationally that lack in-house medical-legal support capability
  • Sole practitioners and small firms handling WorkCover, CTP, and public liability matters
  • Insurance companies and corporate defendants seeking independent expert coordination
  • Litigation support companies seeking specialist subcontractors

5.4 KevLaw AI — Primary Markets

  • Small-to-medium law firms (1–50 lawyers) in Queensland and nationally
  • Personal injury and workers' compensation specialist firms with high document volume
  • Progressive mid-tier firms seeking to reduce administrative costs and improve efficiency
  • Legal aid organisations and community legal centres seeking cost-effective automation tools

6. COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

6.1 Vertical Integration

Conner Law Group's unique structure means a personal injury client can receive legal representation (Conner Injury Law), medical-legal support coordination (MBCS), and digital file management (KevLaw AI) all within a single ecosystem. This reduces friction, improves case quality, and creates operational efficiencies unavailable to standalone competitors.

6.2 Specialisation Without Sacrificing Breadth

Each business unit is a genuine specialist in its domain. Conner Injury Law focuses exclusively on plaintiff-side personal injury — it does not represent insurers or dilute its expertise across unrelated practice areas. This specialist positioning commands referrals from treating practitioners and allied health providers.

6.3 Technology Differentiation via KevLaw AI

No other Queensland personal injury firm has built a proprietary legal technology SaaS platform. KevLaw AI provides Conner Law Group with an internal competitive advantage in efficiency and cost management, while simultaneously creating an external revenue stream that monetises that intellectual property at scale.

6.4 Brand Architecture Clarity

The umbrella brand structure allows Conner Law Group to present a cohesive, authoritative identity while each sub-brand maintains focused market positioning. Clients are not confused by a generalist firm claiming to do everything.

6.5 Brisbane-First, Queensland-Wide

A deliberate focus on Brisbane and South East Queensland means deep local knowledge, established court relationships, and trusted practitioner networks — advantages that national firms struggle to replicate at the local level.

6.6 Accessible Pricing Without Compromising Quality

Conner Injury Law's no-win, no-fee contingency model removes financial barriers for injured claimants. Merkel & Conner's SME-aligned pricing ensures quality legal services are within reach of growing businesses.

6.7 Founder-Led, Technology-Augmented Practice

Ben Alek Conner's background spanning legal practice and technology entrepreneurship gives the group an unusually broad strategic perspective — able to build both the legal practice and the technology layer simultaneously.

8. PRICING & FEE STRUCTURE

8.1 Conner Injury Law — No Win, No Fee

Personal injury matters are handled on a conditional costs agreement (no win, no fee) in compliance with the Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld) and the Legal Profession Regulation 2017 (Qld). Success fees and uplift charges are disclosed transparently at engagement and comply with statutory maximum uplift provisions. Standard contingency fee: 25% of settlement for standard matters; 25–33% for complex or high-value matters, as agreed.

8.2 Merkel & Conner — Commercial Rates

Standard hourly rates: $300–$600/hour (excluding GST) depending on practitioner seniority. Fixed-fee packages for defined scope matters: company formations ($2,500), employment contract suites ($1,800), standard commercial agreements ($850–$2,000), lease reviews ($950–$1,800). Monthly retainer arrangements available for clients requiring ongoing advisory support: $1,500–$3,500/month.

8.3 MBCS — Per-Service Pricing

Transparent per-service fee schedule provided to all client firms upon engagement. Volume discounts available. Representative pricing: medical chronology ($450–$850), IME coordination ($500–$1,200), expert witness brief preparation ($800–$2,500), file preparation bundles ($350–$900).

11. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY

Technology is central to the Conner Law Group competitive model. The Group's technology strategy encompasses:

Internal Operations: KevLaw AI is deployed internally across all legal business units, providing document automation, client intake management, compliance tracking, and file management. This delivers measurable efficiency gains and reduces per-matter administrative cost.

KevLaw AI Product Roadmap:

  • Phase 1 (Q2–Q3 2026): Core document engine for internal use — retainer agreements, letters of demand, settlement correspondence, client update letters
  • Phase 2 (Q4 2026): Invite-only beta with 10–20 Queensland law firms; collect feedback and refine
  • Phase 3 (Q1–Q2 2027): Public launch with formal pricing tiers; national marketing push
  • Phase 4 (Q3–Q4 2027): Integration with LEAP, Actionstep, and Smokeball; expand jurisdiction coverage
  • Phase 5 (2028+): National platform; explore institutional partnerships; potential external funding

Data Security & Compliance: All systems comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and Australian Privacy Principles. Client data is stored on Australian servers. Legal professional privilege protections are built into system architecture. Annual cybersecurity audit.

13. OPERATIONS

Office: Brisbane-based primary office in CBD or fringe CBD location. Professional presentation is essential given the client-facing nature of personal injury and business law practice.

Hours: Standard business hours Monday to Friday. After-hours appointment availability for personal injury clients as needed.

File Management: All matters managed within KevLaw AI's practice management integration layer, ensuring consistent file standards, deadline tracking, and document management across all units.

Professional Indemnity Insurance: Maintained at appropriate coverage levels across all legal entities, reviewed annually.

Trust Accounting: Managed in strict compliance with the Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld) and Queensland Law Society trust accounting requirements.

Client Communication Standards: All client matters have a designated responsible practitioner. Clients receive substantive updates at a minimum every 30 days. Missed calls returned within one business day.
