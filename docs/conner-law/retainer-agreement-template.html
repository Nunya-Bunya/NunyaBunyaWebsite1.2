It must be reviewed and adapted by a Queensland-admitted solicitor for each specific matter before execution. The costs disclosure requirements under ss.308–310 of the Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld) must be satisfied. Practitioners must verify currency of all legislative references before use. This document does not constitute legal advice and does not create a solicitor-client relationship with any person other than the named client.

PART 1 — NATURE AND EFFECT OF THIS AGREEMENT

1.1 This is a Conditional Costs Agreement within the meaning of Part 3.4 of the Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld). It governs the terms on which the Firm will provide legal services to the Client in relation to the above matter.

1.2 The effect of this Agreement is:

(a) If the Client receives a favourable outcome (as defined in clause 4.3), the Client will pay the Firm's professional fees and disbursements from the money or benefit received, as calculated under clause 4.

(b) If the Client does not receive a favourable outcome, the Client will not be required to pay the Firm's professional fees. The Client's liability for disbursements in an unsuccessful matter is set out in clause 5.3.

1.3 The Client has the right to seek independent legal advice about this Agreement before signing it. The Firm encourages the Client to ask any questions before signing.

1.4 The Client may negotiate the terms of this Agreement, including the fee percentage, before signing.

1.5 This Agreement supersedes and replaces any prior costs agreement, engagement letter, quotation, or verbal costs arrangement between the Client and the Firm in relation to this matter.

1.6 This Agreement does not cover services in relation to any matter other than the matter described above.

PART 2 — SCOPE OF REPRESENTATION

2.1 The Firm will represent the Client in the following services in relation to the matter described above:

(a) Investigating and assessing the merits and likely value of the Client's personal injury claim, including reviewing all available evidence;

(b) Obtaining evidence in support of the claim, including medical records, clinical notes, imaging reports, employment records, witness statements, and expert opinions;

(c) Advising the Client about the nature, merits, risks, and likely value of the claim on an ongoing basis;

(d) Preparing and lodging all required notices, statutory claim forms, and pre-litigation correspondence;

(e) Negotiating with the insurer, employer, occupier, or other at-fault party on the Client's behalf;

(f) Representing the Client at any compulsory conference, mediation, settlement conference, or dispute resolution process required by the applicable scheme;

(g) If no reasonable settlement is achieved, commencing and conducting litigation in the District Court or Supreme Court of Queensland as appropriate;

(h) Briefing and instructing a barrister as required;

(i) Reporting regularly to the Client and providing frank and honest advice about all material developments.

2.2 This Agreement does NOT include, and the Firm is not retained to provide services in relation to:

(a) Any appeal from a court judgment, tribunal decision, or assessment outcome (a separate costs agreement will be required);

(b) Any criminal charges or traffic infringement notices arising from the same incident;

(c) Any dispute about workers' compensation benefits beyond those identified in the scope above;

(d) Any matter in a jurisdiction other than Queensland;

(e) Any matter not expressly described in clause 2.1.

2.3 If the scope of the Firm's work needs to change materially, the Firm will notify the Client in writing. Material changes to scope may require a written variation to this Agreement signed by both parties.

PART 3 — CLIENT OBLIGATIONS

3.1 The Client agrees to:

(a) Provide the Firm with complete, honest, and timely instructions and information relevant to the matter — including all facts known to the Client that may assist or adversely affect the claim;

(b) Promptly notify the Firm of any change in the Client's contact details, residential address, health, employment status, or any development in the matter;

(c) Attend all medical examinations, independent medical examinations, compulsory conferences, mediations, hearings, and other appointments as required by the Firm with reasonable notice;

(d) Not communicate directly with any insurer, employer, opponent, or third party about the matter without first seeking the Firm's advice;

(e) Not make any admissions, sign any documents, or accept any payments in relation to the matter without the Firm's prior written advice;

(f) Respond to the Firm's requests for instructions within a reasonable time — generally within five (5) business days unless a shorter timeframe is required due to a deadline;

(g) Follow the Firm's reasonable legal advice about the conduct of the matter, including advice about settlement;

(h) Pay disbursements as required under this Agreement;

(i) Not engage any other law firm to act in this matter without first terminating this Agreement in accordance with clause 8.

3.2 The Firm's ability to act effectively, and the prospects of a successful outcome, depend significantly on the Client's cooperation and candid disclosure of all relevant facts.

3.3 The Client acknowledges that withholding relevant information, providing false or misleading instructions, or failing to cooperate with the Firm may prejudice the matter and may constitute grounds for termination of this Agreement.

4.3 Definition of Favourable Outcome

For the purposes of this Agreement, "favourable outcome" means any of the following:

(a) The settlement of the Client's claim for any monetary amount (including settlement at compulsory conference, mediation, or by agreement at any stage);

(b) A judgment or award in the Client's favour for any monetary amount;

(c) Any result that entitles the Client to receive money or a benefit with monetary value.

4.5 GST

GST (currently 10%) is payable on the Firm's professional fees and on GST-applicable disbursements. All fee amounts in this Agreement are exclusive of GST unless expressly stated otherwise. The Firm will provide the Client with a tax invoice at the conclusion of the matter.

4.6 Costs Recovered from the Other Party

If the Firm obtains an order for the other party to contribute to the Client's legal costs ("party-party costs"), those costs will be applied first to reduce the disbursements payable by the Client. Any surplus party-party costs will be applied to reduce the professional fee payable by the Client under clause 4.1. The Client will not receive party-party costs as additional income — they reduce the Client's overall costs liability.

5.4 Adverse Costs Orders

If the matter proceeds to litigation in a Queensland court and the Client receives an adverse costs order (is ordered to pay the opposing party's legal costs), the Client is personally responsible for those costs. The quantum of an adverse costs order can be substantial. The Firm will advise the Client about this risk before any litigation is commenced, and will obtain the Client's informed instructions before filing any court process.

PART 6 — FIRM'S OBLIGATIONS

6.1 The Firm agrees to:

(a) Act in the Client's best interests at all times, in accordance with the ethical and professional obligations of Queensland-admitted solicitors, including the Australian Solicitors' Conduct Rules as adopted in Queensland;

(b) Conduct the Client's matter competently and with reasonable diligence and efficiency;

(c) Provide the Client with frank, honest advice about the merits, value, and risks of the claim — including advice the Client may not want to hear;

(d) Maintain the Client's confidentiality, consistent with legal professional privilege and professional obligations under Queensland law;

(e) Communicate with the Client regularly — providing a substantive update on the matter at least every 30 days, and within 2 business days of any material development (including receipt of a settlement offer, service of court documents, or any significant change in the Firm's assessment of the claim);

(f) Return the Client's calls or messages within 1 business day;

(g) Obtain the Client's informed instructions before accepting or rejecting any settlement offer, filing any court documents, or taking any action that materially affects the Client's position;

(h) Keep accurate file notes of all client communications and matter developments;

(i) Hold all client money in the Firm's statutory trust account in strict compliance with the Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld) and Queensland Law Society trust accounting requirements;

(j) Account to the Client in full upon settlement or conclusion of the matter, providing a clear itemised statement of receipts and disbursements;

(k) Not settle the matter without the Client's express informed instructions.

PART 8 — TERMINATION OF THIS AGREEMENT

8.1 Termination by the Client

The Client may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing written notice to the Firm. Upon termination by the Client:

(a) If a Settlement Amount has been received at the time of termination, the Firm is entitled to its professional fee and disbursements as calculated under clauses 4 and 5 of this Agreement.

(b) If no Settlement Amount has been received at the time of termination, the Firm may at its election claim reasonable remuneration for work performed to the date of termination, calculated on a time-cost basis at the Firm's standard hourly rates, subject to the provisions of the Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld) and any applicable caps. The Firm will advise the Client promptly of any such claim.

(c) The Client will be responsible for disbursements incurred to the date of termination, consistent with the option selected in clause 5.3.

(d) The Firm will promptly provide the Client with a copy of the file and all documents to facilitate transfer to a new solicitor.

8.2 Termination by the Firm

The Firm may terminate this Agreement by written notice to the Client in the following circumstances:

(a) The Client fails to provide instructions or respond to communications within a reasonable time, causing actual or potential prejudice to the matter;

(b) The Client has provided false, misleading, or materially incomplete information to the Firm;

(c) The Client has acted inconsistently with the Firm's advice or has taken steps in the matter without the Firm's knowledge;

(d) A conflict of interest arises that cannot be resolved;

(e) The Firm's assessment is that the matter no longer has reasonable prospects of success, after notifying the Client of this view in writing;

(f) The Client fails to comply with a material obligation under this Agreement;

(g) The Client's conduct toward the Firm or its staff is threatening, abusive, or otherwise unreasonable.

The Firm will give reasonable notice before terminating, and will assist with an orderly transition to a new solicitor where practicable.

8.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination by either party:

(a) The Firm will provide the Client with copies of all key documents on the file;

(b) The Firm will account for all funds held in trust on behalf of the Client;

(c) The Firm will assist with transition to a new solicitor where this is in the Client's interests.

PART 9 — CONFIDENTIALITY

9.1 The Firm will maintain strict confidentiality of all information provided by the Client in connection with this matter, in accordance with legal professional privilege and the professional obligations of Queensland-admitted solicitors.

9.2 The Firm will not disclose the Client's confidential information to any third party without the Client's consent, except:

(a) As required to progress the matter (e.g., to medical experts, barristers, courts, insurers on a controlled basis);

(b) As required by law or by an order of a court of competent jurisdiction;

(c) As required by the Queensland Law Society or Legal Services Commissioner.

9.3 The Client's information is handled in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles. The Firm's Privacy Policy is available on request.

PART 10 — FILE RETENTION

10.1 Upon conclusion of the matter, the Firm will retain the Client's file for a minimum period of seven (7) years from the date of closure.

10.2 After seven years, the file may be destroyed without further notice unless the Client has instructed otherwise in writing.

10.3 The Client may request copies of documents from the file during the retention period; the Firm may charge a reasonable fee for copying and retrieval.

12.1 Governing Law: This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Queensland, Australia. Any dispute arising from this Agreement is subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Queensland.

12.2 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Firm and the Client regarding costs and the scope of representation for the matter described, and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, or understandings.

12.3 Variation: This Agreement may only be varied by a written document signed by both parties.

12.4 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid, void, or unenforceable, that provision is to be severed and the remaining provisions of the Agreement continue in full force and effect.
