1. BRAND ARCHITECTURE & IDENTITY HIERARCHY

Conner Law Group operates as the umbrella brand. Four sub-brands sit beneath it, each with distinct positioning but sharing the group's visual and verbal foundation.

CONNER LAW GROUP  (Umbrella — Primary Brand)
├── Merkel & Conner         (Commercial & Business Law)
├── Conner Injury Law       (Personal Injury Litigation)
├── MBCS                    (Medical-Legal Consulting & Support)
└── KevLaw AI               (Legal Technology SaaS)

Hierarchy rules:

  • The Conner Law Group umbrella mark appears on all group-level materials: corporate website, investor presentations, group email footers, group social media channels, and cross-brand printed collateral.
  • Each sub-brand operates independently in its own market-facing communications. Sub-brand materials are designed to serve their specific audience — not to promote the group.
  • The phrase "Part of the Conner Law Group" (in Open Sans Light, small point size, Justice Blue) may appear on sub-brand letterhead, email footers, business cards, and website footers. It is not mandatory but is encouraged.
  • KevLaw AI has the most visual flexibility given its technology context, but must remain within the defined group colour palette.
  • No sub-brand may use another sub-brand's logo, colour emphasis treatment, or brand voice on its materials.

Brand Philosophy

Conner Law Group operates at the intersection of authority and approachability. Every brand expression — visual, verbal, or experiential — must answer yes to three questions:

  1. Does this communicate trust?
  2. Does this communicate expertise?
  3. Does this feel distinctly Conner Law Group — not generic legal?

If any answer is no, revise before publishing.

Colour Usage Rules

Law Black (#1A1A1A)

  • Primary text colour on white or Warm Grey backgrounds
  • Full-bleed backgrounds for premium sections (hero panels, quote blocks, cover pages)
  • Do not use as text on Dark Blue or Justice Blue backgrounds — contrast is insufficient
  • Never use pure black (#000000) in brand materials — it reads as harsh and unrefined

Dark Gold (#B8860B)

  • Reserve for maximum impact — no more than 10-15% of any single design
  • Suitable for: primary CTA buttons, underline accents on headings, border rules, key statistics, icon colour, blockquote left borders
  • Not suitable as a background for body text — contrast too low for readability
  • Light Gold (#D4A843) used for hover states only — never as a standalone accent in print

Justice Blue (#1E4D7B)

  • Secondary and H3-level headings, pull-quote text
  • Hyperlink colour in digital documents and web content
  • Background for mid-tier banner sections (with White text)
  • Not suitable on Law Black backgrounds — insufficient contrast

White (#FFFFFF)

  • Primary background for all body content and documents
  • Reversed text on Law Black, Deep Blue, or Justice Blue backgrounds
  • Never used as text on gold or yellow backgrounds

4. TYPOGRAPHY

Primary Typefaces

Heading Font: Georgia (Serif)

Georgia is the mandated brand heading font. Its classical serif form communicates legal authority, tradition, and trust — the appropriate foundation for a legal brand of this calibre.

  • Web alternative if Georgia unavailable: Playfair Display or EB Garamond (both available via Google Fonts)
  • Do not substitute with Times New Roman for brand materials (Times New Roman is reserved for court documents only)

Body Font: Open Sans (Sans-Serif)

Open Sans provides excellent legibility at small sizes, a sense of modernity and approachability, and optimal contrast when paired with Georgia headings.

  • Web source: Google Fonts (fonts.google.com/specimen/Open+Sans)
  • Web alternative if Open Sans unavailable: Lato or Inter

Accent / Label Font: Montserrat (Sans-Serif)

Montserrat is used for all-caps labels, button text, navigation items, and KevLaw AI brand materials where a more technology-forward, geometric feel is required.

  • Use sparingly — one additional typeface beyond the Georgia/Open Sans pairing
  • Never use Montserrat for body copy or extended reading text

Court Document Font: Times New Roman

Times New Roman 12pt is used for formal legal documents submitted to Queensland courts, consistent with court formatting requirements. This applies to court filings and formal letters of demand only — not to marketing materials or brand collateral.

Typography Rules

Do:

  • Use sentence case for all headings in brand materials (not ALL CAPS, except Montserrat labels)
  • Maintain generous line spacing — 1.6 minimum for body text; 1.4 for tight document layouts
  • Left-align body text; centre headings only when used in standalone hero or section-divider contexts
  • Bold defined terms in legal documents on first use
  • Use heading hierarchy in strict sequence — never skip from H1 to H3

Do not:

  • Mix more than two typeface families in any single document or design
  • Use italic for extended body text — italic is for emphasis and pull quotes only
  • Use font sizes below 9pt in client-facing materials
  • Apply faux bold or faux italic — use the actual font weight file
  • Use decorative or script fonts under any circumstances

Clear Space

Maintain a clear space equal to the cap-height of the firm name's first letter on all four sides of the logo. No typography, imagery, graphic element, or border may appear within this clear space.

Minimum Sizes

  • Print: 20mm wide (icon mark only below 20mm)
  • Digital: 80px wide (icon mark only below 80px)
  • Favicon: Icon/lettermark version only

Approved Background Placements

  • Full colour logo: White (#FFFFFF) or Warm Grey (#F5F5F0) only
  • Reversed (white) logo: Law Black (#1A1A1A), Justice Blue (#1E4D7B), or Deep Blue (#0F2E4A)
  • Never place any logo version over a photographic background without a solid-colour container beneath it

Prohibited Logo Treatments

  • Do not rotate, skew, stretch, distort, or crop the logo in any dimension
  • Do not change any colour within the logo outside of approved variants
  • Do not add drop shadows, glows, emboss effects, outlines, or gradients
  • Do not recreate the logo in any typeface other than the approved master file
  • Do not use a logo from one sub-brand on another sub-brand's materials
  • Do not use the Conner Law Group umbrella mark on sub-brand materials except as the approved "Part of the Conner Law Group" text treatment
  • Do not use rasterised (low-resolution) logo files for print — use vector (SVG or EPS) for all print applications

6. PHOTOGRAPHY & VISUAL STYLE

Photography Style — Subject Matter

Approved:

  • Authentic professional portraits of solicitors and staff in natural or professional office environments
  • Brisbane cityscape and identifiable Queensland locations — grounding the brand in its home
  • Client-side imagery: diverse individuals in real-life contexts (not exaggerated injury poses or theatrical distress)
  • Professional workspace imagery: clean desks, legal books, natural light through windows, document review
  • Courthouse exteriors (Queensland courts); legal proceedings in session (tastefully staged or licensed)
  • MBCS: clinical but professional — medical-adjacent imagery conveying expertise, not cold sterility
  • KevLaw AI: screens, interfaces, modern workplace environments, technology in use

Prohibited:

  • Generic handshake stock photography
  • US-specific legal iconography (US Supreme Court, US-style courtrooms)
  • Gavel-and-scales compositions as the primary or dominant image (minor supporting use acceptable)
  • People in obvious pain or distress, grimacing, or in staged injury poses
  • Overly posed "pointing at document" or "smiling at laptop" corporate stock cliches
  • Images without appropriate licensing or client consent (for client-adjacent imagery)

Photography Technical Treatment

Colour Grade: Slightly warm, high-contrast grade — rich deep shadows, strong midtones, no cool or blue cast. This aligns visually with the Law Black and Dark Gold palette and communicates authority.

Composition: Subject positioned with clear breathing room for text overlay. Portrait orientation for team headshots (4:5 ratio, subject centred with natural negative space above). Landscape for hero panels (16:9 minimum, subject in left or right third).

Team Photography Standards:

  • All portraits: consistent lighting (natural window light preferred; professional flash acceptable for consistency), neutral background (light grey or white wall)
  • Business attire: solicitors in suits or professional dress; support staff in smart professional attire
  • Expression: confident and approachable — direct eye contact; no severe or stiff expressions
  • No casual or selfie-style photography for professional use

Graphic Elements

Rule lines: Dark Gold (#B8860B) 1-2pt horizontal rules used to separate major document or design sections.

Icons: Line-style icons, consistent 2px stroke weight, single-colour in brand palette. Recommended libraries: Heroicons, Phosphor Icons (open source).

Quote blocks: Large opening quotation mark in Dark Gold, italic Georgia for quote text, solicitor name in Open Sans Regular below.

Data / statistics: Large Georgia Bold numerals, Open Sans Regular label beneath, Dark Gold underline accent.

Patterns / textures: Subtle grain or noise texture overlays permitted on dark (#1A1A1A) backgrounds only — never on white.

7. TONE OF VOICE — GROUP & PER BRAND TIER

Conner Law Group (Umbrella)

Tone: Professional, authoritative, integrated. This voice is used for group-level communications — investor materials, corporate website, industry presence, media statements, and cross-brand content.

  • Complete sentences, formal but not stiff
  • Refers to the group by name ("Conner Law Group") rather than "we" in most contexts
  • Quantifies where possible: "four specialist business units," "multi-million AUD revenue target"
  • Does not use superlatives without substantiation
  • Confident without being boastful

Example: "Conner Law Group is a Brisbane-based legal services group combining specialist legal practice with proprietary legal technology — built to deliver better outcomes for clients and a more efficient model for the profession."

Merkel & Conner (Commercial & Business Law)

Tone: Commercially-minded, pragmatic, direct. Speaks to Queensland business owners and founders who are intelligent, time-poor, and do not want to be buried in legal theory.

  • Frames advice in terms of business outcomes, not legal process
  • Plain English for concepts; correct legal terminology when specificity matters
  • Peer register — advises, does not lecture
  • References real business risk and real business consequences
  • Serious but not intimidating

Example: "If your shareholder agreement doesn't address what happens when a founder wants to exit, you are one difficult conversation away from an expensive dispute. Let's fix that now."

Conner Injury Law (Personal Injury)

Tone: Empathetic, plain-spoken, confident. Speaks directly to injured individuals who are often stressed, confused, and unfamiliar with legal processes.

  • Uses "you" and "we" — direct, human address
  • Eliminates all legal jargon; explains every step in plain language
  • Acknowledges the emotional reality of injury — not clinical, not cold
  • Confident but never aggressive: "you may be entitled to significant compensation" not "we'll get you millions"
  • Short sentences, short paragraphs, clear headers

Example: "You've been through enough. You shouldn't have to fight through confusing paperwork and legal jargon on top of everything else. We take care of the legal side, in plain English, every step of the way."

MBCS (Medical-Legal Consulting & Support)

Tone: Technical, efficient, professional. Speaks to law firm principals, solicitors, and practice managers. Assumes professional knowledge. Respects the reader's time.

  • Uses correct professional terminology without defining the basics
  • Leads with value and operational efficiency
  • Quantifies benefits: turnaround times, files managed, resource savings
  • B2B register — transactional, clear, and outcome-focused
  • No warmth-for-warmth's-sake; results-oriented

Example: "MBCS coordinates your IMEs, medical chronologies, and expert briefs from instruction to delivery — so your fee-earners focus on legal work, not administration."

KevLaw AI (Legal Technology SaaS)

Tone: Forward-thinking, practical, understated. Speaks to innovative law firm principals and practice managers who are receptive to technology but require proof, not hype.

  • Leads with the problem the product solves — not the product itself
  • Uses specifics: "first draft in under sixty seconds," "reduces document drafting time by 70%"
  • Never uses tech hype language: no "revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing," "cutting-edge"
  • Invites healthy scepticism — then answers it
  • The product is intelligent and serious; so is the voice

Example: "Most law firm document drafting follows predictable formats. KevLaw AI handles the first draft so your solicitors can focus on the judgement that only they can provide."

Language Examples (All Firms)

Do sound like this:

  • "Here's what happened to other clients in your situation, and how we helped them."
  • "This is a strong case. Here's why, and here's what we'll do to maximize your settlement."
  • "We fight for clients. That's what we do."

Do not sound like this:

  • "We shall endeavor to obtain equitable remuneration through litigation proceedings."
  • "Our legal prowess is unparalleled in Queensland."

8. WRITING STYLE & LANGUAGE RULES

Australian English — Mandatory

All Conner Law Group materials are written in Australian English throughout. This is non-negotiable.

  • Colour / colour, organisation / organisation, realise / realise, licence / licence (noun), defense / defence, judgement (general use)
  • Use "solicitor" not "attorney" or "lawyer" in formal contexts
  • Use "proceedings" or "claim" not "lawsuit"
  • Use "barrister" not "trial lawyer" or "litigator" in formal contexts

Grammar & Punctuation

  • Oxford comma: used throughout all group materials ("Merkel & Conner, Conner Injury Law, MBCS, and KevLaw AI")
  • Single quotation marks for primary quoted material in marketing copy; double quotation marks for nested quotes
  • Dates: Day Month Year — "7 March 2026" (not "March 7, 2026" or "7/3/26" in formal copy)
  • Currency: $ for domestic-facing content; AUD or A$ for international-facing content
  • Numbers: write out one to nine; numerals for 10 and above; always numerals for dollar amounts, percentages, dates, and legal timelines
  • Acronyms: spell out on first use with acronym in parentheses — "Motor Accident Insurance Commission (MAIC)" — then use acronym subsequently

Legal References

  • Italicise legislation titles: Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld)
  • Italicise case names: Smith v Jones [2022] QSC 44
  • Use correct jurisdiction citation: (Qld), (Cth), (NSW) as applicable
  • Do not cite legislation without verifying currency — always check current version

Required Disclaimers

All client-facing marketing materials must carry appropriate disclaimers:

Personal injury materials: "This information is general in nature and does not constitute legal advice. Past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Contact Conner Injury Law for advice specific to your circumstances."

Commercial law materials: "The information on this page is general in nature. It does not constitute legal advice and should not be relied upon as such. Please contact Merkel & Conner for advice tailored to your situation."

Testimonial content: "Client testimonials reflect individual experiences and outcomes. Results will vary depending on the specific circumstances of each matter."

KevLaw AI: "KevLaw AI generates first-draft documents for review and approval by a qualified legal practitioner. Outputs do not constitute legal advice. Practitioners retain full professional responsibility for all documents issued."

9. DO'S AND DON'TS

DO

  • Use consistent brand colours for each sub-brand across all materials
  • Use authentic team photography — not generic stock photography
  • Write in Australian English throughout all materials
  • Include appropriate legal disclaimers on all client-facing and marketing materials
  • Use Georgia for headings and Open Sans for body copy on all brand materials
  • Maintain clear space around all logos at all times
  • Quantify claims where possible — specificity builds trust
  • Apply the correct firm voice when producing materials for each sub-brand
  • Review all practice area content for legal accuracy before publication
  • Respond to all Google and social media reviews professionally and promptly
  • Focus on client results and outcomes in all messaging
  • Feature client stories and case results where appropriate
  • Build trust through transparency and expertise

DON'T

  • Introduce non-approved colours (no pastels, no bright red, no orange, no green)
  • Use American spellings or legal terminology ("attorney," "lawsuit," "judgment" in general writing)
  • Make guarantees about outcomes ("we will win your case")
  • Use exaggerated or unverifiable settlement claims in advertising
  • Place one sub-brand's visual identity on another sub-brand's materials
  • Use pixelated, low-resolution, or non-brand-approved imagery
  • Overuse Dark Gold — it loses impact immediately when it appears too frequently
  • Use font sizes below the minimums specified in this guide
  • Publish content without a practitioner accuracy review for any legal practice area content
  • Use any emoji in formal brand materials, letterhead, legal correspondence, or professional social media posts
  • Mislead about fees or costs
  • Use overly casual or playful tone

10. DIGITAL SPECIFICATIONS

Web Design

Layout:

  • Desktop content max-width: 1200px (viewport 1440px)
  • Tablet breakpoint: 768-1024px
  • Mobile breakpoint: 320-767px
  • Standard section padding: 80px vertical (desktop), 48px (mobile)

Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA required):

  • Body text: minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
  • Large text (18pt+ or 14pt+ bold): minimum 3:1 contrast ratio
  • All interactive elements must have visible focus state using brand colours
  • All images must have descriptive alt text

Button Styles:

  • Primary CTA: Dark Gold (#B8860B) background, White (#FFFFFF) text, Montserrat SemiBold, 14px, 6px border radius
  • Secondary CTA: Justice Blue (#1E4D7B) background, White text, Montserrat SemiBold, 14px
  • Ghost / outline: 1.5px border in Law Black (#1A1A1A), Law Black text, transparent background

Digital Image Sizes:

  • Hero images: 1920 x 800px minimum, WebP format preferred
  • Team portraits: 600 x 750px (4:5 ratio)
  • Blog / article featured images: 1200 x 630px
  • Social sharing images (OG): 1200 x 630px

File Formats:

  • Logos: SVG for all web use; PNG at 2x resolution as fallback
  • Photography: WebP primary, JPEG fallback; maximum 200KB per image after compression
  • Print-ready files: PDF/X-1a or PDF/X-4

Email

  • Width: 600px maximum
  • Header block: Law Black (#1A1A1A) background, White reversed logo, full-width
  • Body background: White (#FFFFFF)
  • Body text: Open Sans 14-16px, #1A1A1A
  • Primary CTA button: Dark Gold (#B8860B) background, White text, Montserrat SemiBold
  • Footer: Warm Grey (#F5F5F0) background, Open Sans 11px, #767676; include unsubscribe link, physical address, legal disclaimer, ABN

13. DOCUMENT & CORRESPONDENCE FORMATTING

Client Letters

  • Salutation: "Dear [Client First Name]," — never "Dear Sir/Madam" unless identity is genuinely unknown
  • Structure: Purpose of letter — Key information — Required action — Contact details — Closing
  • Closing: "Yours faithfully" (unknown or fully formal); "Yours sincerely" (named client)
  • Signature block: Full name | Qualification (LLB, LLM) | Firm name | Direct phone | Email

Legal Documents (Retainers, Agreements, Court Documents)

  • Clause numbering: 1. / 1.1 / 1.1.1 format throughout
  • Defined terms: Bold on first use; capitalised throughout the document
  • Signature blocks: Two-column layout, ample pen space; print name below signature line
  • Document footer: "This document is confidential. [Firm Name] | Legal Profession Act 2007 (Qld)"

Internal Documents & File Notes

  • All internal documents: Law Black text on White background
  • File headings: Georgia Bold, matter name and file number
  • Section headings: Open Sans SemiBold
  • Date of note and author name at top of every file note
  • Do not use Microsoft Word default "Normal" style — apply brand-specified font and size

14. KEVLAW AI PRODUCT BRANDING

What is KevLaw AI?

An AI-powered legal research and document drafting tool designed specifically for law firms (initially for Conner Law Group and partners).

Positioning

"KevLaw AI accelerates legal research and document preparation while maintaining the strategic judgment only human lawyers can provide. It's not a replacement for solicitors — it's a force multiplier."

Key Features

  • Demand letter generation (auto-draft based on case details)
  • Legal research aggregation (pulls relevant case law automatically)
  • Contract template library (customisable, client-specific)
  • Case timeline and document management
  • Billing integration

Brand Voice for KevLaw

  • Tone: Professional, practical, empowering
  • Audience: Law firms, legal departments, solo practitioners
  • Value: Saves time, reduces errors, increases profit margins
  • Positioning: "Your AI-powered legal assistant"
  • Tagline: "AI-Powered Legal Work. Human-Quality Results."

KevLaw Visual Identity

  • Distinct from law firm logos (but complementary colours)
  • Should feel modern (AI) but professional (law)
  • Social presence: LinkedIn (professional, thought leadership), blog ("KevLaw Insights" — legal tech, productivity, industry trends)
