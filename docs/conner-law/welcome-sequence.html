Email 2: Day 3 — Understanding Your Rights After an Accident

Subject: What you're entitled to after a car accident in WA

Preview text: Your rights under Washington law — explained in plain English

Hi {{ contact.firstname | default("there") }},

A few days ago, you downloaded our car accident checklist. I hope it's been helpful.

Today I want to cover something most people don't fully understand until it's too late: your rights after a car accident in Washington State.

Here's what the law says:

You can seek compensation for more than just your car.

If another driver caused your accident, you may be entitled to recover:

  • Medical bills — past, current, and future treatment
  • Lost wages — including time off work for doctor visits
  • Pain and suffering — the impact on your daily life
  • Future care costs — rehabilitation, ongoing therapy, or long-term treatment

You have three years to file a claim.

Washington's statute of limitations gives you three years from the date of the accident. That sounds like plenty of time, but evidence disappears, memories fade, and witnesses move away. Starting early gives you the strongest position.

Even if you were partially at fault, you may still have a claim.

Washington uses a "pure comparative fault" system. If you were 20% at fault, you can still recover 80% of your damages. Insurance companies will try to shift blame onto you to reduce what they owe. Don't let them.

You don't have to accept the first offer.

Insurance adjusters often make a quick, low offer hoping you'll take it before you understand the full cost of your injuries. You are never obligated to accept. You have the right to negotiate — or to have an attorney negotiate for you.

If any of this applies to your situation and you'd like to talk through it, our free case assessment is exactly for that. No cost, no obligation.

Email 3: Day 7 — 5 Mistakes People Make After a Car Accident

Subject: 5 mistakes that cost accident victims thousands

Preview text: Avoid these common errors that insurance companies count on

Hi {{ contact.firstname | default("there") }},

After handling hundreds of car accident cases across Washington State, I've seen the same mistakes come up again and again. These are the errors that insurance companies count on — because every one of them makes it easier to deny or reduce your claim.

Mistake 1: Not seeing a doctor right away.

You might feel fine after the accident. But soft tissue injuries, concussions, and whiplash often don't show symptoms for 24 to 72 hours. If there's a gap between your accident and your first medical visit, the insurance company will argue your injuries aren't from the crash.

Mistake 2: Giving a recorded statement to the other driver's insurer.

Their adjuster sounds friendly. They say it's "routine." But recorded statements are designed to get you to say something that undermines your claim. You are not legally required to give one. Politely decline.

Mistake 3: Posting about the accident on social media.

Insurance investigators check Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok. A photo of you at a birthday party, or a post saying "I'm doing better!" can be used to argue your injuries aren't serious — even if you're in constant pain.

Mistake 4: Accepting the first settlement offer.

The first offer is almost always a fraction of what your case is worth. Insurance companies make quick offers because they know most people need money and won't push back. Once you sign, you give up all future claims — even if your injuries get worse.

Mistake 5: Waiting too long to talk to an attorney.

Evidence degrades. Witnesses forget details. Medical records become harder to connect to the accident. The earlier you get legal guidance, the stronger your case.

None of these mistakes are your fault — insurance companies design the process to be confusing. That's why we offer free case assessments. No commitment, no pressure. Just clarity about where you stand.
