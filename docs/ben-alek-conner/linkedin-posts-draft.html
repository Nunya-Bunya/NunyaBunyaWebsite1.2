POST 1: The $16/Month Marketing Stack

I run my entire marketing stack for $16/month.

No, that's not a typo.

Here's the breakdown of what I replaced and what I use instead:

  • Email marketing (Mailchimp) -- Mautic (self-hosted, free)
  • Analytics (Google Analytics) -- Matomo (self-hosted, free)
  • CRM (HubSpot) -- Twenty CRM (self-hosted, free)
  • Dashboards (Databox) -- Metabase (self-hosted, free)
  • Automations (Zapier) -- n8n (self-hosted, free)
  • Booking (Calendly) -- Cal.com (self-hosted, free)
  • Link tracking (Bitly) -- Shlink (self-hosted, free)

Total cost: one Hetzner VPS. $16/month.

Every one of these tools is open-source. They do the same job as the paid versions. In some cases, they do it better because you own the data.

I'm not saying everyone should do this. It took time to set up, and you need to be comfortable with a terminal. But if you're a solopreneur spending $300-500/month on SaaS tools you barely use -- there's another way.

If you want to see the full breakdown, I put together a free one-pager that walks through the whole stack and how it connects. Link in comments.

POST 2: Starting a Business With No Budget

My first paying client came from a Facebook group and a $25 dog walk.

Not a funnel. Not a website. Not a brand identity workshop.

I literally posted "I walk dogs in inner north Brisbane" in a local community group, and someone said yes.

Here's what I've learned launching multiple businesses with almost no money:

What didn't matter:

  • A perfect logo
  • A fancy website
  • Business cards
  • The "right" CRM

What actually mattered:

  • Showing up where people already are
  • Knocking on doors (literally, in some cases)
  • Saying what you do in one sentence
  • Doing the work well enough that they tell someone else

I spent weeks building systems before I had a single customer. Classic mistake. The systems are important eventually, but they're not what gets you your first dollar.

Your first dollar comes from getting in front of one person and solving their problem.

Everything else is a distraction until that happens.

What's the scrappiest thing you did to get your first client? Genuinely curious.

POST 3: Why Small Businesses Overpay for Marketing

Most small businesses don't need a $5K/month marketing agency.

I say that as someone who runs a marketing agency.

Here's what a lot of agencies sell you:

  • A "strategy session" that's really a sales pitch
  • Social media management that's just Canva templates on a schedule
  • Monthly reports full of vanity metrics that don't connect to revenue
  • A 12-month contract before you've seen a single result

Here's what most small businesses actually need:

  • A website that loads fast and tells people what you do
  • A way to capture leads (even just a form and a spreadsheet)
  • Email follow-up so leads don't go cold
  • A booking system so people can actually hire you
  • Analytics that tell you what's working

That's it. That's the foundation. You can build all of that for a fraction of what most agencies charge for "brand awareness."
