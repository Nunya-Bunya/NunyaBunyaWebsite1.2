Ben Alek Conner — Personal Brand Business Plan

Media, Content & Education Business

Version 1.0 | March 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Ben Alek Conner is building a media and content business around a single, compelling thesis: that one person can build multiple profitable businesses, pursue creative ambitions in film, and document the entire journey in real time.

Based in Brisbane, Australia, Ben operates at the intersection of entrepreneurship, filmmaking, health transformation, and high-performance productivity. His personal brand is not a side project — it is a business in its own right, designed to generate meaningful revenue through YouTube, a podcast, digital courses, brand partnerships, speaking, and the eventual commercial release of his feature film.

This business plan outlines the strategy, financial targets, content infrastructure, and team structure required to grow the Ben Alek Conner brand from its current early stage into a $500K+ annual revenue media business within three years.

Core positioning: "Building multiple profitable businesses while creating films and sharing the journey."

Primary platform: YouTube (long-form video as the content engine)

Revenue model: Diversified — ads, sponsorships, courses, speaking, film, brand deals

Year 1 revenue target: $40,000–$60,000 AUD

Year 3 revenue target: $450,000–$600,000 AUD

1. BUSINESS OVERVIEW

1.1 The Opportunity

Australian creators are underrepresented in the business/entrepreneurship content space globally. There is a clear market gap for an authentic, multi-dimensional Australian entrepreneur who builds in public — not just talking about business theory, but actively running agencies, law firms, and film productions while sharing the real numbers, real failures, and real wins.

Ben's competitive advantage is not just his story — it is the fact that his story is still being written, and audiences can follow along in real time.

1.2 Brand Pillars

Health, Fitness & TransformationWeight loss journey, marathon training, smoke-freePeople on their own health journeys

1.3 Unique Selling Proposition

Ben does not create content from behind a whiteboard. He creates it from the field — from actual client calls, actual agency operations, actual film sets, actual race training. This documentary-style authenticity is rare and highly engaging. Audiences follow Ben because he makes them feel less alone in the complexity of pursuing multiple ambitious goals simultaneously.

2. CONTENT STRATEGY BY PLATFORM

2.1 YouTube (Primary Platform)

Format: Long-form videos (10–20 minutes), vlog-style documentary content, business breakdowns

Upload frequency: 2x per week (one business/entrepreneurship video, one lifestyle/transformation/filmmaking video)

Content types:

  • "Build with me" series: documenting Nunya Bunya's growth to $35K MRR
  • "Making the film" series: behind-the-scenes of the feature film production
  • Business breakdowns: deep dives into digital marketing, agency operations, client acquisition
  • Monthly income reports: transparent revenue updates across all businesses
  • Transformation updates: weight loss, marathon training, smoke-free milestones
  • Productivity systems: ADHD management tools, daily routines, content workflows

SEO strategy: Target keywords with strong Australian search intent plus global business/entrepreneurship terms. Focus on titles like "How I built a $35K/month agency in Brisbane," "Running a business with ADHD," "Making a feature film while running 3 companies."

Year 1 subscriber targets: 5,000–10,000

Year 2 subscriber targets: 25,000–50,000

Monetization threshold: 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours (target Month 6–8)

2.2 Instagram

Format: Reels (60–90 seconds), carousel posts, Stories

Frequency: 5x per week (3 Reels, 2 carousels)

Content: Cut-downs from YouTube, quote graphics, behind-the-scenes stills, business milestones

Goal: Drive YouTube subscribers and email list sign-ups

2.3 LinkedIn

Format: Long-form text posts, short-form video, articles

Frequency: 3x per week

Content: Business insights, entrepreneurship lessons, agency growth updates

Goal: Attract speaking opportunities, brand partnerships, high-value consulting clients

2.4 TikTok (Secondary — Year 2)

Format: Short-form video (30–60 seconds)

Content: Business tips, transformation clips, film behind-the-scenes

Launch: Month 7 onwards, using repurposed YouTube content

2.5 Email Newsletter

Platform: ConvertKit (or Beehiiv)

Frequency: Weekly

Content: "The Build" — one lesson from the week across business, fitness, creativity, or systems

Goal: Build owned audience independent of algorithm

Year 1 target: 2,000 subscribers

Year 2 target: 8,000 subscribers

2.6 Podcast (Launching Early 2027)

See separate Podcast Launch Plan document for full strategy. The podcast will extend reach and create additional content for repurposing across all platforms.

3. REVENUE STREAMS

3.1 YouTube AdSense

Revenue generated from YouTube's Partner Program once monetization threshold is reached.

CPM assumptions (Australian creator): $8–$18 AUD CPM (business content attracts premium CPMs)

Month 8 estimate (10K subscribers): $500–$1,500/month

Month 18 estimate (30K subscribers): $2,500–$5,000/month

Month 30 estimate (60K subscribers): $6,000–$12,000/month

3.2 Brand Sponsorships & Deals

Brand deals will be the primary revenue driver in Years 1–2, before course revenue scales.

Target categories: Business software (accounting, CRM, project management), fitness/health products, productivity tools, Australian SMB services

Pricing model:

  • Dedicated YouTube integration (60-second mid-roll): $1,500–$4,000 AUD per video (scales with audience)
  • Instagram Reel: $500–$2,000 AUD
  • Newsletter mention: $300–$800 AUD
  • Podcast sponsorship (from 2027): $500–$2,000 AUD per episode

Year 1 target: 6–10 brand deals ($15,000–$30,000 AUD)

Year 2 target: 20–30 brand deals ($60,000–$120,000 AUD)

Year 3 target: 3–5 long-term partners ($150,000–$250,000 AUD)

3.3 Digital Courses & Products

Course 1 (Launch Month 10–12): "Agency Zero to $35K MRR"

A complete blueprint for building a digital marketing agency in Australia — from first client to full operational capacity. Priced at $497–$997 AUD.

Course 2 (Launch Year 2): "ADHD Entrepreneur's Productivity System"

Systems, tools, and routines for neurodiverse entrepreneurs who want to run multiple businesses without burning out. Priced at $297–$497 AUD.

Course 3 (Launch Year 2–3): "How to Make Your First Feature Film on a Small Budget"

Practical filmmaking for aspiring creators who don't have a studio behind them. Priced at $397–$797 AUD.

Digital downloads/templates: Content calendars, SOPs, business plan templates — $29–$97 each

Year 1 course revenue target: $20,000–$40,000 AUD (first course launch)

Year 2 course revenue target: $80,000–$150,000 AUD

Year 3 course revenue target: $200,000–$350,000 AUD

3.4 Coaching & Consulting

1:1 coaching: 6 spots per quarter at $2,500–$5,000 AUD per month (3-month minimum)

Group coaching program: "Build in Public" cohort — 12 students per cohort at $2,000–$3,000 AUD per cohort (quarterly)

Year 1 coaching revenue target: $30,000–$50,000 AUD

Year 2 coaching revenue target: $60,000–$100,000 AUD

3.5 Speaking

Topics: Entrepreneurship in your 20s/30s, building businesses with ADHD, digital marketing for SMBs, building a personal brand, the intersection of creativity and business

Target events: Business conferences, marketing conferences, university entrepreneurship programs, chamber of commerce events

Fee structure:

  • Year 1: $1,000–$3,000 AUD per engagement
  • Year 2: $3,000–$7,500 AUD per engagement
  • Year 3: $7,500–$15,000 AUD per engagement

Year 1 speaking target: 4–6 engagements ($8,000–$15,000 AUD)

Year 2 speaking target: 8–12 engagements ($30,000–$60,000 AUD)

3.6 Feature Film

Revenue from Ben's feature film currently in production. Expected release in Year 2–3.

Revenue channels: Film festival circuit, streaming platform licensing, direct digital distribution, VOD sales

Conservative projection: $20,000–$80,000 AUD (highly variable; upside scenario significantly higher)

3.7 Nunya Bunya Cross-Promotion

As the business content grows, Nunya Bunya benefits from direct audience conversion — clients who discover the agency through the personal brand. This channel represents additional value that flows to the agency but is not counted as personal brand revenue.

6. TEAM & CONTRACTOR NEEDS

6.1 Immediate Hires (Month 1–3)

Video Editor (Part-time, contractor)

  • Role: Edit raw YouTube footage into final videos, create Reels cut-downs
  • Rate: $35–$55/hour AUD or $500–$800/video
  • Hours: 15–20 hours/week
  • Skills required: Premiere Pro, After Effects, colour grading, captions

Thumbnail Designer (Freelance)

  • Role: Design YouTube thumbnails (2 per week) and social media graphics
  • Rate: $50–$100 per thumbnail
  • Hours: 4–6 hours/week

6.2 Month 3–6 Hires

Social Media Manager / Content Repurposer (Part-time)

  • Role: Take YouTube content, repurpose into Instagram Reels, LinkedIn posts, TikTok, schedule all posts
  • Rate: $25–$40/hour AUD
  • Hours: 10–15 hours/week

Podcast Producer (from Month 10–12, in preparation for 2027 launch)

  • Role: Audio editing, show notes, distribution
  • Rate: $200–$400 per episode
  • Hours: Contract per episode

6.3 Year 2 Additions

Operations/EA (Part-time)

  • Role: Inbox management, sponsorship outreach coordination, scheduling, course admin
  • Rate: $30–$45/hour AUD
  • Hours: 20 hours/week

Copywriter

  • Role: Email newsletter writing, course copy, website copy
  • Rate: $60–$100/hour or project-based
  • Hours: 8–12 hours/week

11. CONTENT PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

Weekly Rhythm

Monday: Batch B-roll and behind-the-scenes filming across business operations

Tuesday: Primary YouTube video filming (talking head, interview, or breakdown format)

Wednesday: Review edit from previous week's video; approve thumbnail; write newsletter

Thursday: Film second YouTube video or podcast content; social media engagement

Friday: Review final edits; approve content for next week; content planning session

Weekend: Rest + capture spontaneous B-roll from real life (training, social, travel)

Monthly Rhythm

Week 1: Monthly income report video + strategy content

Week 2: Lifestyle/transformation content + behind-the-scenes film update

Week 3: Deep-dive business breakdown or tutorial

Week 4: Podcast guest interview (from 2027) + personal vlog
