Episode 1: Starting My Agency & No Other Choice

Format: Docuseries / film essay hybrid

Runtime target: 12-18 minutes

Film: No Other Choice (2025, Park Chan-wook)

Tone: Confident, fast-paced, cinematic. NOT a tutorial. NOT a vlog. A produced piece that makes the viewer think "this guy knows what he's doing."

THE STRUCTURE

Three acts, mirroring the film's structure. Each act opens with the film parallel, then cuts to your real life executing at speed.

ACT 1: "THE LAYOFF" — Why I'm doing this

Film Beat (30-60 sec)

  • Brief, punchy review setup. Who is Park Chan-wook. What is No Other Choice about.
  • Focus on Man-su's moment of being cut loose. He's an expert. He's skilled. But the system doesn't care.
  • Clip references (fair use — brief, transformative commentary): Man-su getting the news, the walk home.

Your Story (2-3 min)

The "why." Don't dwell, don't get emotional — be matter-of-fact. Fast.

Talking head (cinematic — shallow depth of field, good light):

  • "I'm starting a creative agency. Here's why."
  • Quick version of your background — filmmaker, photographer, creative.
  • The honest situation: you have the skills, you have no clients, you have no money coming in from this yet.
  • The parallel: "Man-su is an award-winning papermaker. He knows exactly how to do the job. His problem isn't skill — it's access. That's my problem too."

B-roll to capture:

  • Your desk/workspace setup (make it look good — clean, intentional)
  • The MASTER WORKSPACE on screen (quick flash, don't explain it)
  • Walking through Brisbane — the city you're building this in

Vibe: "I'm not panicking. I have a plan."

ACT 2: "THE PLAN" — Building the machine

Film Beat (30-45 sec)

  • Man-su makes a list. He's methodical. He identifies every competitor, researches them, builds a system.
  • Draw the parallel: "Man-su's approach is psychotic. Mine is... slightly less psychotic. But the discipline is the same. You identify what needs to happen, and you execute."

Your Story (5-7 min) — THE MONTAGE

This is the centerpiece. Fast cuts. Music-driven. Competence porn. Each step gets 20-40 seconds max. No explaining HOW — just showing you DOING it.

Sequence 1: The Foundation

  • ☐ SHOT: Registering the business name (screen recording, fast-forward)
  • ☐ SHOT: ABN registration confirmation on screen
  • ☐ SHOT: Buying the domain — typing nunyabunya.com into the registrar
  • ☐ SHOT: Setting up the logo (quick flash of the brand assets)

Sequence 2: The Website

  • ☐ SHOT: Code on screen, fingers on keyboard, building the site (timelapse)
  • ☐ SHOT: The website going live — refreshing the browser, it loads
  • ☐ SHOT: Scrolling through the finished site on your phone
  • ☐ SHOT: Quick cuts of the case studies, the portfolio page, the booking page

Sequence 3: The Marketing Machine

  • ☐ SHOT: Setting up Mautic (email platform on screen, drag-and-drop email builder)
  • ☐ SHOT: Building a landing page — before/after of blank page → finished
  • ☐ SHOT: Creating a lead magnet (the free audit, the Brand Bible offer — whatever you're using)
  • ☐ SHOT: Setting up Cal.com — the booking link going live
  • ☐ SHOT: The funnel visualised — quick whiteboard sketch or screen diagram showing: ad → landing page → form → email drip → booking

Sequence 4: The Ads & Content

  • ☐ SHOT: Designing social posts (Canva or whatever you use, fast-forward)
  • ☐ SHOT: Writing ad copy (close-up of hands typing, screen showing the words appearing)
  • ☐ SHOT: Scheduling posts — the content calendar filling up
  • ☐ SHOT: Hitting "publish" on the first ad/post

Sequence 5: Going Physical

  • ☐ SHOT: Printing flyers (the printer, the stack of paper)
  • ☐ SHOT: Walking through Brisbane putting up flyers, handing them to business owners
  • ☐ SHOT: Door knocking — approaching a business, the moment before you walk in
  • ☐ SHOT: Sitting down with a potential client (even if it's just a friend playing the part for b-roll)

Editing style for Act 2:

  • Music: something building, rhythmic, confident (think Whiplash rehearsal montage energy)
  • Fast cuts on the beat
  • No voiceover during the montage — let the music and visuals carry it
  • Occasional text overlays: "DAY 1" → "DAY 3" → "WEEK 2" (sell the speed)
  • Sound design: keyboard clicks, mouse clicks, printer sounds, footsteps, door knocks — layer them rhythmically

ACT 3: "THE KILL" — Getting the first client

Film Beat (30-45 sec)

  • Man-su's first "kill." The moment of no return. He commits.
  • "There's a moment in every business where you stop building and start selling. Man-su crosses a line he can't uncross. For me, the line was [picking up the phone / walking into that office / sending that first cold email]. It's not violent, but it's terrifying."

Your Story (3-4 min)

The payoff. This is the emotional climax.

Option A — If you have a real client by filming day:

  • ☐ SHOT: The notification — the email, the Slack alert, the phone call. The moment you know someone is paying you.
  • ☐ SHOT: Your genuine reaction (film yourself checking your phone/laptop and reacting)
  • ☐ SHOT: The Cal.com booking confirmation on screen
  • ☐ SHOT: Walking to the meeting / the handshake / sitting across from your first client
  • ☐ SHOT: Talking head reflection — what it felt like, what it means

Option B — If you don't have a client yet (still powerful):

  • ☐ SHOT: The outreach montage — DMs sent, emails fired, flyers posted, doors knocked
  • ☐ SHOT: The waiting — sitting at the desk, checking the phone, refreshing the inbox
  • ☐ SHOT: Honest talking head — "I haven't landed the first client yet. But Man-su waited 13 months. I'm on month ☑. The machine is built. The work is done. Now I wait."
  • ☐ SHOT: End on you walking out the door, heading somewhere with purpose — the hustle continues

Either way, close with:

  • Quick film review wrap-up. Your rating of No Other Choice. Why you think ORCA voters chose it. What it means to you personally.
  • Tease Episode 2: "Next time — [film name]. And hopefully, a very different update."

SHORTS (4 clips to cut from this episode)

Short 1: "The $16/Month Stack" (30-45 sec)

  • Fast montage of the tools: Mautic, Matomo, Shlink, n8n — all self-hosted
  • Text overlay: "My entire marketing stack costs $16/month"
  • End with the dashboard on screen looking professional
  • Hook: "Everyone told me I need HubSpot, Mailchimp, Calendly..."

Short 2: "Door Knocking for Clients" (30-60 sec)

  • The physical outreach footage — walking up to businesses, flyers in hand
  • Raw, real, no music (or very minimal)
  • Hook: "Day ☑ of starting my agency. No clients yet. Time to knock on doors."

Short 3: "Film Review in 60 Seconds — No Other Choice" (60 sec)

  • Tight, punchy review. Your best film criticism in under a minute.
  • This is the ORCA brand crossover — positions you as a serious critic
  • Hook: "The best film of 2025 that nobody saw."

Short 4: "The Moment I Got My First Client" (30-45 sec)

  • If you get the client: the raw reaction footage, the notification, the feeling
  • If not yet: "I built everything. The website. The emails. The ads. The funnels. Now I wait." (cliffhanger — drives people to the full episode)

PRODUCTION NOTES

Camera

  • Talking heads: tripod, eye-level, shallow DOF, natural light from a window
  • B-roll: handheld where it makes sense (walking, door knocking), tripod for screen work
  • Street photography feel for the Brisbane walking shots — this is your style, lean into it

Audio

  • Talking heads: lav mic or shotgun
  • B-roll: capture ambient sound (city, office, keyboard, footsteps) — use in the edit
  • Music: license 2-3 tracks. One building/rhythmic for Act 2 montage, one reflective for Act 3

Edit Style

  • Color grade: cinematic, slightly desaturated with warm tones (match your street photography aesthetic)
  • Text: minimal, clean, no flashy motion graphics. You're a filmmaker, not a YouTuber.
  • Pacing: FAST in Act 2 (the montage), slower and more personal in Acts 1 and 3
  • Film clips: use sparingly, only to set up the parallel. Your footage should dominate.

What to Film BEFORE You Need It

  • ☐ Screen recordings: business registration, website build, Mautic setup, Cal.com, ad creation (timelapse these)
  • ☐ Flyer design + printing
  • ☐ Brisbane walking shots (golden hour preferred)
  • ☐ Workspace setup — clean it, light it well, film it
  • ☐ Talking heads — film all three acts' talking head segments in one sitting, different shirts if you want to imply different days

FILMING DAY CHECKLIST

Morning (at the computer — screen recordings):

  • ☐ Record timelapse of website work
  • ☐ Record setting up email/funnel/booking
  • ☐ Record ad creation process
  • ☐ Record flyer design

Midday (talking heads):

  • ☐ Act 1 talking head — the "why" (2-3 takes, keep it natural)
  • ☐ Act 2 transition — the "plan" bridge (1-2 takes)
  • ☐ Act 3 talking head — the reflection (2-3 takes)
  • ☐ Film review segments — No Other Choice thoughts (multiple takes, you'll cut these up)

Afternoon (out in the world):

  • ☐ Brisbane walking shots (heading to businesses, street scenes)
  • ☐ Flyer posting / handing out
  • ☐ Door knocking footage
  • ☐ Coffee shop working shot (laptop open, headphones on — the grind)
  • ☐ "Walking with purpose" closing shot
