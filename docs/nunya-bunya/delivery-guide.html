Client Delivery Guide — Nunya Bunya

How to Deliver Starter Package Content to Clients

Option 1: Google Drive Shared Folder (Recommended for Launch)

Cost: $0 | Setup: 10 minutes

  1. Create a shared Google Drive folder per client: Nunya Bunya / [Client Name] / Deliverables
  2. Sub-folders: Blog Posts, Social Media, Image Assets, Reports
  3. Share with client (viewer access) — they can comment but not edit
  4. Upload approved content monthly
  5. Send a Loom walkthrough video (2-3 min) explaining what's included

Why this works: Clients feel organized, everything's in one place, zero cost. Most law firms already use Google Workspace.

Option 2: Client Portal via GoHighLevel (When You Subscribe)

Cost: Included in GHL subscription

  1. Create a client portal in each GHL location
  2. Upload deliverables as documents/files
  3. Client logs in to view reports, approve content, track progress
  4. Bonus: Combine with lead pipeline so they see leads + marketing in one place

Why this works: Professional, branded, integrates with CRM. Best option once you're on GHL.

Option 3: WordPress Client Area

Cost: $0 (if client has WordPress)

  1. Install a simple members plugin (e.g., Members, or use password-protected pages)
  2. Create a /client-portal/ page with links to monthly deliverables
  3. Upload reports as PDFs, blog drafts show in the WP drafts section naturally

Why this works: Client already has WordPress, no new tools.

Option 4: Email + Loom (Simplest)

Cost: $0

  1. Send a monthly email with all deliverables attached
  2. Record a 3-5 min Loom video walking through:
  • What content was created
  • Key metrics from the weekly report
  • Recommendations for next month
  1. Include links to blog drafts in WordPress for approval

Why this works: Zero friction, personal touch, clients love the video walkthrough.

Tips for Client Satisfaction

  1. Always over-deliver early — Send the first month's content before they expect it
  2. Loom > email — A 3-minute video walkthrough makes you look 10x more attentive
  3. Show the process — Clients love seeing the content calendar before the content
  4. Ask for feedback on tone — First month, get explicit approval on voice/tone
  5. Bundle reports with recommendations — Never just send data, always include "here's what we should do next"
  6. Monthly strategy call — Even 15 minutes on Zoom keeps retention high (Growth tier includes this)
