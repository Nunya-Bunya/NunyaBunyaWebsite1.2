SOP-CO-002 — Client Kickoff Checklist

Category: Client Onboarding

Owner: Ben Conner

Created: 2026-03-23

Status: Active

Purpose

Standardize what you need from every new client before any work begins. Nothing starts until everything on this list is confirmed.

Before You Start — Non-Negotiables

These must be completed before you touch anything:

#ItemStatus Template
1Signed contract☐
2First month's payment received☐
3Monthly ad spend budget confirmed (recommend $1,500-$3,000/mo starting)☐

What The Client Does NOT Need to Decide

Make this clear upfront — it saves time and positions you as the expert:

  • Budget split between platforms (Google vs Meta vs other) — your call
  • A/B testing strategy — your call
  • Audience targeting details — your call
  • Ad creative direction — your call (they approve final versions)
  • Technical setup — your call
  • Posting schedule — your call

What they hear:

"I need the contract signed, first month's payment, and your ad budget. Then I handle everything. You'll get a report every two weeks showing how many leads came in and what they cost. That's it."

Onboarding Flow

Day 0:  Contract signed + payment received
Day 1:  Collect all access + assets (this checklist)
Day 2:  Install tracking, audit website, set up accounts
Day 3:  Research competitors, keywords, audiences
Day 4:  Build landing page + lead capture
Day 5:  Launch ads
Day 7:  First check-in call with client
Day 14: First performance report

Add To Contract

The following should be referenced in the contract or sent as an onboarding email immediately after signing:

Suggested contract clause:

"Upon signing, the Client agrees to provide the Service Provider with access to the following within 5 business days: Facebook Business Manager, Google Ads account (or credentials to create one), website CMS/hosting, domain registrar (if DNS changes are required), social media accounts, brand assets (logo, photos), and confirmation of monthly ad spend budget. Delays in providing access may delay campaign launch timelines."

Suggested onboarding email:

Subject: Let's get started — what I need from you

Hey [name],

Excited to kick things off. To get your campaigns up and running as fast as possible, I need a few things from you:

1. Facebook Business Manager — add me as a Partner (I'll send instructions)
2. Google Ads — I'll set this up, just need your approval
3. Website login — [platform name] so I can install tracking
4. Your logo (high res) and any photos of your [business/products/team]
5. Confirm your monthly ad budget (I recommend $X-$Y to start)
6. Best phone number for lead notifications

Once I have these, I'll have ads running within 5 business days.

Talk soon,
Ben
