Business Foundation Kit — Intake Survey

Instructions to the client: Answer every question as honestly and specifically as you can. There are no wrong answers. If you don't know something yet, say "not sure" — that's useful information too. Your answers directly fuel the 20 core documents we'll build for your business.

Estimated time: 30–45 minutes

SECTION 1: YOU & YOUR BUSINESS (Questions 1–8)

1. What is your business name?

(If you haven't decided yet, what are you leaning toward?)

2. What does your business do in one sentence?

(Example: "I help small restaurants get more customers through social media management.")

3. Is your business already operating, or are you in the planning/startup phase?

  • Already operating and making money
  • Operating but no revenue yet
  • Still in the planning stage
  • Just an idea right now

4. What type of business is this?

  • Service-based (you do work for clients)
  • Product-based (you sell physical or digital products)
  • SaaS / Software
  • E-commerce
  • Content / Media
  • Other: ___

5. Are you a solo operator, or do you have (or plan to have) a team?

  • Just me
  • Me + 1–2 people
  • Small team (3–10)
  • Planning to grow a team eventually

6. Why did you start (or want to start) this business?

(What's the real reason — not the polished version. What problem did you experience or see that made you say "I should build this"?)

7. Where do you want this business to be in 3 years?

(Revenue, lifestyle, team size, reputation — whatever matters most to you.)

8. What's your single biggest fear or obstacle right now?

SECTION 2: YOUR CUSTOMERS (Questions 9–17)

9. Who is your ideal customer? Describe them like a real person.

(Age range, job title or role, income level, location, lifestyle — be as specific as possible.)

10. What is the #1 problem or pain point your ideal customer has that your business solves?

11. What happens if they DON'T solve this problem? What does their life/business look like if they do nothing?

12. Have you already worked with any customers or clients? If yes, describe your best one.

(What made them great? What did they buy? How did they find you?)

13. Where do your ideal customers hang out — online and offline?

(Social platforms, forums, events, publications, communities, etc.)

14. When your ideal customer is searching for a solution like yours, what would they type into Google?

(List 3–5 phrases.)

15. What objections or hesitations do potential customers have before buying from you?

(Price too high? Don't trust it? Don't understand it? Already tried something similar?)

16. Is your customer the person who pays, or do they need to convince someone else (a boss, partner, spouse)?

17. How much is solving this problem worth to your customer?

(In dollars, time saved, stress reduced — help us understand the value from their perspective.)

SECTION 3: WHAT YOU SELL (Questions 18–25)

18. List every product or service you offer (or plan to offer). For each one, include:

  • Name
  • Brief description
  • Price (or planned price)
  • Who it's for

19. Which single offering is your "flagship" — the one you'd want most customers to buy?

20. What does a customer actually receive or experience when they buy from you?

(Deliverables, timeline, meetings, results — walk us through it step by step.)

21. How long does it take to deliver your product/service from purchase to completion?

22. Do you offer different tiers or packages (e.g., Basic / Pro / Premium)?

If yes, describe them. If no, would you consider it?

23. What makes your offer different from just hiring someone cheaper on Fiverr or doing it themselves?

24. Do you have any guarantees, warranties, or risk-reversals?

(Money-back guarantee, free revisions, performance guarantees, etc.)

25. What's your capacity? How many customers/clients can you realistically serve per month right now?

SECTION 4: YOUR COMPETITION (Questions 26–30)

26. Name 3–5 competitors or alternatives your customers might consider instead of you.

(Direct competitors, DIY solutions, or "do nothing" all count.)

27. What do your competitors do well?

28. What do your competitors do poorly or ignore?

29. How is your approach fundamentally different from theirs?

(Not just "better quality" — what's structurally different about how you operate, deliver, or think?)

30. What would make a customer choose you over the most popular competitor, even if you're more expensive?

SECTION 5: BRAND & PERSONALITY (Questions 31–37)

31. If your business were a person at a party, how would they talk?

Pick all that apply:

  • Professional and polished
  • Friendly and casual
  • Bold and provocative
  • Warm and empathetic
  • Witty and sharp
  • Calm and authoritative
  • Energetic and hype
  • Other: ___

32. What are 3 words you want people to associate with your brand?

33. What brands (in any industry) do you admire and why?

(Example: "Apple — because everything feels simple and premium.")

34. Is there a tone or style you absolutely do NOT want?

(Example: "Don't sound corporate." or "Nothing cutesy.")

35. Do you have existing brand assets?

  • Logo
  • Brand colors
  • Fonts
  • Social media profiles

(List what exists and share links/files if possible.)

35b. What's your website URL? (if you have one)

(We'll look at your site to match your existing brand. Leave blank if you don't have one yet.)

36. What's your brand's origin story in 2–3 sentences?

(How did this business come to exist? What's the human story behind it?)

37. Do you have a tagline or slogan? If not, is there a phrase you keep coming back to when describing what you do?

SECTION 6: SALES & MONEY (Questions 38–44)

38. How do customers currently find you?

(Word of mouth, social media, Google, referrals, cold outreach, ads, etc.)

39. What's your current monthly revenue?

  • $0 (pre-revenue)
  • Under $1,000/mo
  • $1,000–$5,000/mo
  • $5,000–$20,000/mo
  • $20,000+/mo

40. What monthly revenue would make this business "working" for you?

(The number where you'd feel like it's real and sustainable.)

41. Walk us through how a stranger becomes a paying customer today (or how you imagine it working).

(Example: "They see my Instagram post → click link in bio → book a free call → I send a proposal → they pay.")

42. What's your average sale value (or what do you want it to be)?

43. Do you currently have any of these in place?

  • Email list
  • CRM or contact database
  • Booking/scheduling system
  • Contracts or service agreements
  • Invoicing system
  • Website with clear call-to-action

44. What's your monthly budget for marketing and tools?

  • $0 (bootstrapping)
  • Under $100/mo
  • $100–$500/mo
  • $500–$2,000/mo
  • $2,000+/mo

SECTION 7: OPERATIONS & DELIVERY (Questions 45–50)

45. What does a typical work week look like for you right now?

(Hours spent on the business, what tasks eat most of your time.)

46. What tools or software do you currently use to run your business?

(Anything — Google Docs, Canva, QuickBooks, Notion, a notebook, whatever.)

47. What's the one task in your business you wish you could hand off or automate tomorrow?

48. When you deliver your product/service, what are the key steps from start to finish?

(Example: "1. Client fills out brief → 2. I draft designs → 3. Client reviews → 4. Revisions → 5. Final delivery.")

49. What does amazing client/customer experience look like to you?

(How do you want people to FEEL when they work with you or buy from you?)

50. Is there anything else we should know about your business, your customers, or your goals that we didn't ask?
