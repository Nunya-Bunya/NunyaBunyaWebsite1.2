■ Nunya Bunya 01-Nunya Bunya / Docs

Offer Dissemination Engine

Purpose: Turn clear offers into contextual replies, posts, and outreach across places where people are already talking about the problems Nunya Bunya and Power Portraits solve.

Status: Working operating spec, created 2026-04-14.

1. Current Focus

The website is not the main growth lever right now.

The priority is:

  1. sharpen the offers
  2. find active internet conversations about the problems those offers solve
  3. draft useful, contextual replies
  4. approve and post where platform rules allow
  5. track which offers, problems, and phrases generate replies or calls

The engine should act like a listening and response system, not a spam cannon.

2. Primary Offers To Disseminate

Free Marketing Audit

Problem thread: "My small business marketing is not working and I do not know what to fix."

Best use: Low-friction reply when someone is confused, overwhelmed, or asking for advice.

CTA: Book a 15-minute audit.

Bedrock Lite

Problem thread: "I need a site or landing page, but I am not ready for a full agency."

Best use: Micro-businesses, early-stage operators, solo service providers.

CTA: Ask if they want the basic online foundation mapped.

Bedrock

Problem thread: "I have a website, but it does not generate leads."

Best use: Local service businesses with obvious disconnected marketing pieces.

CTA: Book a call to map the connected system: website, landing page, lead magnet, email, booking, tracking, CRM.

Business Foundation Kit

Problem thread: "I am starting/building a business and need clarity."

Best use: Founders, solopreneurs, builders in public, people asking for business plan or positioning help.

CTA: Generate the free kit.

Power Portraits Headshots

Problem thread: "I need headshots, LinkedIn photos, personal brand photos, or team photos in Brisbane."

Best use: Brisbane professionals and businesses with explicit photo intent.

CTA: Book the relevant session.

4. Operating Rule

Every dissemination item needs four fields:

  • Problem: What pain signal did the person express?
  • Offer: Which offer solves that specific problem?
  • Context: What did they actually say, and what is the useful answer?
  • CTA: What is the lightest next step?

If the reply cannot pass those four checks, skip it.

5. Near-Term Build Path

Phase 1: Offer Control

  • Keep the source of truth in offer-thread-searches.json.
  • Add or remove searches based on actual replies, not vibes.
  • Make every reply generator read from the same offer/search config.

Phase 2: Problem Thread Inbox

  • Store found threads in Supabase before drafting replies.
  • Track platform, thread URL, author, query, problem, offer name, relevance score, and status.
  • Statuses: found, drafted, approved, posted, skipped, replied, converted.

Phase 3: Human Review

  • Draft replies automatically.
  • Require approval before posting or use copy-paste mode where APIs are limited.
  • Keep a reason for every skip so the search terms improve.

Phase 4: More Surfaces

  • X/Twitter first because the existing finder already works there.
  • Reddit next, using subreddit/search monitoring and strict value-first replies.
  • Facebook groups next, but only where group rules allow business advice or offers.
  • LinkedIn next for professional services and Power Portraits.

6. Safety And Platform Fit

The engine should respect platform rules and avoid pretending to be a normal user when it is automating.

Do:

  • join conversations where the problem is explicit
  • give the useful answer first
  • disclose the offer naturally
  • use human approval for replies
  • stop posting in communities that reject promotional replies

Do not:

  • mass-post generic sales comments
  • scrape private groups without permission
  • evade anti-spam systems
  • pretend a paid client result exists when it does not
  • force links into every reply

7. Success Metrics

Track:

  • problems found per day
  • useful drafts generated per day
  • approved replies/posts
  • replies received
  • DMs started
  • calls booked
  • offers clicked
  • deals created
  • clients won

The winning query is not the one with the most posts.

The winning query is the one that produces qualified conversations.
