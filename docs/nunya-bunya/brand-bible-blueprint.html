The Brand Bible — Ultimate Blueprint

Created: 2026-04-07

Purpose: Define the definitive Brand Bible product that Nunya Bunya will create for every client. This is not a document — it's a weapon. It should be the single most valuable physical object a small business owner has ever been handed.

The Vision

When a business owner holds their Brand Bible, they should feel three things:

  1. Clarity — "I finally understand my own business"
  2. Confidence — "I know exactly what to say, how to look, and where to go"
  3. Pride — "This is the most professional thing anyone has ever made for me"

Most small business owners operate from gut feel, scattered notes, and whatever their cousin said at Christmas. The Brand Bible replaces all of that with a single, definitive source of truth that they can open to any page and find an answer.

The Sections (29 total, organized into 5 parts)

PART I: WHO YOU ARE (Identity)

1. Cover Page

  • Business name, logo (full color, large)
  • "Brand Bible" in elegant typography
  • "Prepared by Nunya Bunya" with date
  • Version number (Brand Bibles get updated annually)
  • Premium feel: think Vogue cover, not PowerPoint

2. Table of Contents

  • Clean, elegant, with page numbers
  • Section tabs color-coded by Part

3. Founder's Letter

  • A personal letter FROM the business owner TO themselves (we ghostwrite it, they approve)
  • Why they started this business
  • What they want it to become
  • What they stand for
  • This page alone will make them emotional. That's the point. When they're having a bad month, they open to this page and remember why.

4. Mission, Vision & Values

  • Mission: What you do and for whom (one sentence)
  • Vision: Where you're going (one sentence)
  • Values: 3-5 core values with one-line explanations
  • Each value should have a "what this looks like in practice" example
  • NOT corporate fluff. Real, specific, human.

5. Origin Story

  • How the business started (2-3 paragraphs, narrative style)
  • The turning point / "aha moment"
  • What makes the founder uniquely qualified
  • This is the story they tell at networking events, on podcasts, in interviews

6. Brand Personality

  • If your brand were a person, who would they be?
  • 5 personality traits with explanations
  • Tone spectrum: "We are [warm] not [cold]. We are [confident] not [arrogant]."
  • Communication do's and don'ts with examples
  • Sample social media post in the brand voice vs. NOT in the brand voice

7. Legal & Compliance

  • Business registration (ABN, company structure, trading name)
  • Required licenses and permits for the industry (e.g., health practitioner registration, food safety, trade licenses)
  • Insurance requirements: public liability, professional indemnity, workers comp (with recommended minimums)
  • Industry body memberships and accreditations (e.g., Massage & Myotherapy Australia, Master Builders, etc.)
  • Privacy obligations (Australian Privacy Principles, how you collect/store customer data)
  • Consumer law basics (ACCC obligations, refund policy requirements, advertising standards)
  • Workplace health & safety essentials
  • "Red flags" checklist: things that will get you fined, sued, or shut down if you ignore them
  • This section should scare them just enough to take it seriously, then immediately reassure them with a clear checklist. Not a legal textbook — a practical "here's what you need to have in place before you open the doors" guide.

PART II: HOW YOU LOOK (Visual Identity)

8. Logo Suite

  • Primary logo (full color)
  • Secondary logo (horizontal/stacked variant)
  • Icon/mark only
  • White version (for dark backgrounds)
  • Minimum size requirements
  • Clear space rules (with measurements)
  • What NEVER to do (stretched, wrong colors, on busy backgrounds — show bad examples)

9. Color Palette

  • Primary color with hex, RGB, CMYK, Pantone
  • Secondary colors (2-3)
  • Accent color
  • Background colors
  • Text colors
  • Color hierarchy: when to use which
  • Color combinations that work (show examples)
  • Color combinations that DON'T work (show bad examples)
  • Large swatches — full page, beautiful, frame-worthy

10. Typography

  • Primary heading font (name, weights, where to download)
  • Secondary body font
  • Accent/display font (if any)
  • Font pairing examples (headlines + body together)
  • Size scale: H1, H2, H3, body, caption, button
  • Line height and letter spacing rules
  • Fonts to NEVER use (show examples of bad pairings)

11. Photography & Imagery Style

  • Mood board (6-9 reference images showing the desired aesthetic)
  • Do's: warm lighting, natural tones, lifestyle shots, real people
  • Don'ts: stock photo clichés, overly posed, clinical, cold lighting
  • Photo treatment: filters, color grading, cropping style
  • Example: "Here's a photo that IS on brand" vs "Here's one that ISN'T"
  • Guidelines for phone photography (for when the owner takes their own photos)

12. Graphic Elements & Patterns

  • Icons style (line, filled, custom?)
  • Border/divider styles
  • Background patterns or textures
  • Social media frame templates
  • Business card design
  • Email signature design
  • Any branded graphics (badges, stamps, seals)

PART III: WHO YOU SERVE (Customers)

13. Ideal Customer Profiles (Personas)

  • 3-4 detailed personas
  • Each gets a full page with:
  • Name, age, occupation, photo (stock or AI)
  • Daily routine
  • Pain points (what keeps them up at night)
  • Goals (what they want)
  • How they find businesses like yours
  • What would make them choose YOU over a competitor
  • Direct quotes: "I wish I could find someone who..."
  • Best channels to reach them

14. Customer Journey Map

  • Stage 1: Awareness (they realize they have a problem)
  • Stage 2: Consideration (they research options)
  • Stage 3: Decision (they choose you)
  • Stage 4: Experience (the actual service)
  • Stage 5: Loyalty (they come back)
  • Stage 6: Advocacy (they tell others)
  • At each stage: what the customer thinks, feels, does, and what YOU should be doing

15. Voice of the Customer

  • Best reviews (full quotes, attributed)
  • Common praise themes ("professional", "welcoming", "pain gone")
  • Common objections or concerns and how to address them
  • Testimonial graphics (designed, shareable, social-media-ready)
  • NPS score or satisfaction metric if available

PART IV: WHAT YOU OFFER (Products & Strategy)

16. Service/Product Menu

  • Every service with description, duration, price
  • Presented as a beautiful menu (think restaurant menu design)
  • Grouped by customer need, not by internal categories
  • Each service: who it's for, what to expect, recommended frequency
  • Upsell pathways: "If you liked X, try Y"

17. Pricing Strategy

  • Price positioning (budget / mid / premium — and why)
  • Competitor price comparison (table)
  • Value justification: what makes your price worth it
  • Special offers / first-time pricing
  • Package deals / loyalty pricing

18. Pricing Psychology & Packages

  • Anchoring: show the premium option first so the mid-tier feels reasonable
  • Tiering: Good / Better / Best structure with clear value steps between each
  • Package design: bundle services that naturally go together (e.g., "The Recovery Package: deep tissue + hot stone + stretching consultation")
  • First-visit offers: what to offer to get them in the door without devaluing the service (free consult, not discounted service)
  • Upsell pathways: "Clients who book X usually add Y" — design these intentionally, not randomly
  • Membership / prepaid models: when they make sense and how to price them (e.g., "Buy 5 get 1 free" vs "Monthly membership at 15% off")
  • Price presentation: how the numbers appear on menus, websites, and quotes (never lead with the price — lead with the outcome)
  • Psychological triggers: charm pricing ($89 not $90), decoy pricing, time-based urgency (without being sleazy)
  • This section turns their price list into a revenue strategy. Most small businesses set prices once and never think about it again. This makes them think about it properly.

19. Competitive Landscape

  • Top 5 competitors (name, location, pricing, strengths, weaknesses)
  • Your differentiators (what you do that they don't)
  • Competitive positioning map (2x2 matrix: price vs. quality, or similar)
  • Opportunities they're missing that you can capture

20. Unique Selling Proposition

  • The ONE thing that makes you different, in one sentence
  • The elevator pitch (30 seconds)
  • The dinner party answer ("So what do you do?")
  • The website headline
  • All four should say the same thing differently

PART V: HOW YOU GROW (Marketing & Operations)

21. Marketing Strategy Overview

  • Goals for the next 12 months
  • Key channels and why each one matters
  • Budget allocation (pie chart)
  • Key metrics to track
  • One-page strategy summary (the "cheat sheet" they can stick on their wall)

22. Partnerships & Referral Strategy

  • Top 10 businesses to partner with (specific types, not generic — e.g., for a massage therapist: physios, chiropractors, gyms, yoga studios, hotels, corporate offices, real estate agents doing settlement gifts)
  • Why each partner benefits from referring to you (it has to be mutual — what's in it for them?)
  • Referral agreement template: how it works, what you offer in return (reciprocal referrals, commission, gift cards, co-marketing)
  • Co-marketing opportunities: joint social posts, shared flyers, bundled packages, event collaborations
  • Community presence: local events, markets, sponsorships, charity partnerships — where to show up and why
  • Introduction script: exactly what to say when you walk into a physio's office for the first time
  • Referral tracking: how to know which partners are sending you clients (unique booking links, "how did you hear about us" field, partner codes)
  • This is the most underused growth channel for local businesses. A single strong referral partner can be worth more than $10K in ad spend. Most business owners know they should do it but don't know how to start the conversation.

23. Content Strategy

  • Content pillars (4-5 themes they'll create content about)
  • Platform-by-platform plan (what to post where, how often)
  • Content calendar template (monthly view)
  • 30 content ideas ready to use (headlines + format + platform)
  • Hashtag strategy per platform

24. SEO Strategy

  • Target keywords (primary + secondary + long-tail)
  • Keyword clusters mapped to content
  • Local SEO checklist (Google Business Profile, citations, reviews)
  • Blog post topics mapped to keywords
  • Current rankings baseline (if available)

25. Paid Advertising Playbook

  • Meta Ads: 5 ad copy variations ready to run
  • Google Ads: 3 ad variations ready to run
  • Targeting strategy (audiences, demographics, interests)
  • Budget recommendations
  • Expected metrics (CPC, CPA, ROAS)
  • Ad creative guidelines (what works, what doesn't)
  • Retargeting strategy

26. Email Marketing Playbook

  • Lead magnet strategy (what to offer, how to capture)
  • Welcome sequence (5 emails, fully written)
  • Monthly newsletter template
  • Re-engagement sequence
  • Review request sequence
  • Subject line formulas that work

27. Sales Process

  • How enquiries come in (channels)
  • Response time targets
  • Consultation/discovery process
  • How to close (scripts/talking points)
  • Follow-up cadence
  • Objection handling (top 5 objections + responses)

28. 90-Day Action Plan

  • Week-by-week breakdown
  • Each week: 2-3 specific actions with expected outcomes
  • Milestone markers (end of month 1, 2, 3)
  • KPI targets for each month
  • "If you do nothing else, do THESE 3 things" summary

29. Quick Reference Cards (Pull-Out Section)

  • One-page brand cheat sheet (logo, colors, fonts, voice — laminated)
  • Social media posting schedule (laminated)
  • Elevator pitch card (wallet-sized)
  • Emergency contact card (who to call for what)
  • QR code linking to digital versions of all documents

Design Principles for the Physical Bible

Paper: 160gsm matte coated (thick, luxurious, no bleed-through)

Cover: Leatherette or linen with foil-stamped business name

Binding: Lay-flat binding (so it stays open on a desk) or premium ring binder with custom cover

Size: A4 portrait

Page count: 50-80 pages (depending on business complexity)

Tabs: Color-coded section dividers with printed tabs

Inserts: Pull-out cards in a pocket at the back (elevator pitch, cheat sheet)

Sleeve: Custom cardboard sleeve or box for presentation

The unboxing experience matters. When they open it, it should feel like opening a luxury product. Tissue paper. A handwritten note from you. The Brand Bible in its sleeve. A business card. Maybe a small branded item (pen, notebook). First impressions are everything.

How to Get to 100%

Phase 1: Client Interview (30 min)

Many sections can't be generated from scraping alone. We need a structured client interview:

  • Why did you start this business?
  • What do you want it to become in 5 years?
  • Describe your ideal customer
  • What do people usually say after their first visit?
  • What's the one thing you do better than anyone?
  • What are your top 3 values?

This interview feeds into: Founder's Letter, Origin Story, Mission/Vision, USP, Brand Personality.

Phase 2: AI Generation (automated)

Everything else can be generated by our agents using scraped data + interview responses:

  • Personas (audienceresearchagent)
  • Competitor analysis (competitoranalysisagent)
  • Content strategy (content_generator)
  • SEO strategy (seo_agent)
  • Ad variations (adsagent / creativestrategist)
  • Email sequences (content_generator)
  • 90-day plan (strategy_agent)

Phase 3: Design (semi-automated)

  • HTML template with print-ready A4 styling
  • Mood boards assembled from stock photos
  • Color swatch pages
  • Service menu designed like a restaurant menu
  • Quick reference cards as separate printable pages

Phase 4: Print

  • Export to PDF
  • Print on premium paper
  • Bind in custom cover
  • Add section tabs
  • Package in presentation sleeve

The Killer Feature

Annual Updates. Every year, we update the Brand Bible with:

  • New reviews
  • Updated competitor analysis
  • Refreshed 90-day plan
  • New content ideas
  • Performance data from the past year
  • Any brand evolution (new services, pricing changes)

The client pays $500/year for the update. We print a new edition. They have a living document that grows with their business. This creates recurring revenue AND keeps the client engaged.
