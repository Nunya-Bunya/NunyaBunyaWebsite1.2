Backburner — Nunya Bunya

Purpose: Good ideas that are not active commitments.

This is where tempting projects go when they might be valuable later, but they should not compete with the current action list.

Active Priority Filter

Before moving anything from Backburner to Master Actions, it must pass all three checks:

  • It can produce revenue, proof, or operational leverage within 30 days.
  • It does not distract from the current client delivery and first-dollar priorities.
  • It has a clear next action that can be completed in one work session.

Parked Ideas

Plant Riot Tees Ecommerce Benchmark

Status: Backburnered.

Core idea: Vegan graphic t-shirt ecommerce brand used as a high-end apparel/storefront benchmark for Bedrock ecommerce delivery.

Existing assets:

  • Static ecommerce storefront in 01-Nunya Bunya/Clients/plant-riot-tees/03-site/plant-riot-store/.
  • Product mockups, cart behavior, signup behavior, handoff guide, review checklist, client input list, ecommerce notes, and welcome sequence.

Why it is interesting:

  • Useful reference for Bella Rhyder and future ecommerce clients.
  • Exposes product catalog, cart, checkout, inventory, lifecycle email, and analytics gaps that service Bedrock does not expose.
  • Could become a repeatable ecommerce branch later.

Why it is parked:

  • It is less likely to make money immediately than testing the filmmaker-card collectible idea.
  • Checkout, email, CRM, analytics, and fulfillment are not wired yet.
  • The current priority is demand capture for a more naturally collectible product.

Revive when:

  • The Bedrock service path is stronger, or Bella Rhyder needs an ecommerce implementation.
  • There is a specific product client who needs a storefront.
  • The ecommerce branch can be built as reusable pipeline work rather than a one-off side quest.

Local Lead Machine For Tradies

Status: Backburnered.

Core idea: Build local lead-generation pages for tradies, starting with one high-intent niche such as roof leak repair in Brisbane.

Possible MVP:

  • One landing page targeting one service and one location.
  • Simple lead form saved to Supabase.
  • Email or Slack alert when a lead arrives.
  • One provider partner to receive test leads.
  • First 3 leads free, then charge per qualified lead.

Why it is interesting:

  • It can become a reusable business model.
  • It fits Nunya Bunya's website, lead-gen, SEO, and automation strengths.
  • It can eventually expand across plumbers, roofers, electricians, cleaners, landscapers, pest control, and similar local services.

Why it is parked:

  • Nunya Bunya already has enough active fronts.
  • Mary / Bedrock proof, client acquisition, and core delivery systems matter more right now.
  • It needs a focused provider-acquisition process before it deserves build time.

Revive when:

  • Nunya Bunya has one paying client or a repeatable Bedrock delivery path.
  • There is a clear niche/provider willing to take leads.
  • Ben wants a focused 1-week experiment, not a new forever project.
