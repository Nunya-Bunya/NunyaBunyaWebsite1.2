Nunya Bunya — Client Offboarding Checklist

Standard Operating Procedure: Client Departure Process

Version 1.0 | March 2026

OVERVIEW

A well-executed offboarding is one of the most powerful tools a service business has for generating referrals and future re-engagement. A client who leaves and has a seamless, professional exit experience will refer more clients, leave a positive review, and return when circumstances change — far more often than a client who felt ignored, confused, or poorly managed at the end.

This checklist covers the full offboarding process from the moment a client provides notice to the point where the relationship transitions to alumni status. Every step must be assigned to a named team member and completed within the timeframe specified.

Trigger: Client submits written cancellation notice (email or other written form).

PHASE 1: NOTICE RECEIVED (Day 1–3)

Immediate Actions

  • ☐ Log the cancellation notice in the CRM (HubSpot/Pipedrive) with the date received, notice period end date, and reason for leaving (if provided).
  • ☐ Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours. Send a professional, warm acknowledgement email. Confirm:
  • The notice has been received
  • The final service date (30 days from notice date per the client agreement)
  • The final invoice date
  • The account manager as their ongoing point of contact through the offboarding
Template available: [Offboarding Acknowledgement Email — see email template library]
  • ☐ Notify the full account team of the departure. Assign an offboarding lead (typically the account manager).
  • ☐ Schedule an exit call within the first week of notice, if the client is willing. Frame it as a chance to understand their needs and ensure a smooth handover — not a retention pitch (unless the client opens that door themselves).
  • ☐ Check outstanding invoices. Identify any unpaid balances and flag them to accounts.
  • ☐ Note in CRM: departure reason category (one of: budget, results, competitive loss, internal change, business closed, moving in-house, upgraded their own capability, unknown).

PHASE 2: 30-DAY NOTICE PERIOD — CONTINUED SERVICE (Days 1–30)

During the notice period, services continue at full capacity. The client is still paying and still deserves full attention.

Service Continuation

  • ☐ Continue all agreed deliverables at full scope for the duration of the notice period.
  • ☐ Pause any long-lead projects that will not be completed before the final day. Do not start significant new work (e.g., a website rebuild) that cannot be meaningfully completed or handed over.
  • ☐ Prioritise transfer-ready work. In the final 2 weeks, shift focus to:
  • Creating documentation the client can use post-handover
  • Completing any in-progress content
  • Ensuring all accounts and assets are cleanly organised for transfer
  • ☐ Weekly check-ins during the notice period — brief updates to keep the client informed and the relationship positive.

PHASE 3: ACCESS REVOCATION CHECKLIST (Final 5 Days)

All access must be cleanly revoked or transferred — nothing left dangling. Work through this platform by platform.

Principle: We revoke our own access. We do NOT remove client access to their own accounts. Every account must end up firmly in the client's hands.

Google Ecosystem

  • ☐ Google Analytics (GA4)
  • Remove Nunya Bunya team member(s) as users
  • Confirm the client's own email address has Admin access
  • Export final historical data (traffic, conversions) and provide to client
  • ☐ Google Search Console
  • Remove Nunya Bunya as a delegated owner/user
  • Confirm client email has verified owner access
  • Export keyword performance data for the full engagement period
  • ☐ Google Ads
  • Remove Nunya Bunya's MCC (manager) account link from the client's Google Ads account
  • Confirm client has full admin access to their account
  • Export all campaigns, ad copy, and performance data as a CSV
  • Pause (do not delete) any live campaigns unless instructed otherwise
  • Provide a handover document: campaign structure, bid strategies, and what was working
  • ☐ Google Business Profile
  • Remove Nunya Bunya team members as managers
  • Confirm client (or their new provider) has ownership access
  • ☐ Google Tag Manager
  • Remove Nunya Bunya user access
  • Document all tags, triggers, and variables currently active (export as JSON or PDF)
  • Confirm client has admin access

Meta (Facebook & Instagram)

  • ☐ Meta Business Manager
  • Remove Nunya Bunya's Business Manager from client's Business Manager (revoke partner access)
  • Alternatively, if pages/ad accounts were operated inside Nunya Bunya's Business Manager, transfer them to the client's Business Manager
  • Confirm the client has full admin access to their Business Suite
  • ☐ Facebook Page
  • Remove all Nunya Bunya team members who were added as Page roles
  • Confirm at least one client-controlled admin remains on the page
  • ☐ Instagram Account
  • Remove any Nunya Bunya team accounts linked as Instagram collaborators or third-party app access
  • Confirm client has full account access
  • ☐ Meta Ads Manager
  • Export all ad campaigns, audiences, and creative assets
  • Revoke Nunya Bunya's access to the ad account
  • Pause (do not delete) any live campaigns unless instructed

Website & Hosting

  • ☐ WordPress / Website CMS
  • Delete or deactivate any Nunya Bunya team user accounts
  • Confirm client/their developer has admin access
  • Confirm any plugins purchased by Nunya Bunya on the client's behalf have been transferred (licences in the client's name) or noted for the client to renew
  • ☐ Website Hosting / cPanel / Server Access
  • Remove any SSH keys, cPanel accounts, or hosting access belonging to Nunya Bunya
  • Provide client with all relevant credentials if they were held by Nunya Bunya
  • ☐ Domain Registrar
  • Confirm the domain is registered in the client's name (not Nunya Bunya's)
  • If not, initiate domain transfer to client's registrar account immediately

Email Marketing

  • ☐ Email Platform (e.g., Mailchimp, Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign)
  • Remove Nunya Bunya team member access
  • Export full subscriber list as CSV and provide to client
  • Export all campaign history (open rates, click rates, templates)
  • Confirm client has full admin access to the platform

Social Scheduling Tools

  • ☐ Scheduling tool (e.g., Later, Buffer, Sprout Social)
  • Revoke Nunya Bunya team access
  • Disconnect social accounts from Nunya Bunya's scheduling accounts
  • Confirm all scheduled future posts have been cancelled or handed to the client

Other Third-Party Tools

  • ☐ Any other tools used on the client's behalf (Hotjar, SEMrush access, Ahrefs projects, etc.):
  • Document and either transfer or revoke
  • Record what was used and provide the client with a list so they can assess if they want to continue subscriptions

Password and Credential Handover

  • ☐ Compile all credentials held by Nunya Bunya for the client (website admin, email marketing, etc.) in a password-protected document
  • ☐ Deliver credentials securely (password manager export, secure email, or other mutually agreed method — never unencrypted email for passwords)
  • ☐ Confirm receipt in writing by the client

PHASE 4: FINAL DELIVERABLES HANDOVER (Final 5 Days)

Content and Creative Assets

  • ☐ All original creative files created during the engagement:
  • Adobe files (AI, PSD, INDD)
  • Canva designs (transfer ownership or export as PNG/PDF)
  • Video project files (if applicable)
  • Photography (full resolution)
  • Copywriting documents
  • ☐ All published content (blog posts, social media copy, email campaigns) — export as a document archive
  • ☐ Brand assets created by Nunya Bunya (logo variations, social media templates, graphic elements) — transferred as original files

Reporting and Data

  • ☐ Full performance report history — PDF copies of all monthly reports delivered during the engagement
  • ☐ SEO history — keyword ranking history (CSV export from tracking tool), backlink profile export, technical audit reports
  • ☐ Ads performance data — export from Google Ads and Meta Ads covering the full engagement period
  • ☐ Analytics history — Google Analytics export of traffic data for the full engagement period

Strategic Documents

  • ☐ Content strategy document (if one was created)
  • ☐ SEO keyword strategy — target keyword list with rationale
  • ☐ Social media strategy — platform strategies, brand voice notes, content pillars used
  • ☐ Audience research / buyer persona documents
  • ☐ Campaign notes — what was tested, what worked, what to avoid

PHASE 5: FINANCIAL CLOSE-OUT

  • ☐ Issue final invoice for services rendered in the final month, clearly noting it is the final invoice under the agreement.
  • ☐ Confirm all previous invoices are paid. Follow up on any outstanding balances before final deliverables are transferred.
  • ☐ Settle any outstanding expenses or ad spend pass-through charges (if applicable).
  • ☐ Issue a receipt or final statement upon clearance of all amounts.
  • ☐ Archive client file in the billing system as "Closed — [Date]."

PHASE 6: EXIT SURVEY AND FEEDBACK

  • ☐ Send the exit survey within 48 hours of final service date. Keep it short — 5 questions max.

Suggested questions:

  1. What was the main reason for your decision to end the engagement? (Multiple choice with an "other" text field)
  2. How would you rate your overall experience working with Nunya Bunya? (1–10)
  3. What did we do well?
  4. What could we have done better?
  5. Would you recommend Nunya Bunya to another business? (Yes / No / Maybe)
Tool: Use Typeform, Google Form, or similar. Keep it anonymous-optional.
  • ☐ Record feedback in CRM — even if the client doesn't complete the form, note any feedback given verbally or in emails.
  • ☐ Review feedback internally — share with the full team. Do not be defensive. Look for patterns.

PHASE 7: CASE STUDY REQUEST

  • ☐ Assess suitability. Is this client a good case study candidate? Criteria: strong results achieved, visually interesting industry, client was engaged, results are measurable.
  • ☐ Send the case study request — ideally during the exit call or in the offboarding email. Frame it as an opportunity for the client to get recognition for their business growth.
"We achieved some really strong results together during our time working with [Business Name] — would you be open to featuring your story as a case study on our website? It would highlight your business's growth and we'd send you the finished piece to share on your own channels too."
  • ☐ If yes: Schedule a 20-minute phone or video call to gather the story. Use the Case Study Template.
  • ☐ Send for approval before publishing. Client approves all quotes and data points.
  • ☐ Publish and send the client the final link. Encourage them to share it — it benefits them too.

PHASE 8: GOOGLE / FACEBOOK REVIEW REQUEST

  • ☐ Request a review — ask during the exit call or in the final email. Make it easy: include a direct link.
"If you found value in our work together, we'd love it if you left us a Google review. It takes less than two minutes and genuinely makes a big difference to a small team like ours."
  • ☐ Log whether a review was left and respond to it publicly with gratitude.

PHASE 9: ALUMNI REFERRAL PROGRAM

  • ☐ Introduce the Nunya Bunya Alumni Referral Program during the offboarding.

How it works:

  • Any ex-client who refers a new paying client to Nunya Bunya receives a referral payment of $250 AUD (paid within 30 days of the referred client's first invoice being settled).
  • Referrals can be tracked via a unique referral link or code provided to the client at offboarding.
  • No cap on referrals. Ongoing for as long as the client wishes to participate.
  • ☐ Provide the referral link/code and explain how it works in the offboarding email.
  • ☐ Note the client as an "Alumni Referral Member" in CRM.

PHASE 10: RELATIONSHIP MAINTENANCE (Post-Departure)

A departed client is not a lost client — they are a future referral source and potential returning client.

Month 1 Post-Departure

  • ☐ Personal check-in email or LinkedIn message from the account manager. No agenda, no pitch. Just: "Hope the transition has gone smoothly. Thinking of you and the team — hope things are tracking well."

Month 3 Post-Departure

  • ☐ Value-add check-in. Share a relevant article, industry insight, or a quick note about something you noticed about their industry. Show you're still paying attention without being needy.

Month 6 Post-Departure

  • ☐ Strategic check-in. Ask how business is going. If there's an opening, explore whether their situation has changed and whether they'd be open to a conversation about working together again.
  • ☐ Invite to any Nunya Bunya events, webinars, or content — maintain them in the ecosystem.

Ongoing

  • ☐ Tag as "alumni" in CRM. Send company updates, case studies, and major announcements to alumni list.
  • ☐ Engage on LinkedIn — like and comment on their content.
  • ☐ Acknowledge business milestones — if they announce a new location, product launch, or hire, send a genuine congratulations.

OFFBOARDING PRINCIPLES (Team Reminder)

  1. Never burn a bridge. A departing client is a future referral, a case study, a returning client, or an advocate. Treat every departure as if the relationship is pausing, not ending.
  1. Make them feel like they mattered. Because they did. Be specific about what you enjoyed working on with them. Acknowledge their business, their team, their progress.
  1. Be efficient and thorough. No loose ends. Every access revoked, every file transferred, every invoice settled cleanly. This professionalism will be remembered.
  1. Don't ask why three times. If they don't want to say why they're leaving, accept it gracefully. Pushing for a reason when someone doesn't want to share it damages the relationship.
  1. Make them feel great about having chosen us. The last impression sets the tone for every conversation they'll have about us.
