DAY 7 -- May 7 (Wednesday)

PLATFORM: Facebook

TYPE: Value / Dog Care Tip

CAPTION:

"How much water does your dog actually need on a walk?

General rule: dogs need about 30ml of water per kg of body weight per day. On a warm Brisbane day, bump that up.

A 20kg dog (typical lab) needs roughly 600ml on a normal day. Add exercise and warmth, and that number climbs fast.

I carry fresh water on every walk. Not negotiable. Brisbane is subtropical -- dehydration sneaks up on dogs faster than most owners realise.

Signs your dog is dehydrated:

  • Dry gums
  • Excessive panting that does not slow down
  • Thick, ropy saliva
  • Lethargy after normal activity

Keep water available. Your dog will not ask for it until it is too late.

#DogHydration #BrisbaneDogCare #DogWalkingTips #PawesomeDogWalkers"

VISUAL: Photo of a dog drinking water, or Ben with a portable water bowl on a walk.

CTA: "Questions about dog care in Brisbane heat? Drop them below."

"Paralysis tick season in Brisbane runs roughly August to March, but they do not read calendars.

Even in May, keep your dog's tick prevention current. Inner-north Brisbane has plenty of bush corridors and garden areas where ticks hang out.

After every walk, I do a quick check: behind the ears, under the collar, between the toes, around the face, and under the front legs. These are the spots ticks love most.

If you are not already using tick prevention year-round in Brisbane, talk to your vet. It takes one tick to turn a normal day into an emergency.

#TickPrevention #BrisbaneDogHealth #DogCareTips #ParalysisTick"

May 15-21 | Brisbane-specific tips: heat, training, nutrition, paw care

DAY 15 -- May 15 (Thursday)

PLATFORM: Instagram

TYPE: Value / Dog Care Tip

CAPTION:

"Your dog's recall is not as good as you think it is.

I see it every day at off-leash parks: owners shouting their dog's name 15 times while their dog pretends to be deaf. The dog is not stubborn. The recall was never properly trained.

Three things that actually build recall:

  1. Start in a boring environment with zero distractions (your hallway, not the park)
  2. Use a consistent word -- and only use it when you can enforce it
  3. Make coming back to you more rewarding than whatever they are chasing

Good recall is a safety issue, not a party trick. Dogs with solid recall get more freedom, more off-leash time, and more fun.

#DogTrainingTips #RecallTraining #OffLeashReady #BrisbaneDogs"

"Why sniffing matters more than distance.

A lot of dog owners think a good walk means covering maximum ground. Speed. Distance. Steps.

Dogs disagree.

For your dog, a walk is information gathering. Every tree, post, and patch of grass tells them who has been there, what they ate, and how they were feeling. Sniffing is their version of reading the news.

A 20-minute sniff walk where your dog investigates at their own pace can be more mentally tiring than a 40-minute march.

I let dogs sniff. Not endlessly, but I do not drag them past everything interesting. The walk is for them, not for my step count.

TYPE: Value / Dog Care Tip

CAPTION:

"Leash reactivity is not aggression. It is frustration.

That dog lunging and barking at other dogs on leash? Nine times out of ten, they are frustrated, not aggressive. They want to go say hello but the leash stops them, and they do not know how to handle that feeling.

What helps:

  • Distance. Give reactive dogs more space, not less.
  • Redirect with treats before they hit their threshold.
  • Do not punish the barking. That makes the association with other dogs worse.
  • Work with a qualified trainer if it is escalating.

On walks, I manage group dynamics carefully. If a dog is leash-reactive, they get routes with more space and fewer surprise encounters. Every dog deserves a walk that does not stress them out.

#LeashReactivity #DogBehaviour #DogTrainingTips #BrisbaneDogs"

NOTES FOR BEN

  1. Shoot real content on walks. Every walk is a content opportunity. Quick phone photos of dogs, parks, and moments. Real beats polished every time.
