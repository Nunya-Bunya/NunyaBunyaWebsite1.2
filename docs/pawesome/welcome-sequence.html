Email 2: Why Dogs Need Regular Walks

Send: Day 2 (48 hours after signup)

Mautic email name: pw-welcome-02-dog-health

Subject: Is your dog getting enough exercise?

Alt subject: The walk your dog actually needs (hint: it's not just around the block)

Preview text: Brisbane heat, bored dogs, and what regular walks really do.

Body:

Hey {contactfield=firstname|there},

Quick question — does your dog ever do any of these?

  • Chew things they shouldn't
  • Bark at absolutely nothing
  • Get the zoomies at 9pm
  • Act like they haven't been outside in three years when you grab the leash

That's not bad behaviour. That's a dog who needs more time outside.

Here's what regular walks actually do for your dog:

Physical health. Dogs need 30-60 minutes of exercise daily, depending on breed. Without it, they're more likely to gain weight, develop joint issues, and have digestive problems. A proper walk (not just a lap of the block) gets their heart rate up and keeps them in shape.

Mental stimulation. Sniffing new smells, meeting other dogs, exploring different routes — this is brain food for your pup. A tired dog is a happy dog, and mental exercise tires them out just as much as physical.

Behaviour. Most "problem behaviours" — chewing, barking, digging, anxiety — come from boredom and excess energy. Regular walks are the simplest fix.

Brisbane heat matters too. In our subtropical climate, timing is everything. We walk in the cooler morning hours (7-10am) and late afternoon (4-6pm) to keep paws safe on hot pavement and avoid heat stress. We always carry water and know where the shady routes are.

If your work schedule makes daily walks tricky, that's literally what we're here for. I walk dogs through New Farm Park, Newstead Park, and the river paths Monday to Friday — small groups, proper exercise, photos sent to your phone after.

Hey {contactfield=firstname|there},

A few people have asked how the whole thing works, so let me walk you through it (pun intended).

Step 1: Book online or text me

Pick a day that works on our booking page, or just text me on 0484 604 529. Whatever's easier.

Step 2: Free meet & greet

I come to your place for 15 minutes. Your dog sniffs me, I learn their name and quirks, you tell me anything I need to know (favourite treats, other dog friends, things they're scared of). No charge, no commitment.

Step 3: First walk

I pick your dog up, we head to the park with a small group (max 4 dogs), and we do a proper 45-minute walk. GPS tracked so you can see the route.

Step 4: Photos + update

After the walk, you get photos of your dog sent straight to your phone. Not stock photos — actual pics of your dog having the time of their life.

Step 5: Regular schedule

If you're happy, we set up a regular schedule. Most clients do 3-5 walks per week. You get priority scheduling and the same walker (me) every time.

Common questions I get:

"Do I need to give you a key?"

Totally up to you. Some clients leave a key, some leave the dog in the yard, some are home when I arrive. Whatever works for your situation.

"What about other dogs? Is mine safe?"

I keep groups small (max 4) and I match dogs by size and energy level. Every dog gets a meet & greet before joining a group. If your dog prefers solo walks, we do that too.

"What if it rains?"

We still walk — dogs don't melt! But if there's a storm or extreme weather, I'll text you and we'll reschedule. Safety always comes first.

"What if I need to cancel?"

No worries. Just let me know by 6pm the night before and there's no charge. Life happens.
