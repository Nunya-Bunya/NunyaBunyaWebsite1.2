Executive Summary

Bella Rhyder is a Brisbane-based e-commerce fashion and lifestyle brand built for the modern active woman. Founded on the belief that movement is a way of life — not just a gym session — Bella Rhyder offers premium activewear, swimwear, bags, hats, and accessories designed to carry women seamlessly from morning training to afternoon coffee, from surf to street, without compromise.

Business Model: Direct-to-consumer e-commerce platform with brick-and-mortar expansion goals.

The brand's guiding promise: Move Boldly. Live Freely.

Bella Rhyder launches via direct-to-consumer e-commerce in June 2026, serving Australian women aged 22–45 who prioritise quality, aesthetics, and an active outdoor lifestyle. The business targets $500,000 in annual recurring revenue by the end of Year 3 through a staged growth strategy: e-commerce scale, wholesale boutique expansion in Year 2, and a Brisbane flagship retail presence in Year 3.

Australia's women's activewear market is growing at 7–9% annually and is estimated at over $2 billion AUD. Bella Rhyder is positioned to capture a meaningful share through a differentiated brand identity, influencer-led marketing, and a curated product range that emphasises versatility, quality fabrics, and authentic Queensland lifestyle aesthetics.

Financial Targets:

  • Year 1 (2026–27): $120,000 revenue (E-commerce focus)
  • Year 2 (2027–28): $280,000 revenue (Wholesale expansion)
  • Year 3 (2028–29): $500,000 ARR (Brick-and-mortar launch)

Funding: Bootstrapped / self-funded launch with revenue reinvestment model.

Expansion Roadmap:

  • Phase 1 (2026): E-commerce platform optimization and market penetration
  • Phase 2 (2027): Wholesale partnerships with boutique retailers
  • Phase 3 (2028): Flagship brick-and-mortar store launch in Brisbane CBD
  • Phase 4 (2029+): Multi-location expansion and omnichannel integration

To design activewear and lifestyle products that empower women to show up boldly in every environment — from the gym to the beach, from the boardwalk to the café.

Vision

Core Values

1. Movement Without Limits

Everything Bella Rhyder makes is designed to move with the body — functional, unrestricted, and built for real life. Performance is non-negotiable.

2. Intentional Quality

Each product is crafted from considered fabrics and materials. We choose quality over quantity and design pieces that last beyond a single season.

3. Coastal Confidence

Rooted in Brisbane and the broader Queensland lifestyle, Bella Rhyder draws from sun, surf, and outdoor living. The brand celebrates confidence in the outdoors and authentic Australian identity.

4. Empowered Femininity

We design for strength and softness simultaneously. Bella Rhyder celebrates women in all their dimensions — athlete, adventurer, professional, mother, creator.

5. Sustainability-Conscious Growth

Bella Rhyder is committed to progressively improving the environmental profile of its products — recycled fabrics, reduced packaging waste — without compromising performance or value.

Market Analysis

The Australian Women's Activewear Market

Australia's activewear and athleisure market is valued at over AUD $2 billion and growing at approximately 7–9% per annum, driven by:

  • Post-pandemic health and wellness culture that has permanently shifted consumer behaviour
  • Growing female participation in gym, Pilates, yoga, running, and outdoor activity
  • Normalisation of activewear as everyday wear (athleisure as a lifestyle, not a trend)
  • Social media driving aspirational fitness culture and product discovery
  • Premium segment outperforming mass market as consumers trade up for quality and longevity
  • Queensland's year-round warm climate driving higher per-capita activewear and swimwear spend

The Queensland Advantage

Queensland is Australia's third-largest state by population (5.4 million) and home to Australia's most active outdoor lifestyle culture. Brisbane's rapid growth, the South East Queensland population corridor (Brisbane → Gold Coast → Sunshine Coast), and a climate suited to outdoor living year-round create a uniquely fertile market for a brand built on active, coastal living. No premium activewear brand authentically "owns" Queensland — this is Bella Rhyder's strategic position.

Target Customer Profile

The Bella Rhyder Woman:

  • Age: 22–45
  • Location: Queensland-centric (Brisbane, Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast), with national shipping reach
  • Lifestyle: Active, health-conscious, socially engaged, outdoor-oriented
  • Fitness activities: Gym, Pilates, yoga, running, swimming, beach, hiking, tennis
  • Shopping behaviour: Shops primarily online, discovers brands via Instagram and TikTok, values aesthetics as much as function, willing to invest in fewer, better-quality pieces
  • Income: $65,000–$130,000+ household
  • Values: Health, community, self-expression, travel, sustainability, quality

Business Model

Primary Model: Direct-to-Consumer E-Commerce

Bella Rhyder operates as a direct-to-consumer (DTC) e-commerce brand, selling exclusively through its owned Shopify website. This model maximises margin, maintains full brand control, and enables direct customer relationships from Day 1.

Platform: Shopify (e-commerce), integrated with Klaviyo (email), Judge.me (reviews), and Meta Commerce Manager (social shopping)

Headquarters: Brisbane, Queensland, Australia

Fulfilment: Brisbane-based (in-house Year 1, 3PL evaluation Year 2)

Shipping: National via Australia Post and/or Sendle

Product Sourcing: Initial range sourced from quality Australian and international suppliers with private label branding; custom manufacturing progression in Years 2–3

Revenue Streams

  • Product sales (primary): activewear, swimwear, bags, hats, accessories
  • Curated bundles and sets (higher average order value)
  • Seasonal limited-edition drops (urgency and repeat traffic)
  • Wholesale boutique partnerships (Phase 2)

Phase Overview

Phase 1 — E-Commerce Launch (Year 1: June 2026–May 2027)

Launch DTC Shopify store, establish brand on Instagram and TikTok, build email list, sell initial product range nationally. Focus: brand awareness, customer acquisition, first repeat purchase cycle.

Phase 2 — Wholesale & Boutique Expansion (Year 2: 2027–28)

Approach curated Brisbane, Gold Coast, and Sunshine Coast boutiques with a wholesale offer. Target 10–20 stockist partners. Pop-up retail at markets, fitness events, and surf festivals.

Phase 3 — Flagship Consideration (Year 3: 2028–29)

Evaluate viability of a Brisbane flagship retail store (New Farm, Fortitude Valley, or James Street precinct) as a brand experience hub. Serves brand building, photography, and community events.

Launch Strategy

Phase 1: E-Commerce Launch (June 2026)

Pre-Launch (April–May 2026)

  • Build Instagram and TikTok presence with teaser content 6 weeks before launch
  • Collect email waitlist via landing page with launch-day early access incentive (10% off first order)
  • Seed product with 15–25 Brisbane-based micro-influencers for launch content
  • Complete website, photography, and copywriting
  • Set up Klaviyo welcome sequence and launch-day email campaign

Launch Day (June 1, 2026)

  • Coordinated social media launch across Instagram and TikTok
  • Launch-day email to waitlist: early access + 10% off, free shipping first 24 hours
  • Influencer launch content goes live simultaneously
  • Press release to Australian lifestyle and fashion media

Post-Launch (June–December 2026)

  • Weekly content cadence established
  • First customer UGC campaign initiated immediately
  • Meta paid social ads activated once first sales data validates creative
  • Email nurture sequences built for repeat purchase and referral

Phase 2: Wholesale (2027)

  • Identify 30 target boutiques across QLD and NSW
  • Develop wholesale lookbook and terms sheet
  • Attend trade shows (Life Instyle, Reed Gift Fairs)
  • Target: 10–15 confirmed stockists by end of Year 2

Phase 3: Flagship (2028)

  • Evaluate Brisbane inner-suburb retail opportunity
  • Potential location: James Street, Teneriffe, or New Farm
  • Store concept: brand experience hub with community events and photography studio

  • Activated Month 3–4 once organic has validated creative performance
  • Year 1 budget: $8,000 (concentrated Q3–Q4)

Email Marketing (Klaviyo)

  • Welcome series: 5 emails over 14 days (brand story → hero products → community → reviews → offer)
  • Post-purchase: 3-email series (thank you, style tips, review request)
  • Abandoned cart: 3-email sequence (1 hour, 24 hours, 72 hours)
  • Campaigns: Weekly during launch, bi-weekly ongoing
  • Target open rate: 35%+ | Click rate: 4%+
