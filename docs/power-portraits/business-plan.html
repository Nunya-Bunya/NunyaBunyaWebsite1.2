1. Executive Summary

Power Portraits is a location-based portrait photography studio operating across Brisbane and South East Queensland, founded by Ben Alek Conner. We exist to serve creative professionals — actors, musicians, solopreneurs, and corporate teams — who understand that a portrait is not a photograph but a declaration; that the image which precedes you into every room, every inbox, every casting call, is doing work on your behalf whether you've considered it or not.

The Brisbane portrait market is crowded at its extremes: budget operators cycling clients through factory-lit sessions on one end, and agency-facing commercial studios charging rates that exclude independent creatives on the other. Power Portraits occupies the deliberate middle — premium in execution, accessible in pricing, and personal in a way that neither extreme can replicate.

We offer three individual tiers and a corporate programme, ranging from $350 to $5,000+. Our blended average sits at approximately $530 per individual session and $3,500 per corporate engagement. Year 1 revenue is projected between $90,000 and $123,000 from 150–200 sessions, scaling to $280,000–$320,000 by Year 3 as referral networks mature and corporate accounts compound.

The business operates as a sole trader under ABN 48 890 965 530, with no fixed studio — an intentional choice. Brisbane's architecture, light, and landscapes are the studio. Our infrastructure runs on self-hosted open-source tools (Twenty CRM, Cal.com, Mautic, Matomo) to minimise recurring SaaS costs and maintain full ownership of client data.

Break-even point: 4 sessions per month.

2. Business Overview

2.1 Mission

To give creative professionals in South East Queensland portraits that carry the weight of their ambition — images so precise, so deliberate, that they become inseparable from the person they depict.

2.2 Vision

To build Brisbane's most respected portrait studio for people whose work demands that their image be as considered as their craft; and, in time, to establish Power Portraits as a name synonymous with portraiture that serves ambition rather than vanity.

2.3 Values

Intentionality over Volume. We do not chase throughput. Every session is architected around the individual — their industry, their aspirations, the specific rooms they need their portrait to enter. We would rather photograph four people brilliantly than forty people adequately.

Craft as Discipline. The camera is the instrument; the portrait is the composition. We invest continuously in technique, in lighting knowledge, in the study of how human faces communicate authority, warmth, and presence. The learning never concludes.

Authenticity Without Apology. We capture who you are at your sharpest — not a sanitised corporate fiction, not a filtered fantasy, but the version of you that makes people pay attention. Retouching serves the subject; it never replaces them.

Relationship Before Revenue. Clients are collaborators. The best portrait emerges from trust — from a session where the subject feels seen, not processed. Repeat bookings and referrals are the natural consequence of this philosophy, not the goal that distorts it.

Sovereignty of Tools. Where possible, we self-host our business infrastructure. Client data belongs to us and our clients, not to a SaaS vendor's retention algorithm. This is a quiet commitment, but a firm one.

3. Market Analysis

3.1 The Brisbane Portrait Market

Brisbane's population exceeds 2.6 million, with South East Queensland approaching 3.8 million. The city's creative and professional economy has grown substantially in recent years, driven by infrastructure investment, interstate migration, and the 2032 Olympic Games pipeline. LinkedIn adoption among Australian professionals continues to climb — and with it, the demand for professional imagery that performs in digital contexts.

The portrait photography market in Brisbane can be segmented into four broad categories:

SegmentTypical PricingCharacteristics
Budget Operators$80–$200Mini-sessions, natural light only, minimal retouching, high volume
Mid-Range Generalists$200–$500Wedding/family photographers who offer headshots as a secondary service
Premium Specialists$400–$1,000Dedicated portrait/headshot studios with refined technique and branding
Commercial Studios$1,500–$5,000+Agency-facing, serving advertising and editorial clients exclusively

Power Portraits operates firmly in the premium specialist tier, with corporate offerings that compete at the lower end of commercial studio pricing.

3.2 The Creative Professional Segment

Our primary market — creative professionals — is underserved in Brisbane. Actors need headshots that satisfy agency standards; musicians need press imagery that communicates genre and sensibility; solopreneurs need portraits that position them as authorities in markets where trust is built visually before it is built conversationally.

These clients share several characteristics:

  • Image-literate. They understand visual communication intuitively and can articulate what they need — or at least recognise it when they see it.
  • Budget-conscious but quality-driven. They will invest in quality work, but they need to see the value clearly. They are not corporate clients expensing a headshot; they are spending their own money.
  • Repeat customers by nature. Actors update headshots annually. Musicians release new work. Solopreneurs rebrand, launch new offerings, enter new markets. The relationship, once established, recurs.
  • Referral-rich. Creatives exist in tight professional networks. One strong portrait circulates — through Instagram, through group chats, through casting directors passing names to agents.

Key trigger events: New role or representation, album or project launch, business launch or rebrand, website overhaul, speaking engagement, award nomination, seasonal portfolio refresh.

3.3 The Corporate Segment

Secondary market. HR managers and marketing directors at Brisbane businesses who need consistent, elevated team photography — typically for website "About" pages, annual reports, LinkedIn profiles, and internal communications.

Corporate clients are high-value ($2,500–$5,000+ per engagement), lower-frequency, and won through relationship-building rather than advertising. The decision-maker is rarely the subject; the pitch is operational efficiency and brand consistency, not personal transformation.

3.4 Competitive Landscape

More intentional than the generalists who happen to offer headshots alongside weddings, newborns, and pet photography. Our entire brand, workflow, and expertise is built around portraiture. There is no dilution.

More accessible than commercial studios that serve advertising agencies and require five-figure budgets. A working actor or independent musician can afford our services without compromising on quality.

More refined than "natural light outdoor" operators who conflate casualness with authenticity. We use light — natural and otherwise — with precision. The difference between a snapshot and a portrait is not the camera; it is the intention behind every decision.

More personal than high-volume production lines that process subjects at fifteen-minute intervals. Our shortest session is one hour. We learn names; we study faces; we direct with care. The result is not a headshot — it is a portrait that knows something about the person in it.

The Portrait serves those willing to invest more time and receive a meaningfully larger, more versatile collection. 5. Marketing & Growth Strategy

5.1 Channels

Instagram — Primary B2C Channel

Instagram is where our work lives. Portraits are inherently visual; the platform is the portfolio. Every post is a proof of concept — potential clients see themselves in the subjects we've already photographed.

  • Content: Final portraits (with client permission), behind-the-scenes process shots, before/after retouching sequences, Brisbane location showcases
  • Frequency: 4–5 posts per week; daily Stories during active shoot weeks
  • Hashtag strategy: Local (#BrisbanePhotographer, #BrisbaneHeadshots, #SEQCreatives) and niche (#ActorHeadshots, #MusicianPortraits, #PersonalBrandPhotography)
  • Growth tactic: Collaborate with local actors, musicians, and creatives for styled portfolio-building sessions; cross-tag and co-promote

LinkedIn — Primary B2B Channel

LinkedIn is where corporate decision-makers and senior professionals evaluate whether we are credible enough to photograph their team or their own face. The tone shifts slightly — still literary, but grounded in professional outcomes.

  • Content: Thought pieces on professional image and its commercial impact, corporate case studies (anonymised where necessary), client transformations, industry commentary on visual branding
  • Frequency: 2–3 posts per week
  • Growth tactic: Connect with Brisbane HR managers, marketing directors, and business owners. Comment thoughtfully on their content before pitching. Build familiarity before requesting anything.

Google Business Profile

The unromantic but essential channel. When someone searches "headshot photographer Brisbane," we need to appear — and the reviews need to do the selling.

  • Priority actions: Claim and optimise profile, upload high-quality portfolio samples, request reviews from every satisfied client, post weekly updates
  • Target: 50+ five-star reviews by end of Year 1

Referral Network

The most powerful channel, but the slowest to build. Referrals convert at higher rates, arrive with trust already established, and cost nothing to acquire.

  • Actor/musician referrals: Offer a $50 credit per successful referral. Creatives talk; make the incentive worth mentioning.
  • Corporate referrals: Offer a complimentary session (for the referring contact personally) when they introduce a corporate engagement that books.
  • Partner referrals: Build relationships with complementary service providers — hairstylists, makeup artists, personal stylists, career coaches, acting coaches, talent agents. Reciprocal referral arrangements.

5.2 Content Pillars

All marketing content orbits five themes:

  1. The Work — Finished portraits. The product speaks. Let it.
  2. The Process — Behind the scenes, lighting setups, location scouting, retouching decisions. Demonstrates craft and builds trust.
  3. The Transformation — Before and after. Not in a makeover sense; in a "this is who you were presenting versus who you actually are" sense.
  4. The Perspective — Essays and commentary on visual identity, professional image, the psychology of first impressions. Establishes intellectual authority.
  5. The City — Brisbane as backdrop. Celebrating the locations, the light, the architecture. Positions Power Portraits as deeply local, not a faceless online operator.

5.3 Launch Strategy

  • Months 1–2: Portfolio-building phase. Offer 10–15 complimentary or heavily discounted sessions to targeted creatives (actors with representation, musicians with upcoming releases, solopreneurs with active audiences). These become the foundational portfolio and the first wave of testimonials.
  • Month 3: Full launch. Website live, booking system active, Google Business Profile optimised, Instagram and LinkedIn cadence established.
  • Ongoing: Reinvest 10–15% of revenue into marketing (primarily targeted Instagram ads and Google Ads for "headshot photographer Brisbane" and related terms).

6. Operations

6.1 Client Journey

The client experience is designed to feel considered at every stage — from first enquiry to final delivery.

Enquiry → Discovery Call (15 min) → Booking & Pre-Session Brief
    → Style Consultation (Portrait/Editorial only) → Session Day
        → Culling & Selection → Retouching → Gallery Delivery
            → Follow-Up & Review Request → Referral Invitation

Detailed stages:

  1. Enquiry. Client contacts via website form, Instagram DM, or direct email. Response within 4 business hours.
  2. Discovery Call. 15-minute call (or detailed email exchange) to understand the client's needs, industry, and how the portraits will be used. This is where we listen — not pitch.
  3. Booking. Client selects a package via Cal.com. 50% deposit secures the date. Automated confirmation email via Mautic includes the pre-session brief questionnaire.
  4. Pre-Session Brief. Client completes a brief covering their industry, intended use of images, wardrobe preferences, and any specific requests. For Portrait and Editorial clients, this informs the style consultation.
  5. Style Consultation. (Portrait and Editorial only.) A dedicated conversation — by phone or video — about wardrobe, colour palette, mood, and location selection. This is not optional; it is what separates our work from everyone else's.
  6. Session Day. On-location. Directed with care and specificity. Power Coaching (Editorial only) addresses posture, expression, and physical presence before the camera is raised.
  7. Culling & Selection. Within 3 business days, a proof gallery is delivered. Client selects their final images from the curated set.
  8. Retouching. Professional retouching completed within 5 business days of selection. Standards detailed below.
  9. Gallery Delivery. Final images delivered via Pixieset gallery — beautifully presented, downloadable in high resolution.
  10. Follow-Up. 48 hours after delivery: personal thank-you message, review request, referral invitation.

6.2 Retouching Standards

Retouching at Power Portraits follows a philosophy of precision without fabrication. The goal is to present the subject at their genuine best — not to reconstruct their face.

Standard retouching includes:

  • Skin smoothing (frequency separation technique; preserves texture)
  • Blemish and temporary mark removal
  • Under-eye correction (reduce, not eliminate — humanity matters)
  • Stray hair cleanup
  • Colour grading to brand-consistent tones
  • Background refinement (distracting elements softened or removed)
  • Subtle dodge and burn for dimensional lighting

Retouching does not include:

  • Reshaping of facial features
  • Significant body modification
  • Skin texture removal ("plastic" look)
  • Heavy colour manipulation that misrepresents the subject's complexion

7.4 Pricing Strategy

The three-tier individual pricing is structured around a principle borrowed from restaurant wine lists: the middle option should feel like the natural choice.

  • The Session ($350) anchors the entry point high enough to signal premium quality, while remaining accessible to working actors and early-stage solopreneurs.
  • The Portrait ($650) is the sweet spot — and the package we expect the majority of individual clients to book. The inclusion of style consultation and a substantially larger image set makes the step up from The Session feel obvious.
  • The Editorial ($950) serves clients whose needs are comprehensive. Power Coaching and the strategy call position this as a consultative experience, not merely a longer shoot.
  • Corporate ($2,500–$5,000+) is quoted per engagement. The per-person cost at scale is competitive with mid-range studios, but the consistency, direction, and retouching quality are materially superior.

No discounting. Referral credits and portfolio-building collaborations are the only exceptions. The price communicates the value; reducing it communicates doubt.

8.2 Year 2 — Scale

Year 2 introduces the first structural expansion: a second shooter and a concerted push into corporate accounts.

Key initiatives:

  • Second Shooter. Hire a contract photographer for corporate sessions and overflow individual bookings. This is not delegation of the creative vision — it is capacity expansion under direct supervision. The second shooter handles corporate team sessions (where volume and consistency matter more than singular artistic direction), freeing Ben to focus on individual portrait work where the personal touch is the product.
  • 5 Recurring Corporate Accounts. Target companies that need quarterly or biannual team photography — law firms, consultancies, tech companies with growing headcounts. Retainer-style arrangements at preferred rates.
  • Workshop Launch. A quarterly half-day workshop for solopreneurs: "The Power Portrait Masterclass" — covering posture, expression, wardrobe, and how to get better results from any photographer. $297 per attendee, 15 seats. Revenue diversification and lead generation in one.
  • Price Review. Evaluate whether Year 1 demand supports a 10–15% increase on individual packages. If the waiting list exists, the market is telling you the price is too low.

Year 2 targets:

  • 250–300 individual sessions
  • 15–20 corporate engagements
  • $175,000–$220,000 revenue

8.3 Year 3 — Refine

Year 3 is about margin, not just revenue. The premium solo model — fewer sessions, higher value, deeper client relationships.

Key initiatives:

  • Premium Positioning. Raise individual pricing by 15–20%. The portfolio, the reviews, and the referral network now justify it. Clients at this stage are booking Power Portraits specifically, not "a headshot photographer in Brisbane."
  • Editorial Partnerships. Pursue relationships with Brisbane magazines, creative agencies, and production companies. Provide portraits for editorial features in exchange for credit and exposure. This is brand positioning, not revenue — though the corporate enquiries it generates will be.
  • Corporate Programme Expansion. 20–25 engagements annually. Consider a dedicated corporate sales process — proposal templates, case study decks, quarterly check-in calls with existing accounts.
  • Studio Consideration. Evaluate whether demand and margins justify a permanent studio space. The location-based model has served well, but a studio offers weather independence, equipment permanence, and a tangible brand home. This is a Year 3 evaluation, not a Year 3 commitment.

Year 3 targets:

  • 300–350 individual sessions
  • 20–25 corporate engagements
  • $280,000–$320,000 revenue
  • Gross margin above 65%
