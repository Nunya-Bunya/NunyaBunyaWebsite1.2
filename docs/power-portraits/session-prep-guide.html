Session Preparation Guide

Power Portraits

Your session is approaching. Here is everything you need to arrive prepared — and nothing you don't. Read this once now, and once more the night before. The rest will take care of itself.

What to Wear

Clothing is the variable that shifts a portrait from good to remarkable. A few guiding principles:

  • Bring 1–3 outfits depending on your package. Session clients: 1–2 looks. Portrait clients: 3. Editorial clients: as many as you like — we will curate together.
  • Solid colours photograph best, especially deep tones — navy, charcoal, burgundy, black, forest green, cream. These hold the eye where it belongs: on your face.
  • Avoid busy patterns, large logos, and neon colours. They compete with you, and you should always win.
  • Textures are welcome. Linen, leather, denim, knits, velvet — these catch light in interesting ways and give a portrait dimension.
  • Accessories tell stories. Glasses, jewellery, hats, instruments, tools of your trade, anything that says something true about who you are — bring it.
  • Iron or steam everything. Wrinkles read louder on camera than they do in person. The lens is unforgiving about the things you would rather it forgot.
  • Bring options. More than you think you need. We will choose together on the day, and having a backup takes the pressure off entirely.

Grooming & Skin

The days before your session matter more than the morning of. None of this is complicated.

  • If you have a regular grooming routine, maintain it. Now is not the time to try something dramatically new.
  • Haircuts or colour: at least 5 days before the session, so it has time to settle into something that looks like it belongs to you.
  • Skincare: hydrate well in the days leading up. Drink water like it is your job. Avoid new products that might cause reactions — your skin does not need surprises this week.
  • Shaving: if you shave, do it the morning of. If you don't, don't start now. Both are excellent choices.
  • Nails: if your hands will be in frame — and they often are — they should look intentional. Clean and considered is all that is needed.
  • Hair & makeup: we can arrange a partnered MUA (from $150). Let us know at least 5 days prior and we will handle the rest.

On the Day

  • Arrive as close to on-time as you can. We have planned the light around your session time, and light does not wait for anyone.
  • Bring water. Bring your outfits on hangers. Bring yourself.
  • If you are running late, text Ben directly at [PHONE NUMBER]. No stress — just let us know.
  • We will start with a conversation, not a camera. The first 10–15 minutes are about settling in, talking through the plan, and letting the nervous energy dissolve into something useful.
  • You do not need to know how to pose. That is entirely our job. We direct everything — where to stand, where to look, what to do with your hands, how to hold your chin. You will never be left wondering what comes next.
  • If something feels wrong — an angle, a pose, a location — say so. This is a collaboration, not a performance. Your comfort is not a secondary concern; it is the primary one.

What to Expect

  • Sessions are relaxed, directed, and intentional. There is no rush. We have the time we need.
  • You will hear a lot of "hold that" and "beautiful" and "turn your chin slightly left." This is the rhythm of the thing.
  • We shoot in natural light and studio light depending on the mood we are building. Both are planned in advance based on what suits you and the story we are telling.
  • We may review a few frames together on the camera back during the session — partly to show you what is working, partly because it is genuinely exciting to watch a portrait come together in real time.
  • Music is welcome. If you have a playlist that puts you in the right headspace, bring it. The studio is yours for the duration.

Location

  • Your session location will be confirmed 48 hours before your shoot.
  • Outdoor sessions are weather-dependent. If conditions are genuinely bad, we will reschedule at no cost and no fuss. Good portraits require good light, and we would rather wait for it than fight against it.
  • For indoor and studio setups, we handle everything. Just bring yourself and your wardrobe.

After the Session

  • Relax. You did the hard part.
  • Your gallery will be delivered via Pixieset within [DELIVERY TIMELINE based on package].
  • You will receive a password-protected link to view, download, and share your images at full resolution.
  • Retouching feedback rounds are included in your package. We want you to love every frame — not most of them, every one.

A Note on Nerves

Almost everyone walks in nervous. It would be strange if you didn't — you are about to stand in front of a lens and be seen, deliberately, in a way most people spend their whole lives avoiding. That takes something.

But here is what happens, almost without exception: within fifteen minutes, the nerves quiet. The conversation finds its rhythm. You forget the camera is there, or at least you stop minding that it is. And by the end, most people ask when they can come back.

The camera is not a judge. It is a mirror, held by someone who knows how to find the best angle. Trust the process. We will take care of the rest.
