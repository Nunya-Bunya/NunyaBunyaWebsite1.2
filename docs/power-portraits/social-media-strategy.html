Power Portraits — Social Media Strategy

Last updated: 9 April 2026

Owner: Ben Conner

Brand voice: Literary, unhurried, quietly commanding. Elegant sentences, interesting word choice. Australian English.

Visual identity: Ink #0F0F0F / Parchment #F5F0E8 / Antique Brass #B08D57. Cormorant Garamond (display), Inter (body). Monochromatic colour blocking, directional light, minimal retouching, negative space.

1. Platform Strategy

Instagram (Primary — B2C)

PurposePortfolio showcase. Attract individual bookings from creative professionals across SEQ.
Frequency4–5 posts/week + daily Stories
AudienceActors, musicians, solopreneurs, freelance creatives in Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast, 22–50

Content mix:

  • Portrait posts — Full-bleed, no borders, no watermarks. One subject per image. Let the photograph carry itself.
  • Behind-the-scenes / process — Lighting setups, location scouting, tethered shooting, editing decisions. Show craft.
  • Client testimonials — Designed quote cards (Parchment background, Cormorant Garamond, Antique Brass accent line).
  • Reels (15–45 sec) — Session snippets, before/after reveals, lighting breakdowns, "one light, one subject" mini-studies.
  • Stories (daily) — Polls ("Which crop?"), BTS clips, session countdowns, reshares of client work.

Grid aesthetic: Dark and warm palette. Alternate between portrait posts and text/mood posts. The grid should feel curated but not rigid — no forced 3-column pattern. Let it breathe.

Hashtag strategy (15–20 per post, rotate sets):

  • Local: #brisbanephotographer #brisbaneheadshots #seqcreatives #brisbanecreatives #goldcoastphotographer
  • Niche: #actorheadshots #musicianportraits #editorialportrait #solopreneurbranding #personalbrandphotography
  • Broad: #portraitphotography #headshots #studiolight #portraitmood #naturallight

Engagement rules:

  • Respond to every comment and DM within 4 hours during business hours.
  • Follow and meaningfully engage with local creatives, acting schools (QUT, NIDA alumni), music venues, co-working spaces.
  • No automated engagement. No follow/unfollow.

LinkedIn (Primary — B2B)

PurposeAttract corporate clients. Build authority with HR directors, marketing managers, and founders who need team headshots.
Frequency3 posts/week
AudienceBrisbane-based business owners, HR/people & culture leads, marketing directors

Content:

  • Thought leadership — The commercial value of considered professional imagery. Why the cheapest headshot is the most expensive decision. How visual identity shapes perception before a word is spoken.
  • Corporate shoot case studies — Before/after of a team's visual presence. Process, logistics, outcomes.
  • Articles — Long-form posts on personal branding, the psychology of portraiture, what clients actually need from professional photos.

Tone: Still literary, still unhurried. Slightly more structured than Instagram. No corporate buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "thought leader"). Write like an essayist who happens to run a studio.

Google Business Profile

PurposeLocal SEO dominance. Reviews.
Target50+ reviews in Year 1
Frequency1 post/week (portfolio image + brief caption)

Actions:

  • Respond to every review — positive or negative — within 24 hours.
  • Upload 3–5 new photos monthly.
  • Keep hours, services, and booking link current.
  • Ask every client for a review at gallery delivery (include direct link in the delivery email).

TikTok (Secondary / Experimental)

PurposeReach younger creatives — emerging actors, musicians, content creators
Frequency2–3 posts/week
Audience18–30, Brisbane and broader AU

Content:

  • Short-form BTS (raw, unpolished is fine here — the contrast with the finished work is the point)
  • Transformation reveals (walking in vs. final portrait)
  • "How to pose" tips for non-models
  • Lighting breakdowns ("one window, one reflector, this is what you get")
  • "What I'd charge for this" — honest pricing transparency

Tone: Slightly more casual. Still never cheap, never gimmicky. The intelligence should still be visible. Think of it as a private studio tour, not a performance.

2. Content Pillars

Every piece of content falls into one of five pillars. This keeps the feed balanced and purposeful.

Pillar 1: The Work

Portfolio images. Full-bleed portraits with minimal captioning. Let the photograph do the talking.

Captions: Brief, evocative, about the subject — not about the camera settings. Write about the person, not the process.

"Mara came in saying she doesn't photograph well. She was wrong."

"Thirty seconds into the session, the performance stopped and the person arrived."

Platforms: Instagram (feed + Reels), LinkedIn (selectively), Google Business Profile, TikTok (as transformation reveals)

Pillar 2: Behind the Lens

Process, setup, lighting diagrams, location scouting, editing decisions. This pillar demonstrates craft and intentionality — it answers the question "why should I pay more for this?"

Example posts:

  • Photo of the lighting setup alongside the final image
  • Time-lapse of a culling session
  • Annotated before/after showing what retouching actually means (and what it doesn't)
  • "Why I chose this background colour" — a 60-second explanation of colour theory in practice

Platforms: Instagram (Reels + Stories), TikTok, LinkedIn (editing and selection process for corporate context)

Pillar 3: The Creative

Spotlight on the client's creative work. Tag them, celebrate their projects, link to their portfolio or upcoming show. This is the engine of community and referrals.

Example posts:

  • Portrait of an actor + mention of their upcoming production
  • Musician portrait + link to their new single
  • Solopreneur headshot + shout-out to their business launch

Why this matters: Every client becomes an ambassador. Their audience sees the portrait, wonders who took it, clicks through. Organic referral loop.

Platforms: Instagram (feed + Stories), LinkedIn (if client is a business professional)

Pillar 4: Perspective

Thought pieces on image, perception, personal branding, and the role of portraiture in a digital world. This is where the full brand voice lives — literary, considered, occasionally provocative.

Example posts:

  • "Your headshot is not a photograph. It is a first sentence."
  • "The case against the ring light" — why flattering light and good light are not the same thing
  • "What your LinkedIn photo is actually saying about you"
  • "Portraiture in the age of AI-generated faces" — why the real thing matters more than ever

Platforms: LinkedIn (primary — long-form), Instagram (carousel or single image with long caption)

Pillar 5: Invitation

Availability, session openings, seasonal offers, referral reminders. These are framed as invitations — warm, specific, with a clear reason to act now. Never desperate. Never "BOOK NOW 50% OFF!!!"

Example posts:

  • "I have two studio sessions open next Thursday. If you've been meaning to update your headshots before pilot season, this would be a good week."
  • "Referral note: if someone you've worked with needs portraits, I'll look after them — and I'll send you a print as a thank you."
  • "The winter light in the studio right now is extraordinary. I'm keeping a few afternoon slots open to make the most of it."

Platforms: Instagram (feed + Stories), LinkedIn (session availability for corporate), Google Business Profile

4. Engagement Strategy

Daily Actions (15–20 minutes)

  • Comment meaningfully on 10–15 local creative accounts (actors, musicians, Brisbane creatives, co-working spaces, creative agencies)
  • Respond to all comments on own posts
  • Respond to all DMs within 4 hours during business hours
  • Reshare any client posts that tag Power Portraits to Stories

Weekly Actions

  • Identify 2–3 potential collaboration partners (MUAs, stylists, creative directors, wardrobe stylists, acting coaches)
  • Engage with their content for a week before proposing anything
  • Share client work to Stories (with permission — ask at session or include in release form)

Monthly Actions

  • DM 5 past clients checking in — no sales pitch, genuine interest in their work
  • Review which posts drove the most profile visits and DMs — double down on those formats
  • One collaboration shoot with a local creative (MUA, stylist, model) to generate fresh portfolio content and cross-promote

Principles

  • No follow/unfollow tactics. Ever.
  • No engagement pods or comment groups.
  • No generic comments ("Great shot!" "Love this!"). Write something specific or don't write anything.
  • Build genuine relationships. The goal is to be a known and respected part of Brisbane's creative community, not to game an algorithm.

5. Paid Strategy

Monthly Budget: $300–$500

Split as conditions dictate, but starting allocation:

ChannelMonthly SpendObjective
Instagram (boosted posts)$150–$250Reach + profile visits
Google Ads (Search)$150–$250Direct bookings
LinkedIn$0 (organic only, Year 1)—
TikTok$0 (organic only)—

Instagram Paid

  • What to boost: Top-performing portfolio posts (highest save/share rate, not just likes). Boost posts that are already working organically.
  • Targeting: Brisbane + Gold Coast + Sunshine Coast. Age 25–45. Interests: acting, music, entrepreneurship, personal branding, photography, creative arts.
  • Duration: 5–7 days per boost. $30–$50 per post.
  • Goal: Profile visits and DM inquiries, not vanity reach.

Google Ads (Search)

  • Keywords: "Brisbane headshot photographer," "actor headshots Brisbane," "musician photos Brisbane," "professional portraits Brisbane," "corporate headshots Brisbane"
  • Landing page: Dedicated booking/enquiry page with portfolio samples, testimonials, and a clear call to action.
  • CPA target: Under $50 per enquiry. If CPA exceeds $75 consistently, pause and reassess keyword/ad copy.
  • Ad copy tone: Match brand voice. No "cheap headshots" or "affordable photos." Position on quality and craft.

Rules

  • Never boost a post that hasn't performed organically first.
  • Review ad performance weekly. Kill underperformers at the 7-day mark.
  • No paid LinkedIn until organic reach proves the audience is there (reassess at 6 months).

Quarterly Review

Every 3 months, review:

  1. Which content pillar drives the most enquiries? Increase its share.
  2. Which platform delivers the best cost-per-booking? Shift budget.
  3. Are we reaching the right audience? Check follower demographics against target.
  4. Is the brand voice consistent? Read back through the last 30 posts — does it sound like one person wrote them?

Tracking Setup

  • Website analytics: Matomo (self-hosted, via marketing stack). All social links use UTM parameters: ?utmsource=instagram&utmmedium=social&utm_campaign=portfolio
  • CRM: Twenty CRM. Every enquiry logged with source. Every booking tagged with acquisition channel.
  • Platform analytics: Instagram Insights, LinkedIn Analytics, TikTok Analytics, Google Business Profile Insights.
  • Review in: Metabase dashboard (consolidate key metrics weekly).

7. Visual Guidelines for Social

Portrait Posts (Instagram, LinkedIn, GBP)

  • Full-bleed. No borders, no frames, no watermarks.
  • Export at platform-native resolution (1080x1350 for Instagram portrait, 1080x1080 for square).
  • Colour-accurate. No Instagram filters. No presets that shift skin tones.
  • If the image needs a filter to look good, the image isn't good enough.

Quote / Text Posts

  • Background: Parchment #F5F0E8
  • Text: Ink #0F0F0F
  • Display font: Cormorant Garamond (for the quote or headline)
  • Body font: Inter (for attribution or secondary text)
  • Accent elements: Antique Brass #B08D57 (thin rules, decorative lines, pull-quote marks)
  • Keep text minimal. If it needs more than 30 words on the image, make it a carousel instead.

Stories

  • Dark backgrounds (Ink #0F0F0F or near-black from the shoot environment)
  • Minimal text overlays — Inter, white or Parchment
  • Antique Brass accent elements (thin lines, location tags, timestamps)
  • Use the "close friends" list for VIP clients to share exclusive BTS

Reels and TikTok

  • No flashy transitions. Clean cuts. Let the work carry it.
  • Audio: Ambient studio sounds, understated music. No trending audio that clashes with the brand.
  • Text overlays: Inter, clean, positioned with breathing room.
  • Thumbnail: Choose a frame that works as a still photograph. If the thumbnail doesn't look like a Power Portraits image, reshoot it.
  • Aspect ratio: 9:16 (1080x1920)

Carousels

  • First slide: Hook (Cormorant Garamond headline on Parchment or over a darkened portrait)
  • Interior slides: Mix of images and text. Keep text slides to 2–3 sentences max.
  • Final slide: Clear call to action ("Link in bio to book" or "DM to enquire"). Never aggressive.

Colour Accuracy

  • Calibrate monitor before any social export.
  • Export in sRGB colour space for all social platforms.
  • Never use filters that alter skin tones or colour temperature.
  • If a platform's compression degrades the image noticeably, adjust export sharpening to compensate.
