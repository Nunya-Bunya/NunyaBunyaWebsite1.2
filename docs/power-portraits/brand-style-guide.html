1. Brand Essence

Power Portraits exists at the intersection of portraiture and identity — where a single frame becomes the definitive image of who you are becoming. We don't take headshots. We architect the visual language of ambition.

Our clients are people in motion: actors between roles, musicians between records, solopreneurs between the life they have and the one they're building. They come to us because they understand that perception is not vanity — it is strategy.

Mission

To give creative professionals in South East Queensland portraits that carry the weight of their ambition — images so precise, so deliberate, that they become inseparable from the person they depict.

Brand Promise

Every portrait we deliver is a declaration, not a photograph.

2. Brand Voice

Personality

Power Portraits speaks like someone who has read deeply, observed carefully, and chosen every word on purpose. The voice is literary, unhurried, and quietly commanding — a beat poet who traded the coffeehouse for a darkroom and never looked back.

Voice Attributes

AttributeWhat it meansWhat it doesn't mean
ElegantCrafted sentences, deliberate word choice, rhythmic proseFlowery, overwrought, purple
ConfidentSpeaks with authority, never hedges, never over-explainsArrogant, dismissive, salesy
WarmInviting without being casual, respectful of the person behind the lensFolksy, overly familiar, exclamation marks
PreciseEvery word earns its place — nothing decorative, nothing wastedClinical, cold, stripped bare
EvocativeCreates atmosphere, suggests something larger than the frameVague, abstract, pretentious

Tone Examples

Yes:

"There is a version of you that exists in the minds of strangers — formed from a thumbnail, a profile image, a half-second glance. We make certain that version tells the truth."
"A portrait session with us is not a transaction. It is a conversation between light and intention, conducted in the span of an afternoon."
"You have spent years refining your craft. Your portrait should reflect the same discipline."

Never:

"No fluff, just great headshots!" (cheap, dismissive)
"We keep it real — no BS, just results." (juvenile, generic)
"Ready to level up your brand? Let's goooo!" (cringe, try-hard)
"Book now before spots fill up!!!" (desperate, manipulative)

Writing Rules

  1. Never use more than one exclamation mark per page. Prefer periods.
  2. Contractions are fine — "we don't" reads more naturally than "we do not."
  3. Avoid marketing cliches: "game-changer," "level up," "crush it," "no-brainer," "real talk."
  4. Use em dashes and semicolons freely — they create the rhythm of considered thought.
  5. First person plural ("we") for the studio. Second person ("you") for the client. Never third person ("Power Portraits offers...").
  6. Paragraphs should breathe. Short paragraphs. White space is punctuation.
  7. When in doubt, read the sentence aloud. If it sounds like an ad, rewrite it.

Colour Philosophy: The palette is drawn from the darkroom — deep blacks, warm paper tones, and the amber glow of a safelight. It should feel like holding a fine print in your hands: warm, tactile, considered. Gold is conspicuously absent. Antique Brass is earthier, quieter, more interesting.

4. Photography Direction

Style

Our own visual identity should reflect our craft. All marketing and brand imagery follows these principles:

  • Monochromatic colour blocking — inspired by Sela Shiloni's editorial approach. Subjects against controlled, single-colour environments. The background serves the subject, never competes.
  • Directional, sculpted light — hard light welcome. We are not afraid of shadow. Rembrandt, split, and butterfly lighting patterns dominate.
  • Minimal retouching — skin texture is preserved. We correct light and colour; we do not manufacture faces.
  • Negative space — compositions breathe. The subject does not need to fill the frame to command it.
  • Eye contact or deliberate aversion — every gaze is intentional.

What We Don't Show

  • Overly processed, airbrushed skin
  • Ring light flatness
  • Busy, distracting backgrounds
  • Forced smiles or stiff posing
  • Stock photography aesthetic

5. Logo & Wordmark

Primary Wordmark

POWER PORTRAITS set in Cormorant Garamond SemiBold (600), all caps, with 0.15em letter spacing.

Below the wordmark, a thin horizontal rule (1px, Antique Brass) followed by:

PHOTOGRAPHY set in Inter Medium (500), all caps, 0.25em letter spacing, 60% the size of the wordmark.

Lockup

P O W E R   P O R T R A I T S
————————————————————————————————
        P H O T O G R A P H Y

Usage Rules

  • Minimum clear space: the height of the "P" on all sides
  • On dark backgrounds: wordmark in Parchment (#F5F0E8), rule in Antique Brass
  • On light backgrounds: wordmark in Ink (#0F0F0F), rule in Antique Brass
  • Never place the wordmark over busy imagery
  • Never rotate, stretch, or add effects to the wordmark
  • The wordmark is always horizontal — never stacked vertically

6. UI & Web Design Principles

Layout

  • Grid: 12-column, max-width 1200px, generous gutters (24–32px)
  • Whitespace is non-negotiable. Sections breathe. Content does not crowd.
  • Photography-forward: Images are large, high-resolution, and given prominence. Text supports; it does not compete.
  • Scroll-driven narrative: Pages tell a story from top to bottom. Each section is a chapter.

Buttons & CTAs

  • Primary CTA: Antique Brass background, Ink text, Cormorant Garamond Medium, uppercase, 0.1em tracking
  • Secondary CTA: Transparent with 1px Antique Brass border, Antique Brass text
  • Hover: subtle brightness shift (+-5%), no dramatic colour changes
  • No rounded corners — use sharp rectangles. Softness is not our language.

Cards & Containers

  • Background: Bone or Parchment
  • Border: none, or 1px Ash on hover
  • Shadow: minimal — 0 2px 8px rgba(15,15,15,0.06)
  • Padding: generous (32–48px)

Navigation

  • Fixed top nav, Ink background, Parchment text
  • Wordmark left-aligned, navigation links right-aligned
  • Links in Inter Medium, uppercase, small (13px), wide tracking
  • Active state: Antique Brass underline (2px)

Responsive

  • Mobile: single column, stacked layout
  • Tablet: 2-column grid
  • Desktop: full 12-column grid
  • Breakpoints: 768px (tablet), 1024px (desktop)

7. Brand Applications

Business Documents

All documents (proposals, invoices, agreements) follow a consistent format:

  • Header: Ink background, full-width, wordmark in Parchment, Antique Brass rule below
  • Body: Bone background, Graphite text in Inter
  • Headings: Cormorant Garamond, Ink
  • Accent elements: Antique Brass (rules, highlights, pull quotes)
  • Footer: Minimal — studio name, ABN, contact details in Ash

Email

  • Plain text preferred for personal correspondence
  • HTML templates use the same header treatment as documents
  • Signature: wordmark + one-line contact + thin Antique Brass rule

Social Media

  • Maintain the dark/warm palette across all platforms
  • Typography overlays use Cormorant Garamond on Ink backgrounds
  • Portrait posts: full-bleed, no borders, no watermarks
  • Quote/text posts: Parchment background, Ink text, Antique Brass accents
  • Never use filters that compromise colour accuracy

8. Target Audience

Primary: Creative Professionals

Working actors, musicians, solo entrepreneurs, freelance creatives, and independent artists in South East Queensland who need portraits that match the seriousness of their ambition.

They are:

  • Building careers, not hobbies
  • Visually literate — they know the difference between a good photo and the right photo
  • Active on platforms where image matters (casting sites, Spotify, LinkedIn, Instagram, personal sites)
  • Willing to invest in quality because they understand ROI on perception

They are not:

  • Price-shopping for the cheapest headshot
  • Looking for family portraits or event coverage
  • Interested in gimmicks, props, or novelty

Secondary: Corporate Teams

Small-to-medium businesses in Brisbane/Gold Coast who want cohesive, elevated team photography — not the fluorescent-lit, grey-backdrop corporate standard.

9. Competitive Positioning

Power Portraits sits above the mid-market headshot photographers and below the fashion/editorial studios that don't serve individuals. We are:

  • More intentional than a generalist photographer who also does headshots
  • More accessible than a commercial studio that only works with agencies
  • More refined than the "natural light, outdoor shoot" operators
  • More personal than a high-volume studio that processes clients like a production line

Our edge is specificity: we serve creative professionals, we understand their world, and every decision we make — from lighting to delivery — reflects that understanding.

This document is the single source of truth for all Power Portraits brand decisions. Every piece of content, every template, every client-facing material must align with these standards. When in doubt, return here.
