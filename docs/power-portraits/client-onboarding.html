POWER PORTRAITS — CLIENT ONBOARDING FLOW

Internal Operations Document

Step-by-step playbook: first inquiry to 6-month re-engagement

How to Use This Document

This is the complete onboarding playbook for every Power Portraits client. Eight stages, every one mapped out. If you are reading this as Ben's assistant: follow these stages exactly. Every client goes through this flow, no exceptions. The stages are sequential — don't skip ahead, and don't let a client fall through a gap between stages.

Tools referenced throughout: Gmail, Twenty CRM, Cal.com, Xero, Mautic, Pixieset, Google Drive.

Templates referenced: BOOKING CONFIRMATION TEMPLATE.md, GALLERY DELIVERY TEMPLATE.md, SESSION PREP GUIDE.md, CLIENT INTAKE FORM.md.

Stage 1: Inquiry

What happens: A potential client reaches out. We respond quickly, warmly, and with enough information to move them toward a booking or a consultation call.

Channels:

  • Website contact form (powerportraits.com.au)
  • Instagram DM
  • LinkedIn message
  • Phone call
  • Email

Who does what:

  • Ben (or assistant): Responds to every inquiry. No inquiry goes unanswered.

Timing:

  • Response within 4 business hours. Same day wherever possible. If an inquiry comes in after hours, respond first thing the next business day.

Actions:

  1. Read the inquiry. Understand what they are looking for (headshots, team photos, personal branding, etc.).
  2. Respond using the Inquiry Response email template (see Email Templates folder). Personalise the opening — reference their name, their profession if mentioned, or where they found us.
  3. Include a brief overview of relevant packages (don't dump all four on them if they clearly need one).
  4. Suggest a 15-minute consultation call if they seem undecided, or send the booking link directly if they already know what they want.
  5. Log the lead in Twenty CRM:
  • Name, email, phone (if provided)
  • Source (Instagram, website, LinkedIn, phone, referral)
  • Package interest (if known)
  • Status: "Lead"

Tools: Gmail, Twenty CRM

Template: Email Templates -> Inquiry Response

Notes:

  • Warm tone, not salesy. We are confident in what we offer — no need to oversell.
  • If the inquiry is vague ("How much for photos?"), ask a question back: "I'd love to help — could you tell me a bit about what you're looking for and where you'll be using the images?"
  • If the inquiry comes via Instagram DM, keep it conversational but still professional. Move to email for booking logistics.

Stage 2: Consultation Call (Optional)

What happens: A short phone or video call to understand the client's goals, recommend the right package, and build rapport before they commit.

Who does what:

  • Ben: Runs the call. This is not delegated — the client is buying Ben's eye and direction, so Ben needs to be the voice they hear.

Timing:

  • 15 minutes. No longer unless the client has complex needs (e.g., large corporate booking).
  • Schedule via Cal.com — send the booking link in the inquiry response or follow-up email.

When to recommend a consultation call:

  • Portrait package ($450) and Editorial package ($800): Always recommend.
  • Session package ($250): Optional — only if the client seems unsure or nervous.
  • Corporate package ($2,500+): Always. These require scoping.

Talking points / discovery questions:

  1. "What do you do? Tell me about your work." (Understand their professional context.)
  2. "Where will these images live?" (LinkedIn, website, speaker profile, press kit, social media.)
  3. "Is there an occasion driving this?" (New role, rebrand, book launch, just overdue.)
  4. "How do you want to be perceived?" (Approachable, authoritative, creative, warm, serious.)
  5. "Have you had professional photos before? Anything you loved or hated about the experience?"
  6. "Do you have any references or examples of portraits you admire?" (Helps calibrate style.)

Actions after the call:

  1. Send a short email (within 30 minutes) summarising the recommended package and why it suits them.
  2. Include the Cal.com booking link for the session itself.
  3. Include the Xero invoice link or let them know the invoice is coming shortly.
  4. Update Twenty CRM status to "Consultation Complete".

Tools: Cal.com (scheduling), Phone or Zoom (call), Gmail (follow-up), Twenty CRM

Notes:

  • If the client doesn't want a call and just wants to book, skip this stage entirely. Not every client needs hand-holding.
  • Keep notes from the call in the CRM contact record — they will inform the shot list later.

Stage 3: Booking

What happens: The client selects their package, pays the deposit, and the session is locked in.

Who does what:

  • Ben (or assistant): Sends invoice, confirms booking, updates CRM, adds to calendar.

Timing:

  • Invoice sent within 30 minutes of the client confirming their package.
  • Session is not confirmed until the 50% deposit is received. Make this clear.

Actions:

  1. Generate invoice in Xero:
  • Line items: Package name, package price (inc. GST), 50% deposit due now, balance due on gallery delivery.
  • Payment terms: Due on receipt. Payment via bank transfer or card (Xero handles this).
  1. Send invoice to client with payment link.
  2. On receipt of deposit:
  • Send Booking Confirmation Email (see BOOKING CONFIRMATION TEMPLATE.md). This includes:
  • Session date, time, and meeting point
  • Package summary
  • What to expect next (intake form, prep guide)
  • Cancellation/rescheduling policy
  • Add session to Google Calendar with:
  • Client name, package, location(s), and any notes from consultation
  • 20-minute buffer before session start (Ben's setup time)
  • Schedule the pre-session automated emails in Mautic (intake form at 7 days, prep guide at 48 hours)
  1. Update Twenty CRM:
  • Status: "Booked"
  • Add session date, package, and deposit payment date

Tools: Xero (invoicing), Cal.com (session scheduling), Google Calendar, Mautic (email automation), Twenty CRM

Template: BOOKING CONFIRMATION TEMPLATE.md

Notes:

  • If the deposit is not received within 5 business days, send a gentle nudge: "Just checking in — did you have any questions about the booking?"
  • If no response after 10 business days, follow up once more, then mark as "Stalled" in CRM. Don't chase beyond that.

Stage 4: Pre-Session Preparation (7 Days Before)

What happens: We gather everything we need to tailor the session to this specific client, and we make sure the client arrives prepared and confident.

Who does what:

  • Mautic (automated): Sends the intake form email 7 days before the session.
  • Ben: Reviews intake form responses, prepares shot list and location plan.
  • Mautic (automated): Sends the Session Prep Guide 48 hours before.

Timing:

  • 7 days before session: Client Intake Form email sent automatically via Mautic.
  • 3-5 days before session: Ben reviews completed intake form and prepares.
  • 48 hours before session: Session Prep Guide email sent automatically via Mautic.

7 Days Before: Client Intake Form

The intake form (see CLIENT INTAKE FORM.md) is a questionnaire covering:

  • Professional context (role, industry, audience)
  • Image usage (LinkedIn, website, speaking, social, press)
  • Brand words (3 words describing how they want to be perceived)
  • Style preferences (indoor/outdoor, urban/natural, formal/relaxed)
  • Wardrobe plans (how many outfits, colours, formality)
  • Nerves or concerns about being photographed
  • Any specific shots or angles requested

Action: Mautic sends the intake form link. If the client hasn't completed it by 4 days before the session, send a manual reminder.

3-5 Days Before: Ben's Preparation

Once the intake form is in, Ben prepares:

  1. Location options: Based on the client's vibe and preferences. Scout if it's a new location. Confirm access/logistics.
  2. Lighting plan: Natural light vs. portable flash/reflector based on location, time of day, and desired mood.
  3. Shot list / mood direction: Not a rigid list, but a guide:
  • Key looks to capture (e.g., "confident wide shot against concrete", "warm close-up with natural light")
  • Posing notes based on their body type and comfort level
  • Expression direction (serious/authoritative vs. warm/approachable)

Save prep notes in Google Drive under the client's folder: Power Portraits / Clients / [LASTNAME, FIRSTNAME] / Session Prep.

48 Hours Before: Session Prep Guide

Mautic sends the Session Prep Guide (see SESSION PREP GUIDE.md), which covers:

  • What to wear and what to avoid
  • Skincare and grooming tips (day-of)
  • What to expect during the session
  • Meeting point and logistics
  • What to bring (outfits, accessories, water)
  • Arrival time (5-10 minutes early)

Tools: Mautic (automated emails), Google Drive (shot planning), Twenty CRM

Templates: CLIENT INTAKE FORM.md, SESSION PREP GUIDE.md

Notes:

  • If a client doesn't fill out the intake form at all, don't cancel. Call them the day before and run through the key questions verbally. Capture the answers yourself.
  • For Corporate bookings, the intake form is sent to the primary contact, not every individual being photographed.

Stage 5: Session Day

What happens: The shoot. This is where the work lands. Everything before this stage exists to make this stage effortless.

Who does what:

  • Ben: Runs the entire session. Arrival, setup, direction, shooting, wrap.

Timing:

  • Ben arrives 20 minutes early.
  • Session length depends on package:
  • Session ($250): 1 hour
  • Portrait ($450): 2 hours
  • Editorial ($800): 3 hours
  • Corporate ($2,500+): As scoped

Before the Client Arrives (20 Minutes)

  1. Arrive at location. Scout the light — where is it falling, where will it be in 30/60/90 minutes?
  2. Set up any equipment (portable lighting, reflectors, backdrop if studio).
  3. Take test shots. Dial in exposure, white balance, and composition for the hero spots.
  4. Review the client's intake form answers and prep notes one final time.
  5. Water/coffee ready if studio session.

Client Arrival

  1. Welcome: Warm, genuine greeting. First impressions set the tone for the whole session. If they are nervous, this is where you diffuse it.
  2. Walkthrough: Brief overview of the plan — locations, rough timing, when outfit changes will happen. No surprises.
  3. Check in: "How are you feeling about being in front of the camera today?" Address nerves directly. Normalise it: "Everyone feels this way. That's exactly why I'm here to direct you."
  4. Editorial package only — Power Coaching (30 minutes):
  • Posture check and correction
  • Breathing exercise (box breathing: 4 in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold)
  • Confidence anchoring: Have them recall a moment where they felt powerful and capable. That's the energy we are photographing.
  • Mirror work if studio: Show them their posture before and after adjustments

During the Session

  • Continuous direction. Never leave the client standing there wondering what to do. Every moment is directed: "Turn your shoulders toward me," "Drop your chin slightly," "Think about [thing from coaching]," "That's it — hold that."
  • Positive reinforcement. Regularly: "That's working really well," "Your energy just shifted — that's exactly what we want," "This light on you is incredible."
  • Review frames together. At natural milestones (outfit change, location move), show 2-3 strong frames on the camera back. Builds confidence and buy-in.
  • Wardrobe transitions. Signal clearly: "That outfit has given us everything — let's move to your next look." Allow time and privacy for changes.
  • Energy management. Monitor fatigue. If the client is flagging, take a breather. The final outfit/location should still have fresh energy.

Session Close

  1. "We've captured some really strong work today — I'm excited to show you what we've created."
  2. Confirm delivery timeline (see Stage 6 for specifics per package).
  3. Remind about balance payment: "You'll receive the final invoice with your gallery link."
  4. Thank them genuinely. "I appreciate you trusting me with this."

Tools: Camera, lighting, reflectors, portable backdrop (if applicable)

Notes:

  • Never show bad frames on the camera back. Only show frames where they look great.
  • If conditions change mid-session (weather, light), adapt without making the client feel like something went wrong. Just redirect: "The light just shifted — let's use that."
  • Take behind-the-scenes phone shots/video (with permission) for social media content.

Stage 6: Editing and Delivery

What happens: Cull, edit, retouch, export, deliver. The client receives their gallery and the balance invoice.

Who does what:

  • Ben: All editing, culling, retouching, and gallery setup.
  • Ben (or assistant): Sends delivery email and balance invoice.

Timing by package:

PackageDelivery Target
Session ($250)5-7 business days
Portrait ($450)5-7 business days
Editorial ($800)3-5 business days
Corporate ($2,500+)48-72 hours (same-day preview option if booked)

Editing Workflow

Day 1-2: Cull

  • Import all files to Lightroom.
  • First pass: reject obvious misses (eyes closed, motion blur, bad expression).
  • Second pass: flag selects. Aim for 2-3x the final delivery count as candidates.
  • Third pass: narrow to final delivery count.

Day 2-4: Lightroom Base Edit

  • Apply Power Portraits signature colour grade (consistent across all images).
  • Individual adjustments: exposure, white balance, contrast, crop.
  • Crop to primary aspect ratio based on client's stated usage.

Day 3-6: Photoshop Retouching

  • Blemish removal (temporary imperfections only).
  • Skin texture preserved — natural look, not plastic. Frequency separation where needed.
  • Flyaway hair cleanup.
  • Under-eye softening (not removal).
  • Background cleanup if distracting elements.
  • Teeth whitening only if noticeably off.

Export:

  • High-resolution: JPEG, 300 DPI, sRGB colour space. Full crop.
  • Web-optimised: JPEG, 72 DPI, sRGB. Long edge 2000px.
  • Aspect ratios: 1:1 (social profile), 4:5 (Instagram portrait), 2:3 (standard portrait), 16:9 (LinkedIn banner / website hero).
  • Not every image needs every ratio. Use judgement — headshots get 1:1 and 4:5. Environmental portraits get 2:3 and 16:9.

File naming convention:

LASTNAMEFIRSTNAMEYYYYMMDD_001.jpg

Example: SMITHSARAH20260415_001.jpg

Gallery Delivery

  1. Upload final images to Pixieset gallery.
  • Gallery name: [First Name] [Last Name] — Power Portraits [Month Year]
  • Password-protected (unique password per client).
  • Download enabled for high-res and web versions.
  • Gallery expiry: 90 days from upload. Communicated clearly in delivery email.
  1. Send Gallery Delivery Email (see GALLERY DELIVERY TEMPLATE.md):
  • Gallery link and password
  • Download instructions
  • Explanation of file types and sizes included
  • Reminder of 90-day expiry
  • Soft review request embedded
  1. Send balance invoice via Xero (remaining 50%).
  • If balance is outstanding, gallery password is provided but a note reminds them payment is due. Do not withhold the gallery — this was communicated at booking.
  1. Update Twenty CRM status to "Delivered".

Tools: Lightroom, Photoshop, Pixieset, Xero, Gmail, Twenty CRM

Template: GALLERY DELIVERY TEMPLATE.md

Stage 7: Follow-Up (7 Days After Delivery)

What happens: Check in with the client. Ask for feedback. Request a review. Mention the referral programme.

Who does what:

  • Ben (or assistant): Sends follow-up email.

Timing:

  • 7 days after gallery delivery. Not before — give them time to explore the images.

Actions:

  1. Send follow-up email using the Follow-Up / Review Request template:
  • "How are you going with your new images?"
  • "Have you had a chance to update your profiles?"
  • Ask for a Google review (include direct link). Keep it genuine: "A review means the world to a small business like mine."
  • Mention the referral programme: "If you know someone who could use a professional image update, I'd love the introduction. Referred clients receive 10% off their first session."
  1. If they respond positively, reply personally. If they share where they are using the images (LinkedIn post, new website), engage with it — comment, like, share to Stories.
  2. If they respond with concerns or issues, address them immediately and professionally. Offer a reshoot or additional edits if warranted.
  3. Update Twenty CRM status to "Complete".

Tools: Gmail, Twenty CRM

Template: Email Templates -> Follow-Up / Review Request

Notes:

  • Do not send more than one review request. If they don't leave one after this email, let it go. Do not chase.
  • If they do leave a review:
  • Respond on Google within 48 hours with a personalised reply.
  • Screenshot and post as an Instagram Story.
  • Add to the testimonials section of the website.

Stage 8: Re-Engagement (6 Months Later)

What happens: Automated email reaches out to past clients to invite them back.

Who does what:

  • Mautic (automated): Sends re-engagement email 6 months after gallery delivery.
  • Ben: Responds personally if the client replies.

Timing:

  • 6 months after gallery delivery. Set this up in Mautic at the time of delivery (or as part of the automated sequence triggered by CRM status change to "Delivered").

Actions:

  1. Mautic sends the re-engagement email:
  • Subject line: "It's been six months — has your world changed since we last shot together?"
  • Warm, low-pressure tone. Not pushy.
  • Acknowledge that a lot changes in six months — new role, new business direction, new chapter.
  • Offer 10% loyalty discount on their next session.
  • Include the booking link (Cal.com).
  1. If they reply or book:
  • Update CRM status to "Returning Client".
  • Apply the 10% discount in Xero.
  • Re-enter the flow at Stage 3 (Booking).
  1. If no response:
  • No further follow-up. They stay in the CRM as "Complete".
  • They may receive future re-engagement emails at 12 months (if a second sequence is set up), but do not bombard past clients.

Tools: Mautic (automated email sequence), Twenty CRM, Cal.com

Notes:

  • The 6-month mark is chosen because professional images have a natural shelf life — roles change, appearances change, brands evolve.
  • This is the most cost-effective marketing Power Portraits has. Returning clients don't need selling. They already trust us.

CRM Status Progression

Track every client through these statuses in Twenty CRM:

  1. Lead — Inquiry received, not yet booked
  2. Consultation Complete — Had a call, awaiting booking decision
  3. Booked — Deposit paid, session scheduled
  4. Delivered — Gallery sent, balance invoiced
  5. Complete — Follow-up done, review requested
  6. Returning Client — Re-engaged and booking again
  7. Stalled — Went cold during inquiry or booking stage

Checklist: Don't Miss These

For every single client, confirm:

  • ☐ Lead logged in Twenty CRM with source
  • ☐ Response sent within 4 business hours
  • ☐ Invoice sent within 30 minutes of package confirmation
  • ☐ Booking confirmation email sent on deposit receipt
  • ☐ Mautic automation triggered (intake form + prep guide)
  • ☐ Intake form reviewed and shot list prepared before session
  • ☐ Gallery uploaded to Pixieset with correct naming and 90-day expiry
  • ☐ Balance invoice sent with gallery delivery
  • ☐ Follow-up email sent 7 days after delivery
  • ☐ Google review requested (once only)
  • ☐ CRM status updated at every stage transition
  • ☐ Re-engagement email scheduled for 6 months
