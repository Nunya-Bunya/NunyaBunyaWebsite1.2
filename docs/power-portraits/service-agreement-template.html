3.2 Deposit

A 50% deposit is required to confirm the booking. Your session date is not secured until the deposit has been received.

The deposit is non-refundable within 48 hours of the session date. Outside that window, the deposit is fully refundable if the Client cancels.

3.3 Balance

The remaining balance is due upon delivery of the online gallery. Payment must be received before final high-resolution images are released.

3.4 Payment Methods

Payment may be made by:

  • Bank transfer (details provided on invoice)
  • Credit or debit card
  • PayPal

3.5 Late Payment

If the balance remains unpaid for more than 14 days after gallery delivery, a reminder will be issued. If payment is not received within 30 days of gallery delivery, Power Portraits reserves the right to suspend gallery access until the balance is settled.

4. Scheduling and Cancellation

Life happens. Here is how we handle changes:

4.1 Rescheduling by the Photographer

If the Photographer needs to reschedule for any reason, the Client will be offered a new date at no additional cost. If no suitable alternative can be found, the Client will receive a full refund including any deposit paid.

4.2 Lateness

If the Client arrives more than 30 minutes late without prior notice, the session may be shortened accordingly. The full fee remains payable.

5. Session Details

5.1 Arrival

The Photographer will arrive at the session location approximately 20 minutes before the scheduled start time to set up equipment and assess lighting conditions.

5.2 Duration

Session duration is as specified in Section 2 above and corresponds to the booked package. The Photographer will make every effort to use the full allocated time, but the session may conclude earlier if the shot list is complete and both parties are satisfied.

5.3 Direction

The Photographer provides professional direction throughout the session, including guidance on posing, expression, and positioning. Clients are encouraged to share ideas, references, and preferences — collaboration produces the best results.

5.4 Weather Contingency (Outdoor Sessions)

If an outdoor session is affected by severe weather (heavy rain, dangerous wind, extreme heat), the Photographer will contact the Client as early as possible to discuss options:

  • Reschedule to the next available date at no cost
  • Relocate to a suitable covered or indoor alternative, if one is available and agreed

The decision to postpone will be made in good faith by either party. Light rain or overcast conditions are not automatic grounds for cancellation — sometimes they make for beautiful photographs.

6. Image Delivery

6.1 Delivery Timeline

Timelines begin from the session date. The Photographer will notify the Client if delivery is expected to exceed these timeframes.

6.2 Delivery Method

All images are delivered via a private online gallery (Pixieset). The Client will receive a link and password by email.

6.3 Gallery Access

The online gallery remains active for 90 days from the date of delivery. The Client is responsible for downloading all images within this period.

After 90 days, the gallery will be archived. Retrieval of archived images incurs a $50 archive retrieval fee.

6.4 File Formats

Each delivered image includes:

  • High-resolution version (300 DPI, print-ready)
  • Web-optimised version (72 DPI)
  • Multiple aspect ratios: 1:1, 4:5, 2:3, 16:9

7. Retouching

7.1 Standard Retouching

All delivered images are professionally retouched. Retouching includes colour grading, exposure correction, skin refinement, and minor blemish removal.

7.2 Retouching Philosophy

Power Portraits preserves natural skin texture. We do not over-smooth, heavily reshape, or digitally alter a person's features. The goal is to present you at your best — not as someone else. If you have specific retouching requests, please discuss them before or during the session.

7.3 Feedback Rounds

A feedback round consists of the Client reviewing images and requesting specific adjustments. Requests should be submitted in a single consolidated list per round.

7.4 Additional Retouching

Retouching beyond the included rounds, or requests that go beyond the scope of standard retouching (e.g., background replacement, composite work, extensive body modification), will be quoted separately at $35 per image.

8. Copyright and Usage

8.1 Copyright Ownership

The Photographer retains full copyright of all images produced under this agreement, in accordance with the Copyright Act 1968 (Cth). This is standard practice under Australian law.

8.2 Client Licence

Upon full payment, the Client is granted a non-exclusive, worldwide, perpetual licence to use the delivered, retouched images for:

  • Personal use — social media, printing, gifts, personal websites
  • Professional and commercial use — LinkedIn, company websites, email signatures, press releases, speaker profiles, publications, business proposals, marketing materials, and related professional purposes

This licence is included in the session fee. No additional payment is required.

8.3 Restrictions

The Client may not:

  • Resell, sub-licence, or distribute images to third parties for their commercial use
  • Enter images into photography competitions or awards on the Client's behalf
  • Remove, crop out, or obscure any watermark applied to preview or sample images
  • Materially alter images in a way that misrepresents the Photographer's work (e.g., applying heavy filters that distort colour and quality)

8.4 Portfolio and Marketing Use

The Photographer may use images produced under this agreement for portfolio display, website content, social media, marketing materials, award submissions, and editorial purposes — unless the Client requests exclusion in writing.

A separate Model Release Form will be provided for the Client to specify their preferences regarding portfolio and promotional usage. The Client may opt out entirely, and this will have no effect on the quality of service received.

8.5 Credit

Where images are published by the Photographer, credit to the Client (e.g., social media tagging) may be given but is not obligatory. The Client may request not to be tagged or credited.

9. Model Release

A separate Model Release Form will be provided alongside this agreement. The Model Release governs the specific ways in which the Photographer may use the Client's images for promotional and commercial purposes.

Signing this Service Agreement does not automatically grant the Photographer promotional usage rights. Those rights are addressed exclusively through the Model Release Form.

10. Liability

10.1 Insurance

The Photographer carries current public liability insurance for all sessions.

10.2 Limitation of Liability

The Photographer exercises professional care in all aspects of the session, image handling, and delivery. However:

  • The Photographer is not liable for any loss, damage, or injury arising from circumstances beyond reasonable control
  • The Photographer is not liable for the Client's dissatisfaction with their own appearance in otherwise technically competent photographs
  • In all circumstances, the Photographer's maximum liability under this agreement is limited to the total fee paid by the Client for the session in question

10.3 Equipment and Data

The Photographer uses professional-grade equipment and follows industry-standard backup procedures. In the highly unlikely event of complete data loss (e.g., simultaneous failure of all storage media), the Photographer will offer a complimentary reshoot at the earliest mutually convenient date.

11. Force Majeure

Neither party is liable for failure to perform obligations under this agreement where that failure is caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to:

  • Serious illness or medical emergency
  • Severe weather events (cyclone, storm, flood)
  • Equipment failure despite reasonable maintenance
  • Government restrictions, lockdowns, or public health orders
  • Natural disaster or other extraordinary events

In such cases, the session will be rescheduled to a mutually agreed date at no additional cost. If rescheduling is not possible, the Client will receive a full refund.

12. Privacy

12.1 Data Collection

Power Portraits collects personal information (name, contact details, session preferences) solely for the purpose of delivering photography services, communicating about bookings, and issuing invoices.

12.2 Data Handling

All personal data is handled in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles. Client data is stored securely and will not be sold, shared, or disclosed to third parties except where required by law.

12.3 Images

Session images are stored on encrypted drives and secure cloud storage. The Photographer retains image files for a minimum of 12 months following the session date, after which they may be deleted from active storage.

12.4 Client Rights

The Client has the right to access, correct, or request deletion of their personal information at any time by contacting the Photographer in writing.

13. Dispute Resolution

Both parties agree to resolve any dispute arising from this agreement in good faith. The process is:

  1. Direct discussion — The parties will first attempt to resolve the matter through open and honest communication.
  2. Mediation — If direct discussion does not resolve the dispute within 14 days, either party may refer the matter to mediation through a qualified mediator in Queensland. Mediation costs will be shared equally.
  3. Legal proceedings — Court action should only be pursued as a last resort. This agreement is governed by the laws of Queensland, Australia, and any proceedings will be subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Queensland.

14. Entire Agreement

This document, together with the Model Release Form and any written amendments, constitutes the entire agreement between the Photographer and the Client regarding the services described.

  • No verbal agreements or understandings outside this document are binding.
  • Any changes or additions to this agreement must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
  • If any provision of this agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.
