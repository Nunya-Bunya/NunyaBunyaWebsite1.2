Three Things I'd Try

Let people book their sessions instantly

Right now, people have to call or email to book, which means they might put it off or forget altogether. Imagine if someone could book their next session at 10pm on a Sunday when they're planning their week - no waiting for business hours, no phone tag. 67% of fitness bookings now happen outside business hours when gyms are closed.

Show them exactly where to find you

People are probably googling your address anyway to get directions. What if they could see exactly where you are right on your website? A simple map would save them that extra step and make it feel less intimidating for first-timers who are already nervous about trying a new gym.

Fix the mobile experience completely

Your site isn't loading properly on phones, and that's probably where most people are checking you out. When someone's scrolling through fitness options on their lunch break, a slow or broken mobile site means they'll just move on to the next option. Mobile users are 5x more likely to leave if a page takes longer than 3 seconds to load.

Dylan's got the foundation of something really solid here - the personal training expertise is obvious and the testimonials prove it works. Fix these customer experience gaps and you'll turn more of those website visitors into actual members walking through the door.
