Three Things I'd Try

📍 Google

Get more photos working for you on Google

You've only got 10 photos on your Google listing, which seems light for a restaurant with your reputation and following. When people are deciding where to eat, they're scrolling through photos of your food, atmosphere, and space before they even look at your menu. I'd love to see you showcase more of what makes Massimo special – those hero dishes, the restaurant vibe, happy customers – because visual storytelling is what gets people through your door.

🌐 Website

Let customers order takeaway from their couch

You've nailed the dine-in booking experience, but there's no online ordering system for takeaway or delivery. With your strong local following, you're missing out on all those Tuesday night 'can't be bothered cooking' moments when people want to order from home. Adding online ordering would capture a whole different customer behaviour – especially since you're not on any delivery platforms yet.

🌐 Website

Make it easier for customers to ask questions

Your website shows the phone number, which is great, but there's no contact form or chat option for people who prefer to message rather than call. Some customers want to ask about dietary requirements, group bookings, or special occasions without having to make a phone call. A simple contact form would catch those enquiries that are slipping through the cracks right now.

You've built something genuinely special – that review score doesn't lie – and with a few tweaks to capture more of those online moments, you'll be converting even more of Brisbane into regulars. The foundations are rock solid, now it's about making it even easier for people to choose Massimo.
