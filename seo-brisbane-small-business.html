What Is SEO and Why It Matters

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimisation. In plain terms, it means making changes to your website and online presence so that when someone searches Google for what you sell or do, your business shows up.

Think about how you find a plumber, a dentist, or a good coffee spot. You Google it. Your customers do the same thing. If your business doesn't appear on the first page of results, they'll go to a competitor who does.

Here's why that matters for a small business in Brisbane:

  • 97% of people learn about a local company online before they visit in person
  • The top 3 Google results get over 50% of all clicks
  • Organic search (SEO) drives more traffic than social media and paid ads combined

Unlike paid advertising, SEO doesn't stop working the moment you stop paying. The content you build, the reputation you earn, and the technical improvements you make all compound over time. Six months of solid SEO work today can bring in leads for years.

You don't need to understand the technical details — that's our job. You just need to know that if your website isn't optimised, you're leaving money on the table every single day.

Local SEO vs General SEO

There are two types of SEO, and understanding the difference will save you time and money.

General SEO

General SEO is about ranking for broad keywords that anyone in Australia (or the world) might search for. Think "best running shoes" or "how to train a puppy." It's intensely competitive because you're up against massive national brands and global websites.

Local SEO

Local SEO is about showing up when someone nearby is looking for a specific service. "Plumber Brisbane," "accountant Fortitude Valley," "best Thai food South Bank." These searches have buying intent — the person is ready to hire or buy, and they want someone close.

For most Brisbane small businesses, local SEO delivers a far higher return on investment. You're not competing with every business in Australia. You're competing with the handful of businesses in your area that do what you do. That's a fight you can win.

Local SEO also powers the Google Maps results — that box of three businesses with reviews, phone numbers, and directions that appears at the top of local searches. Getting into that box (called the "Local Pack") can transform your lead flow overnight.

We'll be straight with you — SEO is not a quick fix. Anyone promising you page-one rankings in two weeks is either lying or using tactics that will get your site penalised by Google.

Month 1 — Foundation

Technical audit, fixes, Google Business Profile setup, keyword research, and strategy. You probably won't see ranking changes yet. We're building the foundation.

2

Months 2–3 — Early Signals

Content goes live, on-page optimisation takes hold. You may start seeing your site appear for long-tail keywords and local searches. Google Business Profile starts gaining traction.

3

Months 3–6 — Real Movement

Rankings climb for target keywords. Organic traffic increases. Phone calls and enquiries start coming from Google. This is where the investment starts paying off.

The key word is compounding. Unlike ads, where you pay for every single click, SEO builds momentum. The work you do today delivers results for months and years to come.

Our Tools — Privacy-First Analytics

Most agencies track your website with Google Analytics. We don't. Here's why.

Google Analytics (GA4) sends your customer data to Google. Google uses that data to build advertising profiles and improve their own ad products. Your visitors' behaviour is essentially being shared with the world's largest advertising company — the same company that sells ads to your competitors.

We use Matomo, a self-hosted analytics platform that runs on our own private servers. This means:

  • Full compliance with Australian privacy law
  • More accurate data — Matomo isn't blocked by ad blockers as aggressively as GA4
  • Complete visibility into how visitors find, use, and convert on your website

Your data should work for you — not for Google. That's a core principle at Nunya Bunya.

Common SEO Mistakes Brisbane Businesses Make

After working with dozens of Brisbane small businesses, we see the same mistakes over and over. Avoid these and you're already ahead of most of your competitors.

  • Not claiming their Google Business Profile. This is free, takes 10 minutes, and is one of the most powerful things you can do for local visibility. Yet around half of Brisbane small businesses haven't done it.
  • Having a slow, bloated website. If your site takes more than 3 seconds to load, visitors leave before they see what you offer. Google notices, and your rankings suffer.
  • No location-specific content. Your website says what you do but never mentions where you do it. Google can't connect you to Brisbane searches if "Brisbane" doesn't appear on your site.
  • Ignoring mobile users. Over 60% of local searches happen on phones. If your website is hard to use on mobile, you're turning away the majority of potential customers.
  • Inconsistent business details online. Your name, address, and phone number are different on your website, Google listing, Yellow Pages, and Facebook. This confuses Google and hurts your rankings.
  • Targeting keywords that are too broad. Trying to rank for "plumber" nationally when you serve Paddington to Chermside. Target "plumber Brisbane northside" instead — less competition, higher intent.
  • Paying for SEO with no reporting. If your current SEO provider can't show you exactly what they've done and what changed, you're paying for nothing. Demand transparency.
